# How to be a CEO when AI breaks all the old playbooks | Sequoia CEO Coach Brian Halligan

Video ID: `3UyitfSbY6c`

## Summary
Brian Halligan, former HubSpot CEO and current Sequoia CEO coach, discusses the evolving challenges and demands of leading a company in today's fast-paced, AI-driven environment. He emphasizes that while starting a company is easier, scaling a durable organization is harder than ever due to intense competition. Halligan shares practical advice and philosophical "Haganisms" for aspiring and current CEOs, focusing on leadership traits, hiring strategies, crisis management, and the crucial shift towards customer-centricity, all while adapting to rapid technological shifts.

## Key insights
- **The CEO's Unique Burden:** Being a founder CEO means facing crises alone, demanding relentless commitment (often 7 days a week) and deep obsession with the problem.
- **Scaling is the New Hard:** While starting a company has become easier, scaling it into a durable, high-impact organization is increasingly challenging due to intense competition and market noise.
- **Key Leadership Traits (LOCKS Algorithm):** Successful CEOs are **L**ovable/inspiring, **O**bsessed with their problem, have a **C**hip on their shoulder, are deeply **K**nowledgeable, and are constant **S**tudents of the game.
- **Strategic Executive Hiring:** CEOs dedicate significant time to recruiting; critical advice includes "hire slow, fire fast," valuing robust blind references over gut feelings, avoiding "big company hires" due to potential impedance mismatch, and building diverse teams that balance homegrown talent with experienced leaders (like the 2004 Red Sox).
- **Rapid Decision-Making in the AI Era:** The accelerated pace enabled by AI means companies can achieve more faster, creating a "massive tax on optionality" and requiring CEOs to make quicker, better decisions.
- **Leveraging Crises for Growth ("Haganisms"):**
    - **Don't Nibble, Eat the Sandwich:** Address bad news or difficult situations decisively and completely (e.g., layoffs), rather than in small, drawn-out increments.
    - **Next Play:** Quickly move past mistakes without dwelling or compounding them.
    - **Never Waste a Good Crisis:** Use significant setbacks as opportunities for drastic overcorrection and fundamental, healthy changes that prevent recurrence.
- **Clarity of Responsibility (DRRI):** Avoid diluted accountability—"If you want to kill a plant, have two people water it"—by assigning a Directly Responsible Individual (DRRI) with authority for every key initiative, especially cross-functional ones at scale.
- **The Reality of Progress:** Growth is a continuous grind of "two steps forward, one step back," without a single "silver bullet" solution; sustained effort is key.
- **Prioritizing Value Chain (CV > EV > TV > MV):** For long-term success, companies must prioritize customer value (CV), then enterprise/company value (EV), then team value (TV), and lastly individual manager value (MV) to prevent internal sub-optimization.
- **Shifting Cultural Gravity to Customers:** Companies must consciously decide their center of gravity (customer, employee, or investor-centric); HubSpot successfully shifted from overly employee-centric to customer-centric by integrating customer feedback into management and board meetings and tying compensation to customer retention.
- **AI's Impact on Go-to-Market:** The top of the funnel will transform with AI-powered search optimization (AEO) and conversational avatars on homepages, enabling faster and more informed buyer journeys, though traditional human-to-human enterprise sales will likely be the last white-collar job replaced by AI.
- **CEO's Evolution from Perspiration to Inspiration:** As a company scales, the CEO's role shifts from hands-on perspiration to primarily inspiring the organization and strategically letting go of direct control to foster growth.
- **"Life's Short":** A personal crisis can be a powerful catalyst for re-evaluating one's career and life choices, emphasizing the importance of intentionality and pursuing work that brings joy.

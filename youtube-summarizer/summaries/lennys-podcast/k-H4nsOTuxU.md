# How Anthropic is using Claude to automate its own growth (and why old playbooks are obsolete)

Video ID: `k-H4nsOTuxU`

## Summary
This video details how Anthropic, the fastest-growing company in history (1 to 19 billion ARR in 14 months), is leveraging AI, specifically Claude, to automate its own growth strategies and transform traditional product management playbooks. Amol Evasari, Anthropic's Head of Growth, shares insights into the unique challenges and opportunities of scaling an AI-first company, emphasizing exponential thinking, strategic big bets over micro-optimizations, and the profound impact of AI on roles and workflows. The discussion is most relevant to product leaders, growth practitioners, engineers, and founders in AI-first or rapidly scaling tech companies, offering a glimpse into future operational models.

## Key insights
-   **Unprecedented Growth:** Anthropic grew from $1 billion ARR to $19 billion ARR in just 14 months, with revenue doubling in the past few months and maintaining 10x year-over-year growth. This scale is "absurd" compared to established companies.
-   **Growth as a Company-Wide Effort:** While Amol leads growth, he emphasizes that Anthropic's success is largely due to its world-class research, inference, compute, and product teams (Claude Code, Co-work).
-   **"Success Disasters":** Approximately 70% of Amol's time is spent firefighting issues caused by hypergrowth, such as scaling challenges in acquisition, activation, and monetization, leading to emotional toughness despite "all charts going up and to the right."
-   **Exponential Thinking & Big Bets:** Anthropic prioritizes "larger swings" (50-70% of effort) over small optimizations, driven by the belief that AI's exponential capabilities will unlock 100x to 1000x future product value. This contrasts with traditional growth where 1% gains are significant.
-   **Automating Growth with AI (CASH):** Anthropic's growth platform team developed "CASH" (Claude Accelerates Sustainable Hypergrowth), an effort using Claude to automate growth experimentation. This includes identifying opportunities, building features, testing, and analyzing data, with initial results comparable to a junior PM's win rate, and rapidly improving.
-   **The Importance of "Good Friction" in Activation:** Activation is critical in AI, where capability overhang means models advance faster than user adoption. Anthropic adds friction (e.g., asking users about interests during onboarding) to better understand users and guide them to the right features, leading to higher conversion and long-term retention. This was also a key learning from Amol's experience at Mercury.
-   **Evolving Roles in AI-First Companies:**
    -   **PMs & Designers are "Squeezed":** While engineers gain significant leverage from AI tools (like Claude Code), PMs and designers are struggling to keep up with the increased output, leading to a potential need for more PMs.
    -   **Product-Minded Engineers:** Anthropic actively hires product-minded engineers who can act as "mini PMs." For projects under two weeks, engineers are deputized to manage cross-functional aspects (security, legal, stakeholders).
-   **Focus and Constraints:** Anthropic's deep focus on B2B and coding use cases was driven by leadership (e.g., Ben Mann's 2021 memo) and early necessity (smaller, less funded than competitors). This focus on coding also acted as a feedback loop, accelerating internal research and model development.
-   **AI Safety as a Core Principle & Advantage:** Anthropic is a Public Benefit Corporation (PBC) legally prioritizing public benefit over shareholder value. They are "happy to take a significant commercial hit" for safety, viewing it as a long-term competitive advantage. They believe in "driving the race to the top" by participating and influencing rather than staying out.
-   **Culture & Transparency:** Anthropic fosters a mission-driven culture with high talent density and radical transparency. "Notebook channels" on Slack allow individuals, including leadership, to share thoughts and engage in public debate, scaling beliefs and views across the rapidly growing organization.
-   **AI for Personal Productivity:** Amol uses Claude (Co-work) for various tasks:
    -   **Data Analysis:** Scheduled runs analyze 20-25 charts daily, flagging concerns and insights.
    -   **Admin Tasks:** Booking meeting rooms, archiving emails, filing reimbursements/expenses.
    -   **Managerial Coaching:** Identifying misalignment across Slack, analyzing direct report activities against OKRs, and even generating feedback "as if it were his manager" based on public and internal context.
-   **Personal Resilience & Adaptability:** Amol shares his experience recovering from a traumatic brain injury (TBI), which reinforced the importance of self-care (breaks, meditation) and adaptability. He advises others to "throw out 50-70% of how you operated in the past" in the AI era.

## Patterns & frameworks
-   **"Success Disasters":** A common pattern in hypergrowth companies where rapid success breaks existing systems, leading to urgent firefighting across various functions.
-   **Exponential vs. Linear Thinking:** The core mental model guiding Anthropic's strategy, emphasizing that AI's capabilities grow exponentially, demanding large, transformative bets rather than incremental, linear optimizations.
-   **CASH (Claude Accelerates Sustainable Hypergrowth):** An internal initiative/framework for automating the growth experimentation lifecycle using Claude, encompassing opportunity identification, feature building, testing, and data analysis.
-   **Four Parts of the Shipping Lifecycle:** Identifying opportunities, building features, testing for quality/brand, and analyzing data/gathering learnings. This is the loop CASH aims to automate.
-   **"Good Friction":** The concept that strategic friction in user flows (e.g., asking onboarding questions) can improve activation and long-term retention by better understanding user needs and guiding them to relevant value.
-   **Capability Overhang:** The challenge in AI products where model capabilities advance faster than the product's ability to diffuse these benefits to users, making activation difficult.
-   **Freedom Through Constraints:** A philosophical approach where limitations (e.g., limited funding, small team size, personal injury) force focus and adaptation, ultimately leading to more effective strategies and personal growth.
-   **"Leaving Money on the Table":** A principle of prioritizing long-term brand, user experience, and values (like AI safety) over short-term metric maximization, believing this ultimately drives more sustainable growth.
-   **"Race to the Top":** Anthropic's strategy to actively participate as a leading AI developer to influence the industry towards safer, more beneficial AI development, rather than passively observing.
-   **"Notebook Channels":** An internal communication pattern (used on Slack) where individuals and leaders share thoughts, priorities, and provocative ideas in a Twitter-like feed to scale beliefs, foster transparency, and encourage debate across the organization.
-   **Two-Week Rule for PM Delegation:** A practical framework where engineers are responsible for "PMing" projects that require two weeks or less of engineering time, empowering them and freeing up PMs for larger, more complex initiatives.
-   **"Interdisciplinary Advantage":** The idea that individuals who develop expertise across multiple disciplines (e.g., an engineer who can PM, a PM who can design or has a finance background) become significantly more valuable ("unicorns") in the evolving tech landscape.

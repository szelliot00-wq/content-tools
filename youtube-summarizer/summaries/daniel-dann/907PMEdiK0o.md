# Zoho Projects Review - 2026 | Better Than Asana or ClickUp for Teams?

Video ID: `907PMEdiK0o`

## Summary
This video provides a detailed review and hands-on walkthrough of Zoho Projects, a cloud-based project management platform. The core argument is that Zoho Projects strikes a balance between functionality and ease of use, positioning itself as a more complete and integrated solution than competitors like Asana, Monday, or ClickUp, primarily due to its native integration with the broader Zoho ecosystem (CRM, Books, etc.). The video is most relevant for teams that are outgrowing spreadsheets and simple task managers and need a structured, scalable tool for planning, tracking, and reporting on projects without the complexity or cost of enterprise-level software.

## Key insights
- **The Core Problem Solved:** The most expensive thing in companies is the "time that disappears between tasks" due to a lack of clarity on responsibilities and project status. Zoho Projects is built to solve this by providing a single, clear environment for planning, execution, and tracking.
- **Competitive Positioning:**
    - **Asana:** Strong on task coordination but weaker on built-in time tracking and billing.
    - **Monday:** Has a clean interface but lacks depth in workflow governance and reporting.
    - **ClickUp:** Overwhelms users with too many features, leading to excessive configuration time.
    - **Zoho Projects:** Sits in a "balanced position" with mature features, useful reporting, and integrated time tracking.
- **The Ecosystem Advantage:** The platform's biggest strength is its tight integration with other Zoho products. For example, a closed deal in Zoho CRM can automatically generate a project from a template, and billable hours tracked in Projects can flow directly into an invoice in Zoho Books.
- **AI Assistant (Zia):** The built-in AI can draft project descriptions or generate a full action plan from a simple prompt, saving time during project setup.
- **Gantt Chart Functionality:** The Gantt chart is described as "honest" because when one task's deadline slips, all dependent tasks automatically shift, providing a realistic view of the project timeline.
- **Integrated Time Tracking:** Time tracking is a core feature, not an add-on. Users can log hours manually or run a live timer from any task, including on the mobile app. This data automatically feeds into timesheets, making it ideal for billing and profitability analysis.
- **Centralized Project Hub:** The project overview page acts as a single source of truth, showing a summary, status timeline, and activity stream. Each project also gets its own email alias and has Zoho Click (chat) embedded for seamless communication.
- **Robust Customization & Automation:** Users can create custom fields (and flag them for sensitive data), and build custom workflow rules using a visual editor. The platform comes with pre-configured rules for common actions like deadline reminders.
- **Key Modules:** Beyond basic task management, the platform includes dedicated modules for **Approvals** (managing sign-offs), **Issues** (logging and tracking problems), and **Reports** (with pre-built dashboards for leadership).
- **Professionalism for Clients:** The ability to customize the portal URL with your own branding provides a more professional experience for clients who need to log in and collaborate.

## Use cases
- **Teams outgrowing basic tools:** Companies currently running on spreadsheets, group chats, and simple to-do lists that need a more structured and scalable system.
- **Users of the Zoho ecosystem:** Businesses already using Zoho CRM, Books, or Desk can leverage the seamless, native integrations to automate workflows between sales, support, and project execution.
- **Agencies and consulting firms:** Any business that bills clients by the hour can benefit from the built-in time tracking, timesheets, and integration with invoicing tools like Zoho Books.
- **Teams with fixed deadlines:** The "strict mode" feature, which locks tasks and phases to the project's overall schedule, is ideal for managing projects where deadlines are non-negotiable.
- **Project managers needing high visibility:** The detailed reporting dashboards provide insights into workload, time utilization, and task completion without requiring manual status updates from the team.
- **Remote or field teams:** The mobile app's support for live time tracking allows team members to log their hours accurately from anywhere.

## Patterns & frameworks
- **Integrated Workflow Automation:** The video highlights a pattern of connecting different business functions through automation. A trigger in one application (like a deal closing in Zoho CRM) automatically initiates a process in another (creating a project in Zoho Projects from a pre-defined template), eliminating manual data entry and handoffs.
- **Dynamic Dependency Management:** The Gantt chart functionality represents this pattern. It's not just a static visualization but a dynamic tool where the relationships between tasks are enforced. When one task is delayed, the system automatically recalculates and shifts the schedule for all dependent tasks, maintaining an accurate project forecast.
- **Centralized Information Hub:** The project overview page embodies the principle of a "single source of truth." It centralizes all key project artifacts—summary data, timelines, conversations (via Zoho Click), activity logs, and email correspondence—into one dashboard, reducing the need for team members to switch between multiple applications.
- **Built-in Profitability Tracking:** The platform follows a pattern of embedding financial tracking directly into project execution. By integrating time tracking at the task level and rolling it up into project-level timesheets that can connect to invoicing, it provides a real-time view of a project's cost and profitability.

## Summary
This video is a hands-on review of the Viwoods AiPaper (referred to as "V paper"), an E Ink tablet designed to bridge handwritten note-taking with AI-powered digital tools. The presenter, Daniel, walks through the device's core interaction model, physical specs, AI capabilities, calendar integration, and task management features. The central argument is that the AiPaper preserves the natural feel of writing on paper while eliminating the typical friction of switching to digital tools for follow-up work. The video is most relevant to students, professionals, and knowledge workers who rely on handwritten notes but want AI assistance and digital organization without abandoning their analog habits.

## Key insights
- **Physical design**: The device is 4.5mm thin and weighs 370g, making it comparable in feel to a physical notebook and practical for carrying between meetings, classes, or outdoor use.
- **AI without login friction**: The AI works without requiring a login, meaning users can get started immediately with no account barrier.
- **Multi-model AI support**: The device supports multiple AI models — GPT-4/5 Mini, Claude (Moral), DeepSeek, and V3 — switchable from settings, allowing users to match the model to the task (e.g., research vs. language practice).
- **Core interaction model — Circle, Ask, Continue**: The primary workflow involves three steps: (1) use the lasso tool to circle any content on the page (text, tables, diagrams, mixed content), (2) tap "Ask" to send it to the AI for analysis, and (3) continue working on the same page without being redirected elsewhere.
- **Voice input**: Long-pressing the AI button activates up to 60 seconds of voice recording, which the device auto-transcribes and sends as a query. Short-pressing opens a keyboard for typed input, giving users flexibility based on context.
- **Custom AI commands**: Users can create up to five custom AI shortcuts — for example, a one-tap command to translate English notes into Spanish or summarize content as bullet points — eliminating repetitive prompt entry throughout the day.
- **Export options**: AI responses can be exported as plain text or HTML, with HTML preserving on-screen formatting for accurate sharing with others.
- **Handwriting-to-calendar**: Users can write a note by hand, circle it, select "Create Event," and the device extracts the relevant details, presents a confirmation screen, and adds the event to the calendar.
- **Calendar sync**: The device integrates with both Google Calendar and Outlook, syncing bidirectionally and automatically. Recurring events are supported. If an account is disconnected, locally created events remain on the device.
- **To-do list management**: Handwritten tasks in the daily section can be circled and converted into a structured to-do list. Visual markers help prioritize tasks, and completed items automatically move to the bottom of the list.
- **E Ink display**: The E Ink screen is described as easy on the eyes during long sessions, reinforcing its suitability for extended work.

## Patterns & frameworks

### Circle → Ask → Continue
The device's primary interaction loop. **Circle** any content on the page using the lasso tool (works on text, tables, diagrams, or mixed content). **Ask** sends that selection to the AI for processing (summarization, explanation, key point extraction, etc.). **Continue** keeps the user on the same page — the AI response appears inline without redirecting to another app or screen. The framework is designed so AI assistance integrates into an existing handwriting workflow rather than interrupting or replacing it.

### Custom Command Shortcuts
A personalization layer on top of the AI interaction. Users define up to five named commands with pre-written instructions (e.g., "Translate to Spanish," "Bullet point summary"). Once set up, these commands collapse a multi-step AI prompting process into a single tap, removing the repetitive overhead of working with AI across a full day. The pattern follows a **define once, reuse always** logic.

### Handwriting-to-Digital Bridge
A repeatable pattern applied to both calendar events and to-do lists: write something by hand → circle it → let the AI extract structured data → confirm and sync. This pattern connects analog capture with digital organization without requiring the user to type anything, preserving the speed and naturalness of handwriting while feeding data into structured digital systems (calendars, task lists).

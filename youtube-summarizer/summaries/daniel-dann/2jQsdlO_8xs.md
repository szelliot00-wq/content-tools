# 3 Best Generative AI (GenAI) Courses on Udemy 2026

Video ID: `2jQsdlO_8xs`

## Summary
Daniel reviews three Google-created generative AI courses on Udemy, arguing that most people struggle with AI not because it's too hard, but because they learn it in a fragmented, unstructured way. He presents a three-layer framework: foundation (understanding AI), application (using AI in workflows), and creation (building tools with AI). The courses are positioned as a deliberate progression rather than isolated lessons, giving learners a complete, structured skill set. The video is most relevant to non-technical professionals and knowledge workers who want to move from casual AI experimentation to consistent, practical use in their work.

## Key insights
- The core problem isn't access to AI information — it's that most learners jump between random tutorials and tools without building a coherent system, resulting in lots of information but no real skills.
- Daniel specifically chose three courses because each targets a different layer of AI competency, and together they form a complete learning path rather than isolated skills.
- **Course 1 — AI for Content Creation (Google):** Focuses on using AI as a creative partner, not just a tool. Covers generating images and videos, building presentations in Google Slides from those assets, getting AI-driven creative reviews, and building design guidelines for brand consistency. It's compact and practical, structured as a full workflow from idea to final output.
- **Course 2 — AI for App Building (Google):** Introduces "Vibe coding" — describing what you want in plain language and letting AI help build a working app, lowering the barrier for non-developers. The workflow is grounded in real needs: you analyze your own repetitive tasks and build small tools to solve them. Uses tools like AI Studio for hands-on experience. Emphasis is on understanding how to improve what you build, not just generate it once.
- **Course 3 — AI Fundamentals (Google):** Teaches how AI actually works under the hood — not just definitions, but practical understanding of where AI excels and where it fails. Covers structured prompting frameworks (beyond surface-level "write better prompts" advice), how to integrate AI consistently into daily workflows, responsible AI use, and how to critically evaluate outputs to avoid blindly trusting results.
- All three courses are part of Google's professional certificate program, meaning completions can be added to a portfolio or LinkedIn profile.
- The recommended learning order is: Fundamentals → Content Creation → App Building, moving from understanding to applying to creating.

## Patterns & frameworks

**Three-Layer AI Learning Stack**
A progression model structuring AI learning into three sequential layers:
1. *Foundation* — Understand how AI works, its limits, and how to prompt it effectively (AI Fundamentals).
2. *Application* — Use AI in real creative and professional workflows (AI for Content Creation).
3. *Creation* — Build actual tools and apps with AI assistance (AI for App Building).
The key principle is that each layer builds on the previous one, making learning cumulative rather than fragmented.

**Vibe Coding**
A development approach introduced in the App Building course where you describe desired functionality in plain language and let AI translate that into a working application. Removes the need for deep coding knowledge and frames development as problem-description rather than syntax-writing.

**AI as a Consistent Workflow Partner (vs. Occasional Tool)**
A mental model emphasized in the Fundamentals course: rather than asking AI random one-off questions, you integrate it as a repeatable, structured part of how you work — using defined prompting frameworks and evaluation habits to get reliable, high-quality outputs consistently.

**Structured Prompting Frameworks**
Distinct from generic "write better prompts" advice, this involves thinking through inputs systematically — considering what you want the AI to do, what constraints apply, and how to guide it toward accurate and useful outputs. Treated as a learnable, repeatable skill rather than intuition.

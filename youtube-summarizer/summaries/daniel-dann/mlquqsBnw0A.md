# Airwallex vs Wise vs Stripe - 2026 | Don’t Choose the Wrong Payment Tool

Video ID: `mlquqsBnw0A`

## Summary
This video by Daniel compares three major financial platforms — Airwallex, Wise, and Stripe — to help startups and small businesses choose the right tool for their specific needs. The core argument is that these platforms are not direct competitors but rather sit at different layers of a business's financial stack, and picking the wrong one leads to unnecessary fees, manual work, and dashboard overload. Daniel evaluates each platform across key criteria including multi-currency support, payment collection, team spending, integrations, and fee transparency. The video is most relevant to founders, solopreneurs, freelancers, and small business operators managing cross-border money movement.

## Key insights
- **Airwallex is positioned as the all-in-one solution**: it supports holding 20+ currencies, provides local bank details in 20+ countries, enables payment collection in 70+ countries, supports 160+ local payment methods, and offers company and employee cards with spend controls — all from a single dashboard
- **Airwallex FX rates are described as "close to mid-market"** (not exactly mid-market), and transfers using local rails are free and typically same-day
- **Airwallex integrates natively with Xero, QuickBooks, and NetSuite**, keeping bookkeeping automatically synced — a notable operational advantage for growing teams
- **Wise is best for individuals and solopreneurs**: it uses true mid-market exchange rates with no hidden markups, offers local bank details in 10+ currencies, and shows all fees upfront before a transaction is confirmed
- **Wise's key limitation is its narrow scope**: no employee cards, no website payment collection, and limited scalability — meaning growing businesses will need to bolt on additional tools
- **Stripe excels at payment collection**: it supports 135+ currencies, works with digital wallets and cards, handles subscriptions, marketplaces, and checkouts, and is highly developer-friendly with broad app integrations
- **Stripe's key limitation is outbound money management**: it was not designed for sending money out, managing team spending, or multi-currency treasury — and transaction fees can compound significantly at scale
- **The video introduces a "financial stack layer" mental model**: each platform serves a different function (receiving revenue, sending money internationally, or managing the full financial operation), and mismatching tool to function causes inefficiency
- **A forward-looking selection criterion is emphasized**: choose based on where your business will be in 1–2 years, not just current needs, to avoid costly platform switching later

## Use cases
- **Solopreneur or freelancer working with international clients** → Wise for transparent, low-cost international transfers and receiving payments in local currencies
- **Online business or SaaS startup needing to collect payments from customers** → Stripe for robust checkout flows, subscription billing, and marketplace functionality
- **Startup or SMB operating across multiple countries** → Airwallex for a consolidated financial stack covering inbound payments, outbound transfers, employee cards, and multi-currency management
- **Business paying contractors or suppliers abroad** → Wise for its clean, straightforward international payment flow
- **Business needing accounting software integration (Xero, QuickBooks, NetSuite)** → Airwallex for automatic bookkeeping sync
- **Developer-led team building a custom payment experience** → Stripe for its flexibility and developer-friendly API
- **Company issuing employee spend cards tied to multi-currency balances** → Airwallex for its card issuance and spend control features
- **Business trying to avoid FX conversion losses** → Airwallex for like-for-like settlement in 16+ currencies, or Wise for true mid-market rates

## Patterns & frameworks
- **Financial Stack Layer Model**: The video's central framework. Rather than treating Airwallex, Wise, and Stripe as interchangeable, Daniel frames them as occupying distinct layers — *revenue collection* (Stripe), *international money movement* (Wise), and *full-stack financial operations* (Airwallex). The key insight is to identify which layer your business currently needs most, and whether you need one tool or all three.
- **"Today vs. Tomorrow" Selection Criterion**: A practical decision-making pattern where you evaluate a platform not only against current workflows but against projected business complexity over 1–2 years. This prevents premature tool-switching and the operational friction that comes with it.
- **Needs Audit Before Tool Selection**: Before comparing platforms, the video outlines a checklist of what actually matters — multi-currency holding/receiving/paying, team card issuance, accounting integrations, fee transparency, and transfer speed. This functions as a requirements-first framework to filter options objectively rather than defaulting to brand familiarity.

## Summary
This video by Daniel is a hands-on review of Controlla Voice AI, an AI music tool that transforms raw vocal recordings into studio-quality output using trained voice models. Unlike tools that generate vocals from scratch, Controlla takes your existing recording as the performance base and reshapes it using a cloned model of your own voice. Daniel walks through the full workflow: recording/uploading vocal stems, training a custom voice model, generating a full song using Google Lyra 2 Pro, and comparing the original AI vocal against his custom-trained version. The video is most relevant to independent musicians, producers, and content creators who want to elevate rough vocal ideas without a professional studio setup.

## Key insights
- Controlla's core differentiator is that it **preserves the original vocal performance** rather than generating something entirely synthetic — your recording is the foundation, not a prompt.
- The platform offers two input methods for voice training: **recording directly in the browser** or **uploading pre-recorded dry vocal stems**.
- For best results, Controlla recommends **at least 10 minutes of clean vocal recordings** — no instrumentals, effects, or room reverb.
- Critically, the model requires **actual singing**, not speech, because it needs to learn how your voice behaves across melodies, harmonies, and pitch variation.
- Controlla includes multiple music generation models; Daniel selects **Google Lyra 2 Pro** as the highest-quality option currently available on the platform.
- The **song generation workflow** involves writing lyrics with a verse/chorus structure and adding a detailed style prompt — Daniel specified "modern synth wave pop with retro drums and brighter melodies."
- A standout newer feature is the **"Play in My Voice" button** inside the player, which lets you A/B between the original generated vocal and your custom-trained model without exporting or switching tools.
- The workflow is designed to fit into an existing production process: generate a track elsewhere, sketch a quick vocal idea, bring it into Controlla to polish — no re-recording required.
- Daniel notes that most AI music tools "struggle to preserve personality in vocals," positioning Controlla's voice-training approach as a meaningful step forward on that specific problem.
- The overall pitch is about **speed and accessibility**: train a model, generate a song, and compare versions without a complicated production setup.

- **Content creators** who want original music with their own vocal character but lack studio-quality recording equipment or production skills.

## Patterns & frameworks
**Train → Generate → Compare workflow**
The core repeatable process in Controlla: (1) upload or record clean dry vocals to train a custom voice model, (2) use the song generator with lyrics and a style prompt to build a full track, (3) use the "Play in My Voice" feature to A/B the default AI vocal against your trained model — all inside one platform without exporting between tools.

**Performance-as-base model**
Rather than using a text prompt or style preset as the starting point for vocals, Controlla treats your own recorded performance as the generative seed. The AI reshapes and polishes what you already sang rather than inventing something new — keeping the original timing, phrasing, and intent intact while upgrading the quality.

**Style prompt layering**
When generating a song, Controlla separates lyric structure (verse/chorus layout in the lyrics field) from sonic identity (a detailed descriptive prompt in the style settings). These two inputs are combined to produce a track that matches both the compositional and aesthetic intent — a pattern common to AI music tools but worth noting as a distinct two-field input model.

# Confluence Whiteboards Review - 2026 | Here is How I Fixed My Team’s Messy Meetings

Video ID: `7Q_UQWH-5b4`

## Summary
This video details how Confluence Whiteboards provide a visual, collaborative digital space to combat messy brainstorming and improve team alignment. Daniel demonstrates how these boards, integrated with Jira, help teams capture ideas, prioritize work, and stay synchronized from concept to execution. It's particularly useful for the early stages of a project when quick visual collaboration is needed to turn abstract thoughts into concrete action plans. The tool offers various templates and real-time features, making it relevant for any team looking to streamline their planning and decision-making processes.

## Key insights
- Confluence Whiteboards act as a "digital thinking space" for teams to brainstorm, plan, and make decisions together in real time, especially useful at the start of a project before detailed specs are ready.
- The platform offers a library of templates for common team scenarios, including brainstorming strategies, planning, strategy, diagram boards, team building activities, and agile practices.
- Real-time collaboration allows multiple users to work on the same board simultaneously, adding notes, moving items, and reacting with emojis or comments in context.
- A key feature is the seamless two-way integration with Jira: users can pull existing tasks from Jira onto whiteboards for discussion and prioritization, and conversely, convert whiteboard sticky notes directly into trackable Jira tasks.
- AI assistance can help generate new sticky notes based on discussions and automatically cluster related ideas, bringing structure to potentially chaotic boards.
- Confluence Whiteboards keep all related work in one visual space, eliminating the need to switch between multiple tools for concept development and execution.

## Use cases
- **Brainstorming and Idea Generation:** For teams needing to gather and organize a wide range of ideas visually without chaos.
- **Project Initiation and Planning:** At the early stages of a project, when concepts are abstract and need to be quickly sketched out and agreed upon.
- **Task Prioritization:** Using frameworks like the Prioritization Matrix to visually determine what deserves attention first based on impact and urgency.
- **Structured Discussions:** Facilitating collaborative idea generation, as seen with the Brainwriting board, ensuring all suggestions and discussion rounds are recorded.
- **Workflow and Process Mapping:** Utilizing diagram templates like the swim lanes process map to visualize user journeys across different departments and identify gaps.
- **Agile Ceremonies:** Leveraging agile practice boards for sprint planning, retrospectives, or other agile workflows.
- **Remote Team Collaboration:** Providing a central visual space for distributed teams to work together in real time.
- **Converting Ideas to Action:** Streamlining the process of turning conceptual ideas directly into trackable tasks in project management software (Jira).

## Patterns & frameworks
- **Prioritization Matrix:** A template that divides a board into quadrants based on "impact" and "urgency" to help teams visually identify and prioritize tasks. Items high in both impact and urgency fall into the "top priority zone."
- **Brainwriting Board:** A template designed for structured collaborative idea generation, allowing multiple people to contribute suggestions across different discussion "rounds" in an organized space.
- **Swim Lanes Process Map:** A diagram template used to visualize user journeys or processes across different departments or functions, clearly showing where each team touches a process and helping to spot gaps or overlaps.
- **Agile Practices:** While not a single framework, the video mentions "agile practice boards" as available templates, suggesting support for various agile methodologies and ceremonies (e.g., retrospectives, sprint planning).

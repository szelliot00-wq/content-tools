## Patterns & frameworks
- **Command-driven approach:** A method of interaction where users provide direct, structured commands (e.g., `/` or `@` followed by a plain English instruction) to the AI, which the system executes precisely and consistently, replacing traditional, more conversational prompting.
- **Precision Mode:** The core operational framework within Rocket.new that defines how the command-driven approach works. It emphasizes that commands are "structured instructions that remove ambiguity," ensuring that each action is "executed exactly as intended" without the need for retries or adjustments.
- **From Idea to Interface (via single sentence):** A repeatable process where the initial input for a project is a concise, high-level statement of purpose, allowing the AI to take ownership of the underlying structure and generate a complete, functional interface.
- **Contextual Adaptation & AI Alignment:** A pattern where the AI consistently references the existing project structure and design when making updates, generating new content (e.g., images), or suggesting integrations, ensuring cohesion and preventing the AI from "drifting between ideas over time."

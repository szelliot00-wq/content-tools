# Vani by Zoho Review - 2026 | Brainstorm, Plan, Execute in One Tool - Game Changer?

Video ID: `KwUotvnrchw`

## Summary
This video is a hands-on review of Vani by Zoho, an online whiteboard and visual collaboration platform built around an infinite canvas. The host, Daniel, walks through the platform's core features — including AI tools, templates, DB tables, Kanban boards, and a built-in video call feature called Catch-Up — arguing that Vani consolidates the fragmented toolstack most teams rely on into a single workspace. The central claim is that eliminating context-switching between whiteboarding, project planning, and video conferencing tools meaningfully improves how distributed teams collaborate. The video is most relevant to product managers, team leads, designers, and anyone responsible for facilitating planning sessions, brainstorms, or cross-functional projects.

## Key insights
- **Infinite canvas as the foundation:** The entire Vani workspace is built on an infinite canvas, meaning there are no hard boundaries on how much content a team can add or how complex a session can grow.
- **Template library reduces setup friction:** Templates cover a wide range of use cases — brainstorming sessions, team kickoffs, workflow planning, strategy planning tables — and every element is editable immediately upon dropping onto the canvas, which matters when deadlines are tight.
- **Built-in AI panel (Vani AI):** The AI assistant lives inside the canvas itself. The host demonstrates generating a full decision tree for a loan approval process in seconds, which can then be dragged onto the working area and annotated with pen and marker tools — no copy-pasting into external AI tools required.
- **DB tables for structured data:** Unlike basic whiteboard tools that only offer shapes and sticky notes, Vani includes database-style tables embedded directly in the canvas. Teams can track tasks, metrics, and research data in context with their visuals without switching to a spreadsheet app.
- **Kanban boards and task trackers:** Kanban boards help teams move work through defined stages, while task trackers handle accountability across multiple owners — both live inside the same workspace alongside mind maps and brainstorming visuals.
- **Frames for device/format-specific design:** Teams can insert frames sized for specific devices or social media formats and collaborate on layouts in real time, with everyone seeing accurate dimensions simultaneously.
- **Text tools with inline AI editing:** Copy blocks support font combinations and allow AI-assisted content refinement directly inside the text element, keeping writing and ideation in the same place.
- **Upload and embed capabilities:** Teams can bring in images, documents, audio, and video files, and embed live content like YouTube videos or Google Maps locations directly on the canvas, reducing the need to open separate tabs during sessions.
- **Gadgets for facilitation:** The gadget section includes timers, polls, and a dice tool — practical additions for structured workshop facilitation or breaking decision deadlocks.
- **Pins for canvas navigation:** On a large, busy canvas with multiple contributors, pins allow teams to name and jump to key sections (e.g., feedback zone, design review area, strategy board) without endless scrolling.
- **Contextual comments and reactions:** Feedback is anchored to specific spots on the canvas rather than buried in a separate chat thread, keeping critique tied directly to the relevant work.
- **Flows for stakeholder presentations:** Frames can be sequenced into a presentation flow and exported as a PDF, useful for async stakeholder reviews.
- **Catch-Up (built-in video calls):** This is highlighted as the standout feature. Video calls launch directly inside the workspace, with all participants seeing the same live canvas simultaneously — no screen sharing required. The host argues this removes significant friction for distributed teams doing design reviews or brainstorming sessions.
- **Zen mode:** Clears the toolbar and UI so users can focus purely on the canvas content — a simple but practical focus feature.
- **Integrations:** Already connected with Microsoft Teams and Zoho Mail, with more integrations on the way.
- **Pricing:** Free plan available to start; paid plan includes a 30-day free trial with no credit card required.

- **Product managers** mapping out product roadmaps, decision trees, or feature planning alongside structured task tracking
- **Design teams** working on layout reviews in device- or format-specific frames with real-time collaboration
- **Marketing teams** planning campaigns with kickoff templates, mood boards, and embedded reference materials
- **Project managers** overseeing multi-owner projects who need Kanban boards and task trackers alongside visual planning tools
- **Workshop facilitators** running structured sessions with polls, timers, and dice tools to manage group decisions
- **Stakeholder review sessions** where work needs to be sequenced into a presentation and shared as a PDF for async consumption
- **Research and strategy teams** who need to keep reference documents, data tables, and visual frameworks in one shared context
- **Teams currently using 3+ tools** (e.g., Miro + Zoom + Notion/Trello) who want to consolidate into a single workspace

## Patterns & frameworks
- **Single-canvas collaboration model:** The core pattern is consolidating brainstorming, planning, execution tracking, and communication into one infinite canvas rather than linking separate tools. The logic is that every context switch (opening a new tab, sharing a screen, copying content between apps) introduces friction and causes teams to lose context. Vani's design attempts to eliminate all those switches.
- **Brainstorm → Plan → Execute progression:** The video implicitly frames Vani's toolset around a three-stage workflow: (1) **Brainstorm** using mind maps, sticky notes, AI-generated content, and templates; (2) **Plan** using Kanban boards, task trackers, frames, and DB tables; (3) **Execute** using comments, Catch-Up video calls, flows/exports, and pins for navigation. This progression is reflected in the product's own tagline and the order in which features are demonstrated.
- **In-context AI assistance:** Rather than treating AI as a separate tool you consult externally, Vani embeds AI directly into the canvas (for content generation, decision tree creation, and text refinement). The pattern is: AI output appears where the work already lives, so teams can immediately act on it without a copy-paste workflow.
- **Structured data alongside visuals (DB tables):** A notable design pattern is pairing unstructured visual thinking tools (mind maps, sticky notes, diagrams) with structured data grids (DB tables) in the same space. This bridges the gap between exploratory thinking and rigorous tracking without requiring two separate tools.
- **Anchored feedback model:** Comments and reactions are pinned to specific canvas locations rather than existing in a general chat or comment thread. This mirrors the "annotate the artifact" pattern seen in design review tools, keeping feedback traceable to the exact piece of work it references.

## Key insights
- **Two distinct models for different use cases:** The Link 2 Pro uses a two-axis gimbal for physical AI tracking (follows movement), while the Link 2C Pro uses software-based auto framing (no physical movement, more compact).
- **4K resolution on both** devices, designed to outperform standard webcams particularly in low-light or imperfect room conditions.
- **AI tracking on the Link 2 Pro** physically pans/tilts the camera to follow the subject — useful for presenters and teachers who move around rather than sit still.
- **Auto framing on the Link 2C Pro** keeps the subject centered via intelligent cropping/reframing, better suited for desk-based, static use like meetings.
- **Gesture control** lets users trigger functions (tracking, zoom, whiteboard mode) with hand gestures, reducing reliance on clicking through software during live presentations or recordings.
- **Whiteboard mode** automatically detects and enhances a whiteboard in frame, keeping text readable and properly framed — designed for teaching contexts.
- **Desk View mode** reorients the shot to show content on the desk surface, useful for product demos or showing notes.
- **Natural bokeh** mimics DSLR-style depth of field — blurs the background and keeps the subject sharp, giving a more professional camera look compared to a flat webcam image.
- **Green screen / virtual background** features offer cleaner edge detection than typical webcam software, producing more natural subject cutouts for streams and recorded content.
- **Scene presets** let users save different configurations (e.g., one for meetings, one for demos, one for presentations) for quick switching.
- **AI noise reduction** actively isolates the speaker's voice and filters out keyboard noise, room echo, and background voices — not just a simple noise floor reduction.
- **Multiple audio pickup modes** allow switching between wide-pickup (good for meetings) and focused/directional modes (prioritizes the speaker's voice).

- **Software control** via Insta360 Controller app manages image settings, framing behavior, audio modes, and visual effects from one interface.

## Patterns & frameworks
- **Two-axis gimbal tracking vs. software auto framing:** A repeatable product differentiation pattern — physical movement (gimbal) suits dynamic/mobile users; intelligent crop/reframe (software) suits static/desk users. Helps buyers self-select based on how much they move on camera.
- **Layered feature stack:** Both cameras share a baseline of features (4K, noise reduction, gesture control, bokeh, presets), with the higher-tier model adding the gimbal. This "good/better" ladder is a common product line framework that lowers the entry price while preserving an upgrade path.
- **AI noise reduction as voice isolation, not just noise suppression:** Framed not as "turning down background noise" but as actively focusing on the speaker's voice — a subtle but meaningful distinction in how the feature is positioned and how it performs in real environments.
- **Scene preset workflow:** Save-and-recall configurations for different contexts (meeting, demo, presentation) — a practical workflow pattern for people who use the same webcam across multiple distinct scenarios daily.

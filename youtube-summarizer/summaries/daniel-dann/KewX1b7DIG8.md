## Patterns & frameworks

### **The Single-Workspace AI Workflow**
The central repeatable pattern Daniel demonstrates is keeping all AI interactions anchored to one browser-based workspace. Rather than opening multiple tabs or platforms, the user: (1) opens a page or document, (2) activates the Merlin extension, (3) selects the appropriate model for the task, and (4) iterates within that same session. This pattern reduces friction and preserves context across tasks.

### **Model-Task Matching**
A practical mental model used throughout the video: different AI models are selected deliberately based on what the task demands. Speed-optimized models (Gemini 3 Flash) handle quick summaries; reasoning-oriented models (Claude Haiku 4.5) handle analytical depth. The framework encourages users to think of model selection as a tool selection decision, not a platform loyalty decision.

### **Summary → Analysis → Research Escalation**
Daniel implicitly follows a three-tier escalation pattern for information processing:
1. **Summary layer** — get the facts fast using a speed-optimized model on the article itself.
2. **Analytical layer** — switch models to extract meaning, implications, and gaps.
3. **Deep research layer** — activate multi-source external search to build broader context (market reaction, financial data, media coverage).
Each tier adds depth without requiring a new tool or environment.

### **Create → Refine → Optimize (for content production)**
When working in Google Docs, Daniel demonstrates a content production loop: (1) bring an existing outline into Merlin's context, (2) refine structure for engagement (hooks, pacing, retention), and (3) optimize for distribution (generate clickable titles with different framing angles). This is a repeatable process for any content creator moving from draft to publish-ready material.

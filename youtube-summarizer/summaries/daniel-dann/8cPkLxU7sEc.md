## Summary
This video provides a detailed, updated review of Skillshare, an online learning platform, after two years of continuous use. The presenter highlights Skillshare's unique focus on creative and business skills, distinguishing it from platforms offering broader academic certifications or hardcore programming. He argues that Skillshare offers exceptional value for individuals and teams seeking practical, project-based learning within a collaborative community, emphasizing its affordability and user-friendliness. While not suitable for formal qualifications or advanced technical specializations, Skillshare is presented as a highly effective resource for creative professionals, entrepreneurs, and freelancers looking to develop new skills.

## Key insights
-   **Skillshare's Core Offering**: Skillshare is an online learning platform based in New York, offering over 25,000 classes primarily focused on creative and business skills, including animation, illustration, graphic design, photography, film production, marketing, entrepreneurship, and productivity. It also includes some programming, but it's not a main focus.
-   **Community-Driven Learning**: Unlike platforms focused solely on video consumption, Skillshare is built as a community where users can interact, share projects, and receive feedback, fostering a collaborative learning environment.
-   **Target Audience**: It's ideal for creative individuals (e.g., designers, photographers), entrepreneurs, and anyone who enjoys project-based, hands-on learning. It's not suitable for those seeking academic degrees, accredited diplomas, or intensive technical programming boot camps.
-   **Class Structure**: Skillshare offers "Skillshare Originals" (premium, high-quality courses by industry leaders) and user-created courses by regular instructors. Content is organized into three categories: "Create" (creative skills), "Build" (business skills), and "Thrive" (lifestyle and productivity).
-   **Key Features**:
    -   **Learning Paths**: Curated series of courses designed to guide learners from beginner to advanced in specific skills, similar to Coursera specializations but with a creative focus.
    -   **Hands-on Projects**: Most classes include practical projects where students create something and can share it for community feedback, emphasizing learning by doing.
    -   **Offline Downloads**: Users can download classes to watch offline, making learning flexible for travelers or those with unstable internet.
    -   **Team/Business Plans**: Skillshare offers plans for companies and creative teams to facilitate collective upskilling.
-   **Pros (Advantages)**:
    -   **Exceptional Value**: Unlimited access to over 25,000 courses for a subscription price that can be less than a single course on other platforms.
    -   **User-Friendly Experience**: Clean, intuitive interface, easy signup and navigation, seamless functionality across computers, tablets, and phones, with a genuinely good mobile app.
    -   **Community Aspect**: Fosters engagement, inspiration, and collaboration through shared projects and feedback.
    -   **Bite-sized Classes**: Most classes are 20-60 minutes, perfect for focused learning without requiring extensive time commitments.
-   **Cons (Disadvantages)**:
    -   **No Accredited Certifications**: Courses do not come with formal, recognized credentials, making them unsuitable for resume building for formal qualifications.
    -   **Variable Quality**: While there's some quality control, user-created courses can vary in polish and teaching quality, requiring learners to check reviews.
    -   **Limited for Hardcore Tech**: The platform's strength is not in in-depth programming, data science, or engineering, being heavily skewed towards creative and business skills.
    -   **Subscription-Only Model**: Access is exclusively through monthly or annual subscriptions; individual courses cannot be purchased separately.
-   **Pricing and Comparison**: Skillshare offers monthly and annual subscription options, with the annual plan being significantly discounted. A free trial is available, and an annual subscription is refundable within a certain period. Compared to Udemy (individual course purchases, lifetime access) and Coursera (flexible payments, academic certifications focus), Skillshare's subscription model focuses on all-access for creative skill development.
-   **Overall Worth**: Highly recommended for creative professionals, entrepreneurs, freelancers, and anyone interested in practical, project-based learning across diverse interests without needing formal certifications.

## Use cases
-   Designers looking to learn new creative software or techniques (e.g., animation, illustration, graphic design).
-   Photographers aiming to master video editing or other visual skills.
-   Entrepreneurs needing to understand aspects of branding, marketing, or business analytics.
-   Individuals who prefer to learn by doing and value hands-on projects and peer feedback.
-   Freelancers seeking to expand their service offerings or enhance existing skills.
-   Creative teams or businesses looking for a platform to collectively upskill employees.
-   Learners who enjoy exploring diverse creative and business interests without committing to long, expensive programs.
-   Individuals who travel frequently or have unreliable internet and benefit from offline course downloads.
-   People with limited time who prefer to learn in short, focused segments.

## Patterns & frameworks
-   **Project-Based Learning**: This is a core methodology on Skillshare, where the emphasis is on practical application. Instead of passively watching videos, learners are encouraged to actively create something (e.g., a logo, a short animation, a marketing strategy) as part of their learning, which can then be shared with the community for feedback.
-   **Learning Paths**: These are structured, curated series of courses designed to guide a learner from a beginner to an advanced level in a specific skill. They provide a step-by-step framework for skill acquisition, offering a more guided experience than randomly selecting individual courses.
-   **Skillshare's Course Categorization (Create, Build, Thrive)**: This is an internal framework used to organize the platform's vast content into three main thematic areas:
    -   **Create**: Focuses on artistic and creative skills (e.g., animation, graphic design, illustration).
    -   **Build**: Focuses on business and entrepreneurial skills (e.g., analytics, freelancing, marketing).
    -   **Thrive**: Focuses on lifestyle and personal development skills (e.g., time management, wellness, productivity).

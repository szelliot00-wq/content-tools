## Summary
This video provides a detailed review of Snowball Analytics, an investment portfolio tracking and analytics platform designed for passive income investors. It argues that traditional methods like spreadsheets become unmanageable with growing portfolios and that scattered broker data lacks comprehensive insight. The platform's core offering is to consolidate all investment data into a single, structured dashboard, enabling users to analyze portfolio performance, identify asset overlaps, and automate dividend tracking with greater clarity. It is most relevant for technical investors managing complex passive income portfolios who seek to understand their investments, income sources, and long-term goals in a structured, data-driven manner.

## Key insights
- **Problem Solution:** Snowball Analytics addresses the challenge of managing complex portfolios (over 10 assets) by consolidating data from multiple sources, eliminating the need to juggle broker apps, spreadsheets, and scattered reports.
- **Dashboard Overview:** The platform provides a real-time dashboard showing total portfolio value (e.g., $190,000 in the example), estimated annual income (e.g., $45,000), and overall yield (e.g., 2.41%).
- **Funds X-ray Tool:** This feature "decompresses" complex ETFs, breaking them down into their underlying stock components. This reveals hidden asset overlap and allows investors to understand their actual exposure to specific companies (e.g., Apple) and which companies are truly generating their dividend income.
- **Dividend Calendar:** Found in the tools tab, this shows upcoming dividend payments in a visual grid or list view. It allows filtering by payout frequency (monthly, quarterly, annually) and helps investors identify "empty months" to design a portfolio that generates a smoother, consistent cash flow.
- **Historical Data Access:** Users can access up to 10 years of fundamental data, enabling historical analysis to review how companies managed dividend payments during past market crises.
- **"My Goal" Section:** Investors can set a target (e.g., $1 million) and the system estimates how many years it might take to reach that goal based on current portfolio dynamics, providing a structured, mathematical view of long-term objectives.
- **Not Financial Advice:** The platform explicitly states it is not a broker, does not provide financial advice, and focuses solely on organizing and visualizing portfolio data for clearer investor analysis.

## Patterns & frameworks
- **Consolidated Dashboard Approach:** This involves centralizing all investment data from various sources (broker apps, spreadsheets) into a single, unified interface. It provides a holistic view of the portfolio's structure, performance, and income, simplifying overall management and analysis.
- **Funds X-ray (Decompressor Metaphor):** A systematic tool that deconstructs complex investment vehicles like ETFs. Instead of merely seeing a fund ticker, it reveals the individual underlying stocks, allowing investors to identify true company exposure and hidden asset overlaps, and understand the source of their dividend income.
- **Dividend Calendar (Visual Grid / Structured Timeline):** A planning framework for visualizing and managing upcoming dividend payments. It allows investors to view payouts across different months or in a structured list, filter by frequency, and identify "empty months" to strategically adjust asset allocation for a smoother, more consistent income stream.
- **Goal-Based Projection (Mathematical View of Goals):** A quantitative method for long-term financial planning where a specific target (e.g., $1 million portfolio value) is set. The system then uses current portfolio dynamics and performance to mathematically estimate the time required to reach that goal, transforming abstract aspirations into data-driven projections.

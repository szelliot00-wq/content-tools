## Summary
This video is a hands-on product review of AceEssay AI, a writing platform that humanizes AI-generated text to bypass AI detection scanners. Host Daniel walks through three live tests against major scanning services — ZeroGPT, GPTZero, and Turnitin via Papercheck — demonstrating how the tool rewrites machine-produced content at a linguistic level rather than simply swapping synonyms. The video is most relevant to students, researchers, non-native English speakers, and professionals who work with AI-generated content and need it to pass institutional or platform-level AI detection checks.

## Key insights
- Over 61% of essays written by non-native English speakers are flagged as machine-generated even when the writing is entirely their own, illustrating how aggressive and potentially inaccurate modern scanners have become.
- AceEssay's core humanizer works by restructuring text at a deeper linguistic level — not synonym swapping — which is positioned as the reason it outperforms cheaper alternatives that tend to break academic tone.
- The platform connects directly to the official APIs of major scanning services, meaning the scores shown inside AceEssay match what those platforms would actually return — removing guesswork before submission.
- Two humanization modes are available: **Balance mode** (preserves the original voice more closely) and **Undetectable mode** (pushes harder against scanners at the cost of more aggressive rewriting).
- A **freeze feature** allows users to lock specific words or phrases before humanization, so the tool will not alter content the user wants to preserve — useful for preserving citations, terminology, or specific phrasing.
- A **pass-or-refund credit policy** applies in Undetectable mode: if the output does not clear the scan, credits are returned — meaningfully reducing risk for high-stakes submissions.
- Changes made by the humanizer are highlighted in green in the output, giving users full visibility into exactly what was rewritten.
- **Test 1 (ZeroGPT):** ChatGPT-written text started at 100% machine-generated and came back as human-written after one pass.
- **Test 2 (GPTZero):** A longer piece showed a slightly higher flag percentage, with the platform being transparent about the result and offering the option to run another pass.
- **Test 3 (Turnitin via Papercheck):** The longest text was tested via Papercheck, which generates a detailed PDF report — described as a realistic test since many academic platforms surface findings this way. The overall score came in below the 20% threshold, with no content flagged for reviewer attention.
- The platform includes a **citation generator**, extending its utility beyond humanization to cover more of the broader writing workflow.
- AceEssay operates on a **credit-based system**, with a premium subscription tier available for regular users that offers better per-run value and eliminates the need to manually top up credits.
- A one-click button on the checker results screen sends text directly into the humanizer with no extra steps, and browser notifications are available for longer scans.

## Patterns & frameworks

**Two-Mode Humanization Framework**
AceEssay offers a deliberate spectrum of humanization intensity. Balance mode preserves voice and readability with lighter intervention; Undetectable mode applies more aggressive restructuring to defeat scanner logic. This gives users a conscious tradeoff decision between authenticity preservation and detection avoidance, rather than a one-size-fits-all approach.

**Baseline → Humanize → Re-scan Loop**
The platform's built-in workflow follows a repeatable three-step process: (1) paste text into the checker to get a baseline AI-detection score, (2) send it directly to the humanizer with one click, (3) automatically re-scan the output to confirm the result. This closed feedback loop eliminates the need to cross-reference external tools and keeps the entire process within one platform.

**Direct API Integration for Score Fidelity**
Rather than simulating scanner behavior, AceEssay connects to the official APIs of ZeroGPT, GPTZero, and Turnitin (via Papercheck). The mental model here is that transparency in scoring is a trust mechanism — if the score inside the tool matches what the actual platform returns, users can make confident go/no-go decisions before submission.

**Pass-or-Refund Risk Mitigation Model**
The credit refund policy tied to Undetectable mode functions as a risk-transfer mechanism. By guaranteeing credits back on a failed scan, the platform shifts the downside risk away from the user — particularly relevant for high-stakes or one-shot submission scenarios where failure has real consequences.

**Selective Preservation via Freeze Feature**
The ability to freeze specific words or phrases before humanization reflects a precision-control pattern — allowing users to define boundaries around content that must remain unchanged (technical terms, citations, proper nouns) while letting the tool freely restructure everything else.

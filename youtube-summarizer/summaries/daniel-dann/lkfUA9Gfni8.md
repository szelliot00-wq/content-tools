## Patterns & frameworks
- **"It feels like you just have a different voice" principle:** The core design philosophy of Dubbing AI as presented is that the best voice-changing experience removes the sense of using a tool entirely. When latency is imperceptible and quality is high, the interaction feels natural rather than artificial — this is framed as the key differentiator from legacy voice changers.
- **Layered feature depth model:** The app is structured in a shallow-to-deep pattern — casual users can pick a preset voice and go immediately, while more invested users can dig into pitch/intonation controls, voice cloning, and community libraries. This tiered accessibility model allows broad adoption without overwhelming new users.
- **Ecosystem compatibility as a growth pattern:** Rather than requiring users to change their existing workflows, the tool is designed to slot into whatever setup already exists (Discord, streaming software, game chat). This "fit into existing habits" pattern lowers friction and reduces the adoption barrier significantly.

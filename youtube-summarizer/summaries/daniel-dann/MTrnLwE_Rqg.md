# SciSpace Review (2026) Get Verified Sources Without Jumping Between Databases

Video ID: `MTrnLwE_Rqg`

## Summary
This video is a walkthrough review of SciSpace, an AI-powered academic research platform, demonstrating how it consolidates literature searching, source verification, report generation, and reference organization into a single workspace. The presenter (Daniel) argues that SciSpace reduces the fragmentation typical of academic research by eliminating the need to jump between multiple databases and tools. The video covers the full workflow from submitting a research request through to exporting a finished, citation-backed report. It also demonstrates a ChatGPT integration that brings SciSpace's academic search capabilities into a conversational interface. The video is most relevant to researchers, students, and professionals who regularly conduct literature reviews or produce evidence-based reports.

## Key insights
- SciSpace accepts natural language prompts to initiate research, making it accessible without specialized search syntax knowledge.
- The platform's **Deep Research mode** searches across multiple academic databases simultaneously, designed for large-scale literature reviews rather than simple one-off queries.
- A **Verified Report** option performs additional source validation and citation checking before generating output — distinguishing it from tools that generate plausible-sounding but unverified answers.
- An **Expert processing mode** allocates additional computational resources for complex, broad evidence-gathering tasks.
- Before beginning the full analysis, SciSpace asks clarifying questions to narrow scope — in the demo, it confirmed focus on general-purpose AI assistants and autonomous agents, coverage of 2024–2026 research, both quantitative and qualitative outcomes, and a cross-industry perspective.
- The platform creates a **report plan** (defining document structure, target audience, key sections, and research constraints) before writing the actual report, improving logical flow and alignment with the original request.
- Progress can be monitored in real-time and intermediate files inspected during the analysis process.
- Generated reports are **editable** inside SciSpace after generation — users can update sections, refine conclusions, and add new sources before exporting.
- Reports can be exported as **Markdown or PDF**, supporting sharing, publishing, or further editing outside the platform.
- All supporting files created during the workflow (working documents, research files, the report plan) remain accessible in the **All Files panel** after completion.
- SciSpace integrates directly with **ChatGPT** via a one-click connection, allowing users to submit research requests conversationally while SciSpace handles academic search and source verification in the background.
- Inside the ChatGPT integration, users can open a **full research workspace** to review individual papers, adjust what columns/information are displayed in the research table, and compare studies side by side.
- From the ChatGPT integration, users can jump directly into SciSpace's native environment to use AI-generated summaries, smart filters, and notebook saving features.

## Use cases
- **Graduate students** conducting systematic or scoped literature reviews for theses or dissertations.
- **Academic researchers** who need to survey a field rapidly and produce a structured, citation-backed overview.
- **Professionals and consultants** who need evidence-based reports grounded in peer-reviewed literature (e.g., healthcare, policy, tech strategy).
- **Content creators or journalists** covering science and research topics who want verified sources without deep database expertise.
- **Teams already using ChatGPT** who want to add verified academic search without switching tools entirely.
- Anyone who needs to **compare studies across a topic** and extract structured data (quantitative and qualitative) into an organized table.
- Researchers who need to produce reports in **specific formats (Markdown/PDF)** for publication, sharing with stakeholders, or further editing.

## Patterns & frameworks

**Deep Research Mode**
A search configuration that queries multiple academic databases simultaneously rather than a single source. Used for comprehensive literature reviews where coverage breadth matters. Contrasted with a standard report format, which presumably searches less broadly.

**Verified Report Pipeline**
A multi-step output process that adds source validation and citation checking on top of the standard report generation. The key differentiator is that claims are tied back to real, checkable academic papers before the final document is produced — not generated from model knowledge alone.

**Clarifying Questions → Scope Confirmation → Analysis Loop**
Before running the full research task, SciSpace pauses to ask clarifying questions (focus area, date range, outcome types, industry scope), gets user confirmation, then resumes automatically. This scoping step prevents wasted compute on off-target literature.

**Report Planning Stage**
Prior to writing, the platform generates a structural plan (sections, target audience, constraints) and presents it as an intermediate artifact. This plan-then-write approach mirrors professional document workflows and helps ensure the final output is coherent rather than a raw dump of findings.

**Single Workspace Research Loop**
The overarching pattern of the platform: search → collect → organize into files → plan report → write and verify → edit → export, all without leaving the environment. Designed to replace a fragmented multi-tool workflow (separate database, separate AI assistant, separate writing tool).

**ChatGPT + SciSpace Hybrid Interface**
A composable workflow pattern where ChatGPT handles the conversational/prompt layer and SciSpace operates as a background tool providing verified academic retrieval. Users get natural language interaction from ChatGPT and source credibility from SciSpace, with the ability to escalate into SciSpace's full native environment when deeper analysis is needed.

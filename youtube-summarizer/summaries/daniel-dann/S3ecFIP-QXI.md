## Patterns & frameworks

**Account Map (Pre-Setup Planning)**
Before creating any cloud phones, document each account's platform, purpose, owner, and primary task. This prevents creating redundant or undefined environments and gives every cloud phone a clear role from day one.

**One Environment Per Account Rule**
Each account or workflow gets its own dedicated cloud phone — no consolidation. This enforces clean separation of login sessions, app data, notifications, and daily activity.

**Focused App Installation**
Each cloud phone should only contain apps directly relevant to its designated workflow. Keeping environments lean reduces confusion and prevents scope creep between accounts.

**Structured Organization System**
Use clear naming conventions, folders for grouping related profiles, and tags for quick filtering. The goal is to find any environment instantly by searching rather than scrolling — critical when managing 10+ profiles.

**Sequential Usage Routine**
Work through cloud phones one at a time: open one, complete all tasks for that account, close it, then move to the next. This linear pattern prevents context-switching errors and cross-account mistakes.

**Living Documentation**
Maintain a simple external reference (spreadsheet or internal note) tracking account names, cloud phone names, purpose, login email, and task owner — a lightweight but durable source of truth as the setup scales.

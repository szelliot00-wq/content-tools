## Key insights
- **Dynamic vs. static QR codes is the critical fork**: Dynamic codes let you update the destination URL after printing without reprinting physical materials — essential for packaging, menus, or any long-lived campaign asset.
- **Customization depth with a practical caveat**: QR Tiger offers pattern options, adjustable eye shapes (the three corner anchor squares), logo upload with automatic color matching, gradient overrides, frames, and template saving. However, complex patterns can fail to scan on older Android cameras — simpler designs are safer for broad audiences.
- **Analytics go beyond scan counts**: The dashboard tracks interaction volume over time, date/time trends, top countries and cities, and device breakdown — data that informs where to focus campaign spend.
- **Campaign-level controls are a differentiator**: Features include expiry dates (code auto-deactivates when a promo ends), total scan caps, geofencing, UTM parameter injection into destination URLs, and real-time activity alerts — all without external integrations.
- **GS1 Digital Link support targets retail and logistics**: QR Tiger supports GS1 Digital Link QR codes with fields for GTIN (Global Trade Item Number), batch/lot number, serial number, and consumer product variant. Relevant to the industry's Sunrise 2027 initiative preparing retail POS systems for 2D barcode scanning.
- **Two GS1 versions exist**: Simplified and Extended — the Extended version carries the full depth of product data attributes and is the one covered in the video.
- **Dual export for packaging compatibility**: GS1 outputs can be exported as both a standard QR format and an EAN barcode, allowing packaging to support both legacy 1D scanners and modern 2D scanning in parallel.
- **Bulk creation handles up to 3,000 items per file upload**, with customization applied across the entire batch and export in print-ready formats including custom paper sizes — targeting packaging runs, multi-location rollouts, and event check-ins.
- **API access enables full workflow automation**: Teams can plug QR creation, bulk output, and performance reporting directly into existing systems without manual steps.
- **Enterprise tier includes**: Centralized management, user permissions, and business-grade compliance built in natively.
- **Competitor comparison summary**:
  - *Me QR*: Good entry-level dynamic + basic reporting; limited on bulk and analytics depth.
  - *Flowcode*: Strong on marketing analytics; narrow in scope, lacks bulk and API capabilities.
  - *QR Code Monkey*: Best free static option with above-average design; no editing or tracking post-creation.
  - *QR Stuff*: Clean interface for basic static/dynamic use; weak on management depth and batch creation.
  - *Canva's built-in tool*: Convenient inside Canva layouts; no live link editing, no reporting, no campaign management. Recommended workaround: build in QR Tiger, import into Canva.
- **HTML embed snippets** are available per code for direct webpage placement, and full interaction history is exportable as CSV.

## Patterns & frameworks

**Generate vs. Manage distinction**
The video's central mental model: judging a QR tool only by output quality misses the operational layer. The right question is whether you are *generating* a code (one-time, static) or *managing* it (dynamic, trackable, controllable). This reframes the buying decision from aesthetics to workflow fit.

**Dynamic-first default**
A recommended decision rule: for any business use case, default to dynamic QR codes over static. The rationale is asymmetric — dynamic codes cost nothing extra in most tiers but eliminate the need to reprint if anything downstream changes. Static codes offer no recovery path once printed.

**Campaign asset lifecycle framework**
QR Tiger is positioned around treating a QR code the way a marketer treats a paid ad: create it, control its behavior (expiry, scan limits, geo), measure its performance (analytics dashboard), optimize it (update destination URL, retarget for new campaigns), and archive it (CSV export of full history). This maps the full lifecycle of a marketing asset onto what is traditionally seen as a static physical element.

**GS1 Digital Link data hierarchy**
A structured data model for product QR codes: GTIN (required, unique product identifier) → optional key qualifiers (batch/lot, serial number, consumer product variant) → destination method (redirect URL, file, or landing page). This hierarchy lets the same code carry both consumer-facing content and supply-chain traceability data simultaneously.

**Competitor segmentation by ceiling**
The comparison uses a "what does this tool top out at?" framework rather than feature checklists. Each competitor is characterized by its practical ceiling: free/static (QR Code Monkey), analytics-only (Flowcode), basic dynamic (Me QR, QR Stuff), design-tool convenience (Canva) — with QR Tiger positioned as the only option with no ceiling across creation, analytics, bulk, API, and compliance.

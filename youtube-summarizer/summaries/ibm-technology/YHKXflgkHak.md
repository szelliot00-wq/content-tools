# Live from Think 2026: AI operating model, VC funding & CAIO evolution

Video ID: `YHKXflgkHak`

## Summary
Recorded live at IBM Think 2026 in Boston, this episode of *Mixture of Experts* features IBM leaders and CIO adviser Tim Crawford discussing the maturation of enterprise AI adoption. The panel covers IBM's latest announcements (including the "Bob" coding agent and IBM Concert), findings from the IBV CEO study, the evolving role of the Chief AI Officer (CAIO), and a Crunchbase data point showing AI now commands 66% of global VC funding. Throughout, the conversation balances enthusiasm for AI's productivity gains with strong warnings about governance, trust, and risk.

## Key Insights
- **AI maturity is shifting from siloed to end-to-end:** Organizations are moving past department-specific AI pilots toward complete, cross-functional AI-driven life cycles and outcomes.
- **"Bob" is more than a coding agent:** IBM's Bob has evolved into a broad productivity tool used across consulting, document creation, and daily workflows — not just software development.
- **Executive AI literacy directly drives organizational AI speed:** The depth of a leader's personal understanding and use of AI demonstrably accelerates their organization's AI progress.
- **Governance must be built in from the start, not added later:** Guardrails, explainability, and traceability for AI agents are essential upfront — a rogue agent with good intentions can still consume massive resources and cause serious damage.
- **64% of CEOs trust AI for major strategic decisions — but this trust is fragile:** Panelists warn this confidence is based on implicit trust and could plummet quickly if a significant AI-related breach or failure occurs, which some experts predict is likely in 2026.
- **The CAIO role is real but still evolving:** Some CAIOs lead AI projects, others are evangelists, and others are merging into operations. The most effective model appears to be a cross-functional AI council with joint accountability rather than a single siloed executive.
- **The CAIO role may be temporary:** If an organization already has a strong, strategic CIO, a separate CAIO may be redundant — similar to how CDO roles were often created to compensate for weak CIOs.
- **AI represents 66% of global VC funding, but the definition of "AI investment" is very broad:** Much of this capital is funding industry-specific business solutions that use AI as a tool, not pure foundational model research — meaning the category now encompasses nearly all software investment.
- **Lessons from the cloud era apply directly to AI governance:** Just as cloud environments eventually became *more* secure than on-premises through automation and structured governance, AI can reach the same level of trust if organizations get ahead of it with the right tools and frameworks.
- **An AI council is strongly recommended for every organization:** Cross-functional collaboration between AI leaders, legal, compliance, risk, and business owners is critical to moving fast while avoiding undue risk.

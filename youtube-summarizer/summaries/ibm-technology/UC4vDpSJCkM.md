# How to Pass Context in an Agentic AI Flow

Video ID: `UC4vDpSJCkM`

## Summary
The video explores the critical role of context in agentic AI flows, contrasting it with traditional OAuth systems. It defines traditional context by "who," "what," and "conditions" (scopes) within a single application-resource interaction. Agentic context, in contrast, is far more complex, dynamic, autonomous, and involves orchestration, multiple agents, LLMs, and various interacting systems, necessitating a broader understanding of contextual elements. The video advocates for "context engineering" to effectively manage these multifaceted factors and achieve accurate responses.

## Key insights
- Traditional context, as seen in OAuth, is relatively simple, focusing on a user granting an application specific permissions ("who," "what," and "conditions" or scopes) to interact with a single resource during a single login.
- Agentic AI systems introduce significant complexity due to their autonomous, dynamic, and non-deterministic nature, involving orchestration, multiple agents, LLMs, and delegated tasks across multiple applications and resources.
- The context in agentic flows expands to encompass multiple dimensions: the linguistic prompt, the situational environment state, specific resource capabilities, user details, the LLM model being used, and crucially, task history for learning and improvement.
- Effective management of agentic systems requires a shift from solely focusing on prompt engineering to adopting "context engineering," which involves considering all system states and memory elements to shape interactions and retrieve appropriate, allowed responses.

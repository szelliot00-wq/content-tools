# Promptware, cloud security trends for 2026, and what the Xbox One hack means for cybersecurity

Video ID: `HycfWDXMr5Q`

## Summary
The podcast episode discusses several key cybersecurity trends. It introduces "Promptware" as a comprehensive killchain for AI attacks, urging defenders to assume AI agents are insider threats and focus on post-initial access defenses. The panel also highlights that cloud attackers are now targeting interconnected "ecosystems" rather than just infrastructure, exploiting identities and integrations. Furthermore, ransomware gangs are increasingly "living off the land" using native system tools to evade detection, and a significant, often overlooked, vulnerability lies in critical infrastructure running on outdated, unpatchable "rusting edge" systems. Finally, the episode touches upon the Xbox One's decade-long unhacked status, finally breached by a complex hardware manipulation.

## Key insights
- **Promptware & AI Security:** AI vulnerabilities extend beyond prompt injections; the "Promptware killchain" highlights a seven-step attack lifecycle. Organizations must assume AI models and agents are insider threats and focus on in-depth defensive strategies that break the attack chain *after* initial access, treating it largely as an identity and access management (IAM) problem.
- **Evolving Cloud Attacks:** Threat actors are shifting from targeting hardened cloud infrastructure to exploiting the "cloud's edges"—interconnected ecosystems of identities, integrations, APIs, and administrative tools. A defense mindset must transition from infrastructure to an "ecosystem problem," accepting shared responsibility and elements outside direct control.
- **Ransomware's "Living Off the Land":** Ransomware gangs are increasingly using built-in operating system tools (e.g., PowerShell, Windows utilities) to conduct attacks, largely due to improved EDR detection and decreased payment frequency/amounts. This "living off the land" approach makes attacks harder to detect as they blend with legitimate traffic, with potential for AI to accelerate such tactics.
- **The "Rusting Edge" of Critical Infrastructure:** A significant and overlooked cybersecurity threat lies in critical infrastructure and manufacturing running on extremely old, often unpatchable systems (e.g., Windows 95, custom DOS kernels). This presents a massive capital expenditure problem, as upgrades are costly and often not prioritized until a major incident occurs.
- **Perseverance of Hacking:** The 13-year-long unhacked status of the Xbox One, finally overcome by a highly technical hardware voltage manipulation, illustrates the extreme perseverance of hackers. This event, while a curiosity for consumers, provides insights into system resilience and the potential for long-term digital preservation through emulation.

# How Agentic AI Transforms Maintenance and Asset Decisions

Video ID: `KVFrBtkLg4I`

## Summary
This IBM Technology video explains how agentic AI is transforming asset management in industries like manufacturing, aviation, and infrastructure. It contrasts traditional "systems of record" (which track historical data) with emerging "intelligent systems of action" that reason, plan, and act autonomously. The video walks through a concrete example of a maintenance technician supported end-to-end by an AI agent — from automated work order preparation through hands-free field diagnosis to compliance-complete closeout.

## Key insights
- **Systems of record → systems of action:** The core shift is from software that passively logs the past to software that actively reasons about the future and initiates work.
- **Agentic AI handles pre-work autonomously:** Before a technician logs in, an AI agent prepares the work order, schedules it, and lines up parts, tools, and diagnostic guidance — reducing manual coordination overhead.
- **Human-AI collaboration in the field:** Technicians work hands-free, describing observations verbally or via camera/smart glasses; the agent processes sensor data, identifies root causes, and overlays step-by-step procedural guidance in real time.
- **Incomplete closeouts are a major hidden cost:** Skipped documentation, unrecorded parts, and deferred compliance steps are a leading cause of rework and compliance gaps — agentic AI addresses this by prompting completion in real time.
- **Scalability is the forcing function:** Too many high-stakes asset decisions still depend on too few skilled people; AI agents are positioned as the solution to scale expert judgment across large, distributed operations.
- **The job isn't done when the repair is done:** The intelligent system treats work as complete only when the record is complete — shifting accountability from physical fix to documented, auditable outcome.

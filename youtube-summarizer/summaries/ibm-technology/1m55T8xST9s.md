# Five AI Risks That Can Get You Fired—And How to Avoid Them

Video ID: `1m55T8xST9s`

## Summary
This video outlines five AI-related workplace risks that can result in termination or serious organizational damage. The presenter covers risks ranging from using unapproved tools to deploying unsupervised AI agents, emphasizing that most people who cause these incidents believed they were simply being productive. The core message is that AI use without proper governance and verification is where careers and organizations get into trouble.

## Key insights
- **Shadow AI**: Using personal or unapproved AI tools for work creates data breach risk — 1 in 5 organizations have reported breaches caused by shadow AI (IBM). Blocking tools often backfires, as employees find workarounds and the organization loses visibility entirely.
- **Data leakage**: Pasting proprietary code, customer records, or sensitive data into unapproved tools sends it to third-party servers, where it may be used to train future models — making it effectively unrecoverable.
- **Hallucination laundering**: AI confidently generates false information; when employees copy-paste that output into reports or filings without verification, *their* name and credibility are on the line — not the AI's. Legal and executive decisions have gone badly wrong this way.
- **Prompt injection**: Attackers can override an AI system's instructions via crafted inputs. Indirect injection is especially dangerous — malicious instructions hidden inside documents, emails, or web pages the AI retrieves, requiring no suspicious user input at all.
- **Unauthorized agentic AI**: Employees spinning up autonomous AI agents connected to internal systems risk accidental data deletion, unauthorized emails, and "zombie agents" — forgotten, still-authenticated agents that become unmonitored backdoors into organizational systems.
- **The governance gap is the real risk**: The common thread across all five is the absence of an AI governance framework — clear policies on approved tools, permitted data, and agent lifecycle management protect both employees and organizations.
- **Inaction is also a risk**: Avoiding AI entirely to "stay safe" leaves individuals and teams behind the curve — the goal is *governed* AI use, not no AI use.

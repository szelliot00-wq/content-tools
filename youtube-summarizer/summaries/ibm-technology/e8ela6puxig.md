# IAM for AI: 4 Steps to Secure and Futureproof Agentic Systems

Video ID: `e8ela6puxig`

## Summary
This video outlines a four-step maturity model for implementing advanced Identity and Access Management (IAM) strategies for AI agents and agentic systems. It addresses critical risks such as establishing accountability, enforcing least privilege, preventing abuse, and safeguarding data in non-deterministic AI environments. The model guides organizations from an ad-hoc approach to an adaptive, continuously evaluating security posture for their AI systems.

## Key insights
- **AI-Specific IAM Challenges:** Traditional IAM models are inadequate for non-deterministic AI agents, necessitating new strategies to address risks related to accountability, least privilege enforcement, abuse prevention, and data safeguarding.
- **Four-Step Maturity Model:** The proposed model provides a structured path for securing AI systems: Ad Hoc (no controls), Foundational (basic controls), Enhanced (advanced controls), and Adaptive (continuous, risk-based controls).
- **Foundational Controls (Step 2):** Essential first steps include assigning non-human identities to agents, establishing basic delegation and "on behalf of" capabilities, and implementing a SIEM for auditability and compliance.
- **Enhanced Security (Step 3):** This stage emphasizes treating agents as first-class citizens with ephemeral, task-specific credentials, implementing fine-grained and contextual-based access, and enabling real-time anomaly detection.
- **Adaptive Systems (Step 4):** The highest level of maturity involves continuous authentication, risk-based reauthentication for sensitive operations, and real-time revocation of access, crucial for dynamic and non-deterministic agentic flows.
- **Phased Implementation:** Organizations are encouraged to adopt this maturity model progressively, starting with foundational elements and building up to more sophisticated, adaptive controls rather than attempting to implement all measures simultaneously.

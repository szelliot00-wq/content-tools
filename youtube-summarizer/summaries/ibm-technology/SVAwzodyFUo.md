# How to Manage Your AI Before It Makes the Wrong Decision

Video ID: `SVAwzodyFUo`

## Summary
The video argues that most organizations lack proper governance for their AI systems, and introduces ISO 42001 as the international standard for AI Management Systems (AIMS). It walks through the standard's Plan-Do-Check-Act structure, its risk-based approach, and its major sections covering context, leadership, planning, operations, and continuous improvement. The presenter also compares ISO 42001 against the NIST AI Risk Management Framework and the EU AI Act, positioning them as complementary rather than competing frameworks.

## Key insights
- **Accountability gap is the core problem**: When an AI makes a wrong decision (e.g., denying a loan), most organizations cannot identify who validated the model, who monitors it post-deployment, or who is responsible — ISO 42001 addresses this directly.
- **ISO 42001 is a management system, not a checklist**: It governs the full AI lifecycle through continual improvement cycles, and is auditable and certifiable — analogous to what ISO 27001 did for information security.
- **Risk is multi-layered**: The standard explicitly calls out four risk categories — data risk (bias/quality), model risk (accuracy/explainability), system risk (integrations/security), and usage risk (misuse/unintended consequences).
- **The PDCA cycle structures AI governance**: Plan (define policies and risk criteria) → Do (deploy with controls) → Check (monitor for bias drift and unintended outcomes) → Act (improve continuously).
- **Three frameworks serve different purposes and stack together**:
  - *NIST AI RMF*: Voluntary, flexible guidance — defines what "good" looks like.
  - *ISO 42001*: Prescriptive, certifiable governance structure — proves compliance.
  - *EU AI Act*: Binding law with enforcement and penalties — mandates what you must do.
- **Risk tiering matters**: Not all AI systems carry equal risk; controls should be proportional to the perceived risk level of each system.
- **Governance is a competitive advantage**: Organizations that embed AI governance into their management systems will scale faster and more safely than those who treat it as a checkbox or ignore it entirely.

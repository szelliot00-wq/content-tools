# Claude Mythos, Project Glasswing and AI cybersecurity risks

Video ID: `Yae74guiqq8`

## Summary
The video discusses Anthropic's decision to delay the public release of its powerful Mythos AI model, dubbed "Project Glasswing," due to its advanced cybersecurity vulnerability-finding capabilities and associated risks. It also delves into the booming financials of major AI labs like OpenAI and Anthropic, highlighting their rapid growth, enterprise focus, and the significant costs of AI development and inference. Furthermore, the episode explores AI's potential in scientific discovery through a "GPT1900" experiment and showcases a practical application of multimodal AI for indexing and searching historical sports video content.

## Key insights
-   Anthropic's "Project Glasswing" with its Mythos AI model underscores the profound cybersecurity risks posed by highly capable AI, leading to an unprecedented decision to withhold public release and prioritize collective safety measures.
-   The strategic dilemma in AI development involves balancing open research (like public attack disclosures for cryptography) with restricting access to powerful AI's "weaponized" capabilities, such as autonomous exploit generation, to prevent misuse.
-   Frontier AI companies are experiencing explosive revenue growth, but are also incurring unprecedented costs from both model inference and exponentially increasing model development, leading to a strong shift towards enterprise-focused business models.
-   Current AI excels at "normal science" (puzzle-solving within established paradigms) but struggles with "revolutionary science" (creating entirely new paradigms), suggesting that true scientific advancement requires integrating AI with experimental feedback loops rather than just text corpora.
-   Multimodal AI, combining vision, audio, and language, is proving highly effective in transforming vast, unstructured historical video archives into searchable, intelligent knowledge systems, despite challenges like evolving broadcast graphics and commentary ambiguity.

# Build, Reuse, or Hybrid? How Orchestration Powers Agentic AI

Video ID: `tNQPNBQC5kg`

## Summary
This video explores the three main approaches to building agentic AI systems: building from scratch, reusing pre-built components, or combining both in a hybrid model. Using a dinner party cooking analogy, the hosts explain that regardless of which path a team chooses, an orchestration layer is the critical element that ties everything together. The video provides a practical checklist to help teams evaluate which approach best fits their needs.

## Key insights
- **Agentic AI goes beyond text generation** — these systems plan, act, use tools, make decisions, and move tasks forward across an entire technology stack.
- **Orchestration is the backbone** of any agentic AI system, managing task routing, policy enforcement, identity, tool invocation, and agent coordination — without it, components operate in isolation.
- **Build when** your workflow is truly unique, you have engineering capacity, and you can tolerate a longer ramp-up time before seeing value.
- **Reuse/buy when** a pre-built component covers most of your needs with minimal engineering, its behavior is predictable, and it fits your governance model.
- **Hybrid approaches** are valid — you can reuse some components while building custom ones where differentiation matters most.
- **Security and guardrails must be considered** at the orchestration level, especially when integrating pre-built or third-party components.
- **Orchestration enables flexibility** — you can swap out models or tools without breaking downstream experiences, ensuring consistent governance, performance, and safety.
- **Practical checklist:** List use cases → Choose build/reuse/hybrid → Define your orchestration layer → Pilot and measure results.

# Dan Swift - CEO Numentum

Video ID: `GG6g3oC2KjI`

## Summary
This "Payments Leader Show" episode features Dan Swift, CEO of Numentum, who shares his leadership philosophy centered on compassion and vulnerability, informed by his personal struggles. He discusses modern enterprise sales, advocating for a human-centric approach that prioritizes building genuine relationships and trust over technology-driven, high-volume outreach. Swift emphasizes the importance of understanding buyers as individuals, leveraging "relationship capital," and creating an authentic experience that adds real value rather than noise.

## Key insights
- **Leadership Driven by Compassion:** Dan Swift leads with empathy and vulnerability, seeing his past struggles with anxiety and depression as a "superpower" that helps him understand and support his team. He believes in creating an environment where people feel comfortable sharing their challenges.
- **Relationship Capital as the Core of Sales:** Swift argues that building authentic human relationships and leveraging warm introductions are paramount in sales, yielding significantly higher success rates (over 70%) compared to cold outreach (under 1%). He advocates for "lateral selling" and investing time in understanding connections.
- **Critique of Current Sales Technology Use:** While recognizing technology's potential, Swift expresses concern that AI and current sales tech often facilitate "shitty messages at scale," increasing noise and eroding trust. He hopes future technological advancements will focus on bridging the gap between buyers and sellers by delivering genuine value.
- **The "Infinite Mindset" and Giving Back:** Swift champions an abundant mindset, believing that helping others and giving back, even without expecting immediate returns, eventually brings positive outcomes. This extends to how professionals engage on platforms like LinkedIn, focusing on providing value to their network.
- **The Need for Authenticity and Immersion:** He advises sales professionals to be their genuine selves, immerse themselves deeply in their industry and product knowledge, and continuously learn. Swift also stresses the importance of aligning with a company that allows employees to show up authentically.
- **Prioritizing Self-Kindness and Celebrating Success:** Swift shares a personal reflection on his past failure to be kind to himself and celebrate achievements. He encourages listeners to prioritize self-care, manage pressure, and acknowledge their successes to maintain well-being and long-term effectiveness.

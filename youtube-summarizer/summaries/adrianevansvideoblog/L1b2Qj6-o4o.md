# Candice Pressinger part two

Video ID: `L1b2Qj6-o4o`

## Summary
In part two of this interview, Candice Pressinger, a payments security expert, discusses the intersection of fraud prevention, security, and innovation in the payments industry. She explores how organized cybercrime operates like sophisticated tech businesses, why security should be viewed as a growth lever rather than a compliance burden, and the importance of collaboration — both internally and across the wider payments ecosystem. The conversation closes with optimism about agentic commerce as the next major frontier in payments innovation.

## Key insights
- **Fraudsters operate like high-tech businesses:** They are globally coordinated, data-driven, and highly agile, making them extremely difficult to combat and requiring constant innovation to stay ahead.
- **Prevention over reaction:** Stopping fraud upstream — before it reaches the checkout — is critical, as catching it early saves merchants significant downstream pain and loss.
- **Security is a growth lever, not a compliance burden:** The biggest myth in the industry is that security slows business down; in reality, effective security reduces false positives and drives revenue growth.
- **False positives are a massive hidden cost:** While fraud costs the industry ~$300 billion, incorrectly blocking legitimate customers (false positives) costs an estimated $600 billion — nearly double the fraud loss itself.
- **One in two blocked customers never return:** Overzealous security measures permanently damage customer relationships, making precision in fraud detection commercially essential.
- **Cybercrime would rank as the world's third-largest economy:** If measured as a country, global cybercrime and fraud would sit behind only the US and China in GDP terms, underscoring its enormous scale.
- **Consortium data sharing is key:** Collaborating with other acquirers and merchants to anonymize and pool data enables real-time, adaptive fraud detection that no single organization could achieve alone.
- **Build alliances early:** Whether internally (sales, legal, data, platform teams) or externally, establishing relationships and clear responsibilities before they are urgently needed is fundamental to success.
- **Agentic commerce is the next big opportunity:** Candice sees agentic commerce as a transformative innovation akin to contactless payments, and is focused on ensuring its security so consumers and merchants can participate without fear.
- **The "Three C's" framework:** The host summarized the conversation around **Curiosity**, **Collaboration**, and being on the **Cusp** of innovation as the defining themes of Candice's professional mindset and approach.

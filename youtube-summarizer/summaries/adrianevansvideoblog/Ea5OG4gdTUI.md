# Sulabh Agarwal - Managing Director, Global Head of Payments Accenture

Video ID: `Ea5OG4gdTUI`

## Summary
This episode of the Payments Leader Show features Sulabh Agarwal, Global Head of Payments at Accenture, in conversation with host Adrian Evans. Sulabh draws on over 22 years at Accenture and an earlier career at Capital One to explore the biggest opportunities and threats in the global payments industry. The discussion spans tokenization, data, agentic AI, cross-border payments, cybersecurity, leadership culture, and a forward-looking view of where the industry will stand by 2028.

## Key insights
- **Payments is foundational infrastructure:** Like oil, payments underpin every economic transaction. The industry works best when invisible, but failures immediately make front-page news, underscoring its critical importance.
- **Cross-border payments remain a major opportunity:** Significant friction still exists in cross-border transactions, and solving this efficiently represents one of the largest growth areas in the industry.
- **Data is massively underutilized:** Enormous volumes of transactional data sit fragmented across issuers, acquirers, networks, and merchants. Unlocking and connecting this data—potentially through transactional foundation models—could drive far better decision-making and new propositions.
- **Tokenization is transformative:** Tokenizing money and assets enables programmability, fractionalization, collateralization, and atomic settlement. Real-world examples include instant car ownership transfer and smart-contract-based ticket sales. Stablecoins, CBDCs, and tokenized deposits are all accelerating this shift.
- **Agentic AI is a dual-edged force:** AI agents will drive massive efficiency gains and new commerce models, but they will also place enormous pressure on system throughput, resilience, and security—legacy batch systems will not survive in a real-time agentic world.
- **Cybersecurity and quantum computing pose existential threats:** The Methos breach highlighted systemic vulnerabilities. As quantum computing matures and combines with agentic AI, the attack surface expands dramatically, requiring real-time, immune-system-like defenses rather than periodic patching.
- **High fixed costs constrain innovation:** The payments industry's capital-intensive nature means most budgets are consumed by running and regulating existing systems, leaving little for discretionary innovation. Agentic AI's efficiency gains could help release capital for propositional development.
- **Scale and value-based pricing are the winning strategies:** Successful fintechs and PSPs are either building scale aggressively through M&A or breaking the transaction-volume-to-price correlation by pricing for value—as seen with Stripe, Wise, Adyen, and others.
- **Leadership culture drives competitive advantage:** Organizations that move fastest share clear vision, consistent follow-through, and deliberate capability building—Sulabh's "four C's": Clarity, Consistency, Capability, and Culture. Trust is the binding thread internally and externally.
- **Market-by-market dynamics shape adoption:** Mandated innovation (e.g., India's UPI, Brazil's Pix) accelerates adoption far faster than purely market-driven approaches, highlighting that regulatory force can be a powerful catalyst.
- **2028 outlook — four headline predictions:**
  1. AI and agentic AI will be widely adopted across payments, driving data utilization and operational efficiency.
  2. New consumer and SMB/corporate payment propositions will proliferate globally, inspired by leading markets.
  3. Security, resilience, and modernization will dominate investment agendas as incident risk rises sharply.
  4. Tokenization will mature—consumers will have an additional payment instrument in their wallets, and wholesale money and asset tokenization will accelerate significantly.
- **Final guiding principle:** The same strategy does not fit every organization. Leaders must identify their specific opportunity, commit to it with conviction, and follow through consistently.

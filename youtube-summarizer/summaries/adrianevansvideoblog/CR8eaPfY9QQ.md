# Adrian Evans My Career Compass

Video ID: `CR8eaPfY9QQ`

## Summary
Adrian Evans, a career coach and payments industry recruiter, hosts a solo episode of the Famous Leader Show to share his "Career Compass" framework. Using the four points of a compass as a metaphor, he walks through five principles for proactively managing your career: knowing where you're headed, building peer-to-peer relationships, accessing the hidden job market, negotiating appropriate reward, and the overarching skill of asking effectively. He draws on personal stories from recruitment, a career plateau, and coaching clients to illustrate each point.

## Key insights
- **North — Know your next and next-next destination.** Always have clarity on where your career is headed, not just in the short term but one step beyond that. Anchor this in a personal purpose (Evans' is to positively impact 20,000 careers).
- **Daily outreach is non-negotiable.** Even 20–30 minutes a day compounds to 200+ hours of relationship-building per year — a career safety net that protects you from sudden redundancy or stagnation.
- **South — Communicate peer-to-peer.** Don't talk up or down to anyone. In interviews and client meetings alike, treat every conversation as a business discussion between equals, not a supplication.
- **East — Most great opportunities are hidden.** The best roles are never advertised; they must be mined through proactive relationship-building, strategic targeting of ideal contacts, and positioning yourself before a need formally exists.
- **West — Negotiate your worth, not just your merit.** Understand the full impact of your role (revenue, cost, risk), secure sponsors who advocate for you, and use a structured framework (Evans' SMART model) to evaluate total compensation, not just base salary.
- **The glue: ask, effectively and appropriately.** Asking — politely, precisely, and with mutual benefit in mind — is the skill that unlocks mentors, opportunities, and doors. Evans cold-emailed a respected author and got half an hour that changed his career.
- **Integrity is a competitive advantage.** Doing what you say you'll do is increasingly rare and builds the trusted relationships that ultimately drive career growth, since high-value decisions are always made by humans, not algorithms.
- **Use the "So what / Now what" reflection loop.** After any learning, capture: what was the insight, what does it mean for me, and what one action will I take? This converts passive listening into forward momentum.

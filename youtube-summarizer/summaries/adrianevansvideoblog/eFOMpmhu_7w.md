# Ugne Buraciene - Group CEO payabl. The vital difference between ambition and consistency.

Video ID: `eFOMpmhu_7w`

## Summary
This episode of the Payments Leader Show features Ugne Buraciene, Group CEO of payabl., in conversation with host Adrian Evans. Ugne shares her leadership philosophy centered on consistency over ambition, drawing parallels between completing an Ironman triathlon and scaling a fintech company from 30 to 350+ employees. The conversation covers her approach to continuous learning, ego management, team building, cross-border payment frictions, and the culture she fosters at payabl.

## Key insights
- **Consistency beats ambition.** Ambition is the spark, but daily discipline and consistent effort — not high-pitched moments — are what produce lasting results, in business and athletics alike.
- **Dissect the gap between where you are and where you want to be.** Whether training for an Ironman or scaling a company, the method is the same: identify strengths, map weaknesses, and work systematically on each.
- **Learn to love the hard things.** Ugne couldn't swim a year before completing the Ironman. Embracing difficult disciplines — rather than avoiding them — is a key driver of growth.
- **Scaling requires formalizing what informality once handled.** At 30 people, knowledge spreads organically and informal control works. At 350+, the absence of documented policies and procedures becomes a costly liability.
- **Managing your ego is an ongoing leadership discipline.** The desire to launch shiny things fast can drive an organization into the ground. Recognizing that impulse and deferring to team input is a skill Ugne actively works on.
- **A strong exec team is non-negotiable.** Delegating full ownership of competency areas to trusted specialists frees the CEO to focus on growth while ensuring decisions are well-grounded.
- **Confidence is learned, not innate.** It accumulates through experience and preparation — a reassuring reframe for anyone who feels they lack it naturally.
- **Cross-border payment complexity is still vastly underestimated.** Despite being a constant conference topic, the true financial impact on merchants — especially small businesses — remains far greater than the industry acknowledges.
- **AI is a double-edged sword for fraud.** The same tools that help institutions detect and prevent fraud are being used by bad actors, making it a perpetual arms race rather than a solvable problem.
- **Product development should be driven by listening, not valuation optics.** payabl. invests heavily in client interviews, industry reports, and event attendance to ensure what they build solves real problems rather than chasing trends.

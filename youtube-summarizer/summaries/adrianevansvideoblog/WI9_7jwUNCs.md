# Ray McDonnell

Video ID: `WI9_7jwUNCs`

## Summary
Ray McDonnell, founder of RMD Payments Solutions, reflects on his unique career journey from leaving school at 17 and discovering dyslexia to leading client management and sales across Europe at JP Morgan. He emphasizes the importance of continuous learning, data-driven decision-making, and client advocacy within the complex payments ecosystem. McDonnell also highlights his passion for fostering diversity and inclusion, and his current venture focuses on simplifying payments for businesses seeking optimization.

## Key insights
-   **Non-Linear Career Path & Continuous Learning:** McDonnell's unconventional journey, including pursuing an academic degree later in life while working full-time and raising a family, demonstrates that continuous learning and strategic goal-setting (e.g., 1, 3, 5-year plans) are vital for career progression, regardless of an initial non-traditional start.
-   **Dyslexia as a Strategic Advantage:** What was once seen as an "embarrassment" and disadvantage transformed into a strength, enabling him to "see things differently" and approach problems with unique angles, fostering innovation in leadership and client engagement.
-   **Client-Centricity and Data-Driven Optimization:** Placing the merchant at the center of every decision and leveraging data for automation, real-time insights, and incremental improvements (e.g., increasing "share of wallet" by 1%) is crucial for driving efficiency and value in the payments industry.
-   **The Business Case for Diversity & Inclusion:** Beyond ethical considerations, actively promoting diversity and inclusion—and ensuring all voices are heard—leads to new ideas, enhances problem-solving, boosts reputation, and opens new revenue channels by better understanding diverse customer needs.
-   **Simplifying Payments Complexity:** Recognizing the "explosion in complexity" within the payments ecosystem, McDonnell's current work with RMD Payments Solutions focuses on translating this complexity into simple, actionable strategies that help businesses optimize their payment flows, reduce costs, and mitigate risks.
-   **Core Principles for Future Leaders:** Essential advice for future payments leaders includes maintaining relentless curiosity ("no stupid questions"), unwavering consumer focus, the courage to "fail fast," building respect and trust, adapting to AI, and approaching all tasks with passion and dedication.

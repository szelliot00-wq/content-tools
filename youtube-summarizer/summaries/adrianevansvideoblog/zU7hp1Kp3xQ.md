# Ian McLernon - Group Chief Markets Officer Remy Cointreau

Video ID: `zU7hp1Kp3xQ`

## Summary
Ian McLernon, Group Chief Markets Officer at Remy Cointreau, speaks with host Adrian Evans about his unconventional career spanning retail, cosmetics (Christian Dior/LVMH), and luxury spirits. He reflects on building multiple careers across continents, leading through crises, and what separates strong leaders and brands from weak ones. The conversation covers themes of adaptability, humility, storytelling, and the evolving nature of luxury.

## Key insights

- **Luck is manufactured.** The more you practice and try different things, the luckier you get — a belief Ian attributes to Gary Player's golf philosophy.
- **Career paths are no longer linear.** Moving sideways and switching categories is often more valuable than climbing a single ladder. The common thread should be passion for the work, not title progression.
- **You don't need to be the product expert — surround yourself with people who are.** The biggest leadership leap is accepting you can't know everything and building teams that complement your gaps.
- **"Story living" beats storytelling.** Consumers don't want to be broadcast at; they want to be immersed in a brand experience they remember and retell. Heritage, provenance, and terroir are competitive advantages if brought to life experientially.
- **Customer experience can build or kill a brand faster than any media spend.** A great retail or service moment creates lasting memory and organic advocacy; a bad one erases years of brand investment.
- **Saying "I don't know" is acceptable; saying "I don't care" is not.** Vulnerability and honesty in leadership — especially in crises — build trust more effectively than projecting false certainty.
- **Overcommunicate in uncertainty.** During the pandemic and other crises, clear, frequent, short communications — even without all the answers — kept teams grounded.
- **Sales is undervalued.** Frontline salespeople and brand ambassadors drive 30%+ variance in results. Over-indexing on marketing at the expense of sales capability is a common organizational mistake.
- **Feedback is a gift, but annual appraisals are too infrequent.** Continuous, direct feedback — including formal 360s — is essential for growth. Adapt how you give it based on the individual and culture.
- **Cultural fluency is a skill requiring constant maintenance.** Erin Meyer's *The Culture Map* is recommended; leadership and communication styles must adapt market by market, and what works in the US does not automatically work in the UK or Asia.
- **Embrace VUCA** (Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, Ambiguity). The ability to thrive in ambiguity, rather than resist it, is a prerequisite for success in modern international business.
- **It's okay to fail — just move forward at pace.** Spend enough time on the diagnostic to understand what went wrong, but don't linger on the post-mortem. Even failed initiatives are typically 40% positive.
- **Strong brands will consolidate; weak "luxury" brands will fade.** High price alone does not make a brand luxury. True luxury requires craftsmanship, heritage, genuine differentiation, and consistent desirability.
- **Give back.** Reaching out respectfully for advice or mentorship is highly effective, and those who have benefited from mentors have a responsibility to reciprocate.

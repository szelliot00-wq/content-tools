# I Took All 18 Anthropic Courses in One Weekend (Honest Review)

Video ID: `T-3bE2IIK4M`

## Summary
A content creator reviews all 18 of Anthropic's free Claude training courses after completing them in a single weekend, passing all certifications. The core argument is that most of these courses are either outdated, redundant, or filled with non-actionable "fluff," and that hands-on experimentation beats passive course consumption for learning AI tools. The video is most relevant to business owners, AI learners, and professionals evaluating whether to invest time in Anthropic's official training resources.

## Key insights
- Anthropic offers 18 free, full-length training courses with certifications — most people are unaware they exist
- The certifications are essentially worthless: questions are trivially easy and can be answered by pasting them directly into Claude
- The reviewer scored 99% across all certifications (one question wrong), reinforcing the low bar
- Only 2–3 courses are genuinely worth time: **Claude 101**, **Claude Code 101**, and **Intro to Claude Co-work** — and even these have significant gaps
- Claude Code 101's strongest content: the Claude.md file, skills, MCPs, and context management — described as "the core of what makes Claude useful"
- The Co-work course is notably outdated — it completely omits **Connections** (integrating external tools like Slack, email, Canva), which is described as one of the most useful Co-work features
- The **Building with the Claude API** course is the most technically comprehensive, covering prompting, model temperature, evals, tool calling, embeddings/vectors, and MCPs — but has heavy overlap with the Amazon Bedrock and Google Cloud Vertex courses (pick one, not all three)
- **Claude Code in Action** is ~80% a rehash of Claude Code 101 with minor additions on hooks and SDKs
- The **AI Fluency** course is singled out as a complete waste of time — its "4D framework" (Delegation, Description, Discernment, Diligence) sounds smart but is described as "a load of hot air" for anyone actually trying to automate a business
- **AI Fluency for Educators**, **AI Fluency for Students**, **AI Fluency for Nonprofits**, **Teaching AI Fluency**, and **AI Fluency for Small Businesses** are all dismissed as non-actionable fluff
- **Intro to Sub-agents** is skipped in favor of skills, which are described as far more practical for real-world use
- **MCP Advanced Topics** is interesting but assumes technical knowledge that would make the course itself unnecessary
- The reviewer's repeated thesis: the best way to learn any of this is to **use the tool on a real problem**, not watch courses
- The real skill isn't knowing how to use Claude technically — it's **identifying which business processes can be automated** and where AI will make a meaningful impact

## Use cases
- **Complete beginners to Claude** — Claude 101 provides a reasonable starting orientation
- **Non-technical business owners** — Co-work course (with the caveat that Connections is missing) is most applicable
- **Developers and technical users** — Building with the Claude API course; choose one of the three API-focused courses (Claude API, Amazon Bedrock, or Google Cloud Vertex) based on your infrastructure
- **Anyone evaluating Anthropic's certifications** — This video is a direct signal not to treat them as meaningful credentials
- **Business operators looking to automate workflows** — The reviewer recommends skipping courses entirely in favor of identifying bottlenecks and asking Claude directly how to solve them
- **AI educators or trainers** — The AI Fluency for Educators course is explicitly recommended against, even for this audience

## Patterns & frameworks
- **Learn-by-doing over passive consumption** — The reviewer's core mental model throughout: for every course, the recommendation is to use the feature hands-on rather than watch/read about it. E.g., "use an MCP and create your own MCP server" rather than taking the MCP course.
- **Skills as the core automation primitive** — "Skills" (reusable, reliable AI-driven processes) are positioned as the most important concept in agentic AI, more practical than sub-agents for real-world business use. The reviewer ties this to converting existing business SOPs into automated workflows.
- **Identify → Build → Learn loop** — The recommended learning path: (1) identify a real problem in your business or life, (2) ask Claude how to solve it, (3) build it using the features Claude recommends. This produces both learning and a usable output simultaneously ("killing two birds with one stone").
- **Anthropic's 4D Framework (dismissed)** — Delegation, Description, Discernment, Diligence — presented in the AI Fluency course as a framework for working with AI. The reviewer explicitly rejects this as non-applicable to real business automation.
- **Course overlap heuristic** — For the three API-level courses (Claude API, Amazon Bedrock, Google Cloud Vertex), the reviewer notes they cover nearly identical content tailored to different infrastructure providers and advises treating them as interchangeable — do one, cherry-pick from the others if needed.

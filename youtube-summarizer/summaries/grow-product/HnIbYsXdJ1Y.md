# She Built AI at Apple, Meta & Google... Here's What She Looks for When Hiring AI PMs

Video ID: `HnIbYsXdJ1Y`

## Summary
This video features Lisa Hang, creator of Gemini Gems and an experienced AI Product Manager from Apple, Meta, Google, and now SVP at Xero, discussing her insights on AI product management. She introduces Gemini Gems as custom versions of Gemini for specific use cases, highlighting three essential gems for PMs. The discussion also covers the process of building successful AI features, contrasting product cultures at tech giants, and what she looks for when hiring AI PMs, concluding with a roadmap for aspiring AI PMs in a rapidly evolving field. The video is highly relevant for product managers looking to leverage AI tools, build AI-powered products, or advance their careers in the AI space.

## Key insights
-   **Gemini Gems enhance LLMs with context:** Gemini Gems are custom versions of Gemini that address the key problem of LLMs lacking context by pre-loading specific information (role, company strategy, writing style, product history). Lisa uses the analogy of a "general contractor" (standard LLM) versus a "master craftsman" (Gemini Gem) that already knows your specific preferences and context.
-   **Three must-have gems for Product Managers:**
    1.  **Writing Clone:** Upload PRDs, emails, Slack messages to create first drafts that mimic your communication style, accelerating communication tasks.
    2.  **Product Strategy Advisor:** Include company strategy, market position, go-to-market plans, and competitor analysis to get a thought partner for strategic decisions.
    3.  **User Research Synthesizer:** Upload user interview transcripts, survey data, or customer support tickets to synthesize insights, ask Q&A, and sort through data quickly.
-   **Creating a Gemini Gem is simple:** The process involves three steps: 1) Give clear, detailed instructions, 2) Add knowledge (upload relevant documents), and 3) Test and iterate until satisfied with the output.
-   **Origin of Gemini Gems:** The feature was developed in 2023 to help users discover more capabilities of LLMs by allowing custom personas and instructions. It accelerated development after OpenAI launched custom GPTs, but focused on personal productivity rather than a 3rd-party app ecosystem.
-   **Key PM skill categories for leveraging AI:** Product managers need to master product strategy, execution, communication (to many stakeholders), and research (competitors, users) to effectively apply AI tools.
-   **Common mistakes with Gemini Gems (and similar AI tools):**
    1.  **Vague instructions:** Be specific and detailed, not generic like "help me write better."
    2.  **Missing key context:** Upload company documents, example emails, and personalized data.
    3.  **Lack of specialization:** Create specialized gems for different jobs rather than one general-purpose gem.
    4.  **No iteration:** Continuously test, tweak instructions, and update knowledge for improvement.
-   **Product culture differences at tech giants:**
    *   **Apple:** Product is "supreme," with an extremely high bar for craft and detail, sometimes driving teams "crazy" for perfection.
    *   **Meta:** Very data-driven and experimentation-focused culture, with emphasis on execution and impact.
    *   **Google:** The most technical culture, expecting PMs to be deeply technical and partner closely with engineering counterparts.
-   **Hiring an AI PM:** Lisa looks for standard PM skills (strategy, vision, metrics, cross-functional) plus "extras" like grit, growth mindset, and a willingness to push beyond expectations. These qualities indicate fast learners who thrive in rapidly changing environments.
-   **Building AI for AR/wearables (Meta Ray-Ban Stories AI assistant):** The development faced significant constraints including small form factor, fashionability, comfort, privacy concerns (camera on face), user interaction, and extreme engineering complexity. The future of AI in AR will likely see a wave towards on-device processing for privacy, reduced battery constraints, and wider adoption.
-   **Xero's Jax: A financial super agent:** This AI initiative aims to automate financial workflows for small businesses, offloading manual work and providing deep insights from transaction-level data. Accuracy is paramount ("accuracy down to the decimal matters").
-   **Challenges of building AI agents, especially in finance:** LLMs alone are not great at math, accounting, or tax. Xero addresses this with deep domain knowledge of financial workflows, leveraging extensive internal data for personalization and insights, and using a hybrid system (LLMs + programmatic code) for control and high quality. Robust quality measurement, human evaluators, and LLM judges are critical.
-   **Measuring AI agent success:** This involves three phases: 1) **Baseline Quality** (is AI doing what it's supposed to?), 2) **User Engagement** (adoption, usage, retention, CSAT), and 3) **Business Impact** (revenue attribution, hitting business metrics).
-   **Agent-led growth is a future concept:** While intriguing, Lisa believes it's "pretty far away." The current focus for AI adoption should be on building user trust and getting companies to delegate more tasks to agents, as human monitoring is still crucial, especially in finance.
-   **B2B SaaS companies must integrate AI:** Lisa strongly advises every B2B SaaS leader to "deeply integrate agents AI everywhere" as the future of software will be fundamentally different and is coming "very very fast."
-   **Future of AI Product Management:**
    *   **AI won't replace PMs:** AI excels at specific tasks, but PMs are paid for "product judgment" – the ability to interpret unclear signals, form opinions, and make taste-driven decisions, which AI cannot replicate.
    *   **The PM role will evolve:** PMs will be "supercharged" by AI for execution tasks. The PM, UX, and engineering roles may become more hybrid, with PMs expected to be "builders" who can create designs, prototypes, and even basic code using AI tools.
    *   **Market competitiveness:** The PM market is more competitive, but this is a "time of transformation." PMs should learn new tools and reinvent themselves.
-   **"Lean in" even without an official AI role:** PMs not working on AI features should use readily available consumer AI tools (Claude, OpenAI, Gemini) for personal projects, learning their capabilities, and even building their own "Gems" or "GPTs" to gain experience.
-   **Standing out as an AI PM job seeker:** Beyond foundational PM skills, demonstrate passion and grit. An example given was a candidate who watched 3 hours of TikTok videos by small business coaches to summarize financial needs, showing deep initiative despite not having prior AI experience.
-   **Staying on the bleeding edge:** Actively use AI tools in personal and professional life, prototype ideas with AI, use them for research, competitive analysis, and design. Follow 3-5 high-quality AI news sources without overloading on content.
-   **Roadmap to becoming an AI PM:**
    1.  **In-job experience:** Lean into AI opportunities in your current role if available.
    2.  **Side projects:** Build AI products or "Gems" on your own to explore ideas with maximum flexibility.
    3.  **Networking:** Continuously build and maintain a professional network; referrals can be key.
    4.  **Interview practice:** Treat interviewing as a skill. Practice extensively through mock interviews with current/former employees of target companies and use online resources. Repetition is key.
-   **Passion and grit are critical:** Be genuinely interested in the role, company, and product, as this passion will show through in interviews and contribute to better performance in the role.

## Use cases
-   **Product Managers:**
    -   Creating personalized AI assistants for writing, strategic thinking, and user research synthesis.
    -   Accelerating communication tasks (e.g., first drafts of emails, PRDs).
    -   Getting thought partnership for product strategy and decision-making.
    -   Synthesizing large volumes of user research data (interviews, surveys, support tickets).
    -   Prototyping new features and designs rapidly using AI tools.
    -   Staying technically relevant and understanding AI capabilities.
-   **Product Leaders/Teams:**
    -   Building and sharing custom AI "Gems" or "projects" with teams to standardize context and improve collective productivity.
    -   Integrating AI agents into B2B SaaS products to automate workflows and provide insights.
    -   Developing robust evaluation and quality measurement frameworks for AI features, especially in high-stakes domains like finance.
-   **AI Product Managers and Builders:**
    -   Designing AI products with technical constraints (e.g., wearables, on-device AI).
    -   Building AI agents that require high accuracy and reliability (e.g., financial systems).
    -   Defining success metrics for new AI features, from quality to business impact.
    -   Navigating the balance between leveraging new AI tech and focusing on core user needs.
-   **Individuals seeking AI PM careers:**
    -   Understanding the core skills and "extras" hiring managers look for.
    -   Strategies for gaining AI experience through personal projects and consumer tools.
    -   Methods for showcasing passion, grit, and initiative in job applications and interviews.
    -   Preparing for the specific interview formats of top tech companies.

## Patterns & frameworks
-   **Gemini Gems:** A customizable AI tool within Gemini that allows users to create specialized versions of the LLM by providing context, instructions, and knowledge files. It aims to make LLMs more effective by tailoring them to specific use cases.
-   **Three Must-Have PM Gems:**
    1.  **Writing Clone:** A specialized gem for communication tasks, trained on personal writing style.
    2.  **Product Strategy Adviser:** A specialized gem for strategic thinking, fed with company and market data.
    3.  **User Research Synthesizer:** A specialized gem for consolidating user feedback and insights.
-   **PM Skill Categories:** A mental model breaking down PM responsibilities into four core areas: 1) Product Strategy, 2) Execution, 3) Communication, and 4) Research (competitors, users).
-   **Xero's Jax: Financial Super Agent:** A specific application of AI agents designed to automate financial workflows for small businesses within the Xero platform, leveraging deep domain knowledge and proprietary data.
-   **Hybrid AI System (Xero's approach):** Combines LLMs with programmatic code to achieve high accuracy and control, particularly crucial in domains like finance where "accuracy down to the decimal" matters.
-   **Three Phases of Measuring AI Success:** A framework for evaluating AI products, progressing from: 1) **Baseline Quality** (evaluations, human annotators, LLM judges), to 2) **User Engagement** (usage, adoption, retention, CSAT), and finally to 3) **Business Impact** (monetization, revenue attribution).
-   **Agent-Led Growth (future thesis):** The concept that future software adoption and selection will be driven by AI agents interacting with other agents, rather than purely human decision-making. Lisa acknowledges its potential but believes it's still "quite a ways away."
-   **AI PM Career Roadmap:** A structured approach for aspiring AI PMs, emphasizing in-job experience (if possible), side projects, networking, and rigorous interview preparation.
-   **Iterative Product Development (AI context):** The principle of "test and ship and learn" applied to AI products, recognizing the rapid pace of change in technology and user behavior necessitates quick building and iteration over excessive pre-planning.

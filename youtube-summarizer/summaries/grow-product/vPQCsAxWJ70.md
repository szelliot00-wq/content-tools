# 90% of AI PMs Practice Interviews Wrong

Video ID: `vPQCsAxWJ70`

## Summary
This video argues that 90% of aspiring AI Product Managers (PMs) practice for interviews incorrectly by over-focusing on case studies, when in reality, the vast majority of the process consists of behavioral questions. The presenters, Akash and Bart, break down the AI PM behavioral interview into four key categories: AI product experience, ML team collaboration, AI-specific tradeoffs, and ethics/safety. They then conduct detailed mock interviews for each category, providing real-time feedback and analysis. This video is essential for any product manager looking to transition into an AI PM role, as it provides a clear framework and concrete examples for mastering the specific types of questions they will face.

## Key insights
- **Behavioral Questions Dominate:** The biggest finding from helping over 50 candidates land AI PM jobs is that case interviews are only about 10% of the process. The other 90% is a series of behavioral questions, which are critical to master even for roles at companies like OpenAI and Meta that do use cases.
- **Four Core AI PM Behavioral Question Categories:** Candidates must be prepared to answer questions in four main areas:
    1.  **AI Product Experience:** "Tell me about a time you shipped an AI product."
    2.  **ML Team Collaboration:** "Tell me about a conflict with an AI research team."
    3.  **AI-Specific Tradeoffs:** "How do you trade off accuracy vs. cost?"
    4.  **Graceful Failures, Ethics & Safety:** "How do you deal with bias or hallucination?"
- **"Tell Me About Yourself" Is Really "Why Should We Hire You?":** The initial introduction should be a concise (<2 minutes) career narrative focused on your relevant AI/ML experience. Akash's example traces his career from SaaS to search relevance (ThredUp), AI bots (Epic Games), ML risk algorithms (Affirm), and AI growth features (Apollo), directly answering why he is a fit for an AI PM role at OpenAI.
- **Storytelling is Crucial for Product Experience Questions:** When describing shipping an AI product (e.g., AI bots in Fortnite), structure the story clearly.
    - **Problem:** High new user churn (90% to 65% retention).
    - **Root Cause:** New players were "stomped" and skill-based matchmaking pools were too small regionally.
    - **Solution:** AI bots disguised as humans for new players to compete against.
    - **Iteration:** Moved from poor, rule-based AI to more human-like neural network AI.
    - **Impact:** A 7-8% retention lift, generating hundreds of millions in revenue.
- **A Framework for Evaluating ML Models:** A strong answer to technical questions should be structured and go beyond textbook definitions. Akash uses a three-level framework:
    1.  **Offline Evaluation:** Go beyond precision/recall. Use techniques like "axial coding" to identify and group specific failure cases. Create evaluation frameworks with "few-shot" examples (good, ok, bad) for engineers to "hill-climb" against.
    2.  **Online Evaluation:** Standard A/B testing, measuring core product metrics (e.g., acceptance rate, send rate, open rate of an AI-written email).
    3.  **Business Impact:** Connect the model's performance to high-level business metrics like customer retention, upgrades, or revenue.
- **Resolving Conflict Requires a Nuanced Approach:** When discussing conflict with an AI team (e.g., using personal data for pricing at ThredUp), show a multi-faceted resolution strategy. Instead of a single group meeting, Akash addressed individual concerns: he used C-suite buy-in for "creepiness" concerns, the legal team for legal concerns, and facilitated a team discussion to reframe ethical concerns.
- **Prioritize Ethics & Safety Over Shipping Pressure:** Companies want PMs who will make the "righteous stand." In a follow-up story, when racial bias was discovered in the pricing algorithm, Akash immediately paused the project, "ate the blame" for the delay, and then re-pitched it later. This built trust, led to the team solving the bias problem, and ultimately contributed to his promotion.
- **A Good AI Strategy Solves a Business Problem:** An AI strategy shouldn't be about "using AI." It should be about using AI to solve a core business problem, like improving retention at Apollo. The strategy had a multi-year horizon with three vectors (email writer, email warm-up, email responses) and was directly tied to business outcomes (10-20% retention increase, contributing to a $2.5B valuation).
- **The Six Overriding Skills for All Answers:**
    1.  **Be Specific:** Use real stories, real architecture, and real outcomes.
    2.  **Connect Technical to Business:** Always end with the business impact and metrics.
    3.  **Show Iteration:** Include failures and experiments; it makes stories more credible.
    4.  **Demonstrate Collaboration:** Mention specific team members (engineers, designers, CEO).
    5.  **Include Ongoing Operations:** Talk about how work is still in progress.
    6.  **Use the STAR-M Method:** Situation, Task, Action, Result, **Metrics**.

## Use cases
- **Aspiring AI PMs:** Use the four categories as a study guide to prepare stories and practice for behavioral interviews.
- **Current PMs:** Frame your existing experience using these AI-specific lenses to better articulate your qualifications for an AI role.
- **Hiring Managers:** Adopt these four question categories to effectively screen and evaluate the unique skills required for AI PM candidates.
- **Interview Coaches:** Use this video's structure and mock examples to train clients on how to deliver high-impact answers in AI PM interviews.

## Patterns & frameworks
- **Four Categories of AI PM Behavioral Questions:** The video's core framework for preparing for interviews, focusing on (1) Product Experience, (2) ML Team Collaboration, (3) AI Tradeoffs, and (4) Ethics & Safety.
- **Three-Level Framework for ML Model Evaluation:** A structured way to answer technical evaluation questions.
    - **Level 1: Offline Evaluation:** Evaluating the model with static datasets before it's live. This involves using techniques like **Axial Coding** (systematically grouping failure types) to create robust evaluation sets.
    - **Level 2: Online Evaluation:** A/B testing the model with live users and measuring direct product metrics.
    - **Level 3: Business Impact:** Measuring the model's effect on high-level company goals like revenue, retention, and growth.
- **STAR-M Method:** An enhancement of the standard STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) interview response method. The added "M" for **Metrics** emphasizes that every story must conclude with a specific, quantifiable business impact.

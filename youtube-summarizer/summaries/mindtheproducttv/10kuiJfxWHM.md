# Enter the era of the product creator - Christian Idiodi at #mtpcon London 2026

Video ID: `10kuiJfxWHM`

## Summary
Christian Idiodi, partner at Silicon Valley Product Group, argues that AI is ushering in a new era for product management — one that separates **creators** from **consumers**. Using the story of "Jim," a burned-out PM drowning in reactive work, he diagnoses a discipline that has drifted toward ceremonies, coordination, and administrative overhead. His core argument is that AI automates the low-value execution tasks (PRDs, roadmaps, data synthesis), which elevates — not eliminates — the work that has always mattered: strategy, discovery, judgment, and human creativity. This talk is most relevant to product managers, designers, engineers, and product leaders who want to understand how to position themselves and their teams for the AI era.

---

## Key insights

- **Jim's story is a diagnosis, not just a narrative.** The opening vignette of a PM firefighting through a chaotic day (outage, 112 emails, skipped lunch, missed dinner) is used to illustrate how many PMs have allowed their role to become purely reactive — coordination, status updates, and administrative work rather than proactive problem-solving.
- **Three forces are converging to create an era shift:** (1) Technology is now the business, not a servant to it — companies still treating tech as a cost center will lose. (2) AI is changing the economics of work: faster productivity, lower headcount needs, higher competitive intensity, and higher risk of waste. (3) Expectations increase as capability increases (Jevons Paradox) — faster shipping means customers expect faster improvements; more personalization capability means they expect more personalization. This is a *bar-raising* moment, not a downsizing one.
- **AI automates the execution layer, not the thinking layer.** PRDs, roadmaps, email responses, meeting notes, data analysis, and even prompting itself can be done by agents. If a PM defines their job as any of these things, they are describing a role that can be automated.
- **The three pillars of product creation have always been:** (1) selecting which problems to solve (strategy), (2) solving those problems (discovery), and (3) getting solutions to customers (delivery). AI is primarily disrupting *delivery*. This elevates what's left — strategy and discovery — which have always been the core of the discipline.
- **Creators vs. consumers is the defining split.** Idiodi's family "AI Thursdays" experiment (kids must create something with AI to earn dinner) revealed that people naturally fall into one of two camps: those who use AI to *create* and those who use it to *consume*. PMs must be firmly in the creator camp.
- **"Jim the AI PM" is still miserable.** After learning AI tools, Jim can write better PRDs faster, attend three meetings via AI proxies, and synthesize data more quickly — but he's just accelerating his project-management misery. Using AI to go faster in the wrong direction is not transformation.
- **The highest use of AI for product people is improving thinking, judgment, and creativity** — not replacing them. AI helps get thoughts out of your head and communicate them to collaborators (e.g., vibe coding a prototype to show rather than tell). "Just because you can code doesn't make you an engineer."
- **Product sense is the new imperative.** Product sense = knowing what it takes to build a good product for your customer within the real constraints of your business. AI solves for the 70% probabilistic consensus; product work solves for the edges, the unsaid, the undiscovered — what no training model has seen.
- **Business acumen is now a core technical skill.** The strongest PMs will be *product business leaders* who understand revenue, cost dynamics, regulatory risk, and market shifts. If you can't translate product work into business outcomes, you'll be outcompeted — not by AI, but by someone who can.
- **Strategy is how you win when everyone can build.** When building is cheap, the ability to *focus on the right problems* becomes the differentiator. Speed amplifies waste as much as it amplifies progress — "when you can ship faster, you can also waste faster."
- **Discovery is the real competitive advantage.** Most ideas — including AI-generated ones — will still be bad. AI can generate 10,000 ideas instantly. The discipline's job is to de-risk the gap between a good idea and a great product.
- **Human skills are not soft skills — they're the competitive moat.** Trust, influence, emotional intelligence, empathy, social awareness, critical thinking, and perseverance are increasingly what differentiates product people. These are explicitly what AI cannot replicate.
- **Build to learn vs. build to earn.** PMs and designers build to learn (prototypes, discovery, de-risking). Engineers build to earn (scalable, reliable, maintainable, secure production systems). Conflating these is a root cause of "AI slop" and wasted engineering effort.
- **Leaders must create environments for creation.** You cannot fund projects while demanding empowerment, require long-term roadmaps while expecting innovation, or micromanage while expecting ownership. AI increases the *urgency* of building empowered product teams — it doesn't replace the need for them. Without product vision, AI is random experimentation; without strategy, it's noise; without empowered teams, it's "a way to get garbage out faster."
- **Context engineering is replacing prompt engineering.** The magic isn't knowing how to write a prompt — any agent can do that. The magic is bringing deep contextual understanding of what makes a good decision in your specific environment.

## Use cases

- **Burned-out PMs** who feel like firefighters and want a framework for reclaiming proactive, creative work.
- **PMs adopting AI tools** who need to distinguish between using AI to accelerate busywork versus using it to sharpen thinking and creativity.
- **Product leaders and executives** assessing how to restructure teams and expectations in the AI era — specifically around empowerment, vision, and strategy.
- **Organizations still treating technology as a cost center** that need a leadership argument for why tech is the business, not a support function.
- **Designers** rethinking their role in AI products — solving for trust, transparency, explainability, and human-AI interaction rather than static UI.
- **Engineers** navigating AI-introduced technical debt, data pipeline complexity, and the elevated need for systems thinking.
- **Product managers trying to upskill in AI** who need a clear filter: invest in judgment and product sense, not just tooling.
- **Conference organizers, coaches, and educators** in product management looking for frameworks to reorient curricula around strategy, discovery, and human skills.
- **Teams doing vibe coding or rapid prototyping** who need clarity that the output is a communication tool for learning — not a production artifact.

## Patterns & frameworks

**The Three Pillars of Product Creation**
Strategy (which problems to solve) → Discovery (how to solve them) → Delivery (how to build and ship). AI disrupts delivery most, which elevates strategy and discovery. Every technology disruption in history has followed this pattern — elevating what's left above the disrupted layer.

**Jevons Paradox applied to product**
The more efficiently something can be done, the more demand there is for it. Applied here: as AI makes building cheaper and faster, customers expect *more* and *better* products, not fewer. This makes product leadership *more* critical, not less.

**Creators vs. Consumers framework**
A binary lens for evaluating how individuals and teams are engaging with AI. Creators use AI to amplify their output and thinking. Consumers use AI to receive and pass along outputs. In the AI era, only creators generate lasting competitive value.

**Build to Learn vs. Build to Earn**
PMs and designers build to learn — prototypes, experiments, and discovery artifacts designed to reduce risk and answer questions. Engineers build to earn — production systems optimized for scalability, reliability, and security. Mixing these up leads to waste and AI slop.

**The Empowered Team prerequisite**
AI amplification only works in an environment designed for empowered teams. The formula: product vision + product strategy + empowered teams = AI as a force multiplier. Without any one leg, AI accelerates dysfunction instead of value creation.

**"You are the substance" principle**
When using AI, the PM brings the answers — AI provides options, alternatives, and exploration. The flow is: articulate your thinking → use AI to explore trade-offs and alternatives → seek explanations to build context understanding. Never start with AI's answers and reverse-engineer your thinking from them.

**Context Engineering over Prompt Engineering**
The evolving skill isn't knowing how to phrase a prompt — it's understanding the deep context (business constraints, customer nuance, ethical considerations, organizational history) that makes a decision good. This context is uniquely human and cannot be commoditized.

## Key insights
- Gemini 3.1 and Google AI Studio are now full-stack, offering server, database, and multiplayer capabilities, making it a powerful and free tool for AI prototyping and coding.
- The "prototype-first" product development methodology (build base template, prototype feature, iterate with AI, collaborate, build real product) enables much faster validation and feedback compared to the "waterfall" approach.
- AI Studio facilitates rapid prototyping by allowing users to replicate existing UIs from screenshots, leverage AI (via Gemini Gems) for design critiques and suggestions, and natively integrate with Google APIs like Nano Banana.
- Creating a base template and using a "remix" feature in AI Studio is crucial for iterative development, allowing easy restarts and enabling multiple team members to prototype from a consistent foundation.
- Prototyping makes product feedback more concrete and effective than slide decks or documents, encouraging earlier and more engaged input from stakeholders and real users.
- In the AI era, code is becoming easier and faster to create than traditional documentation, making "prototype-first" a must-have skill for product managers and engineers to accelerate product development and validation.

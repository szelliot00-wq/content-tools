# Master Gemini in Google Docs, Sheets & Slides in 18 Min (5 Real Use Cases)

Video ID: `DIrg_kHMhbw`

## Summary
This video provides a practical, "no hype" tutorial on leveraging Google Gemini within Workspace applications like Sheets, Docs, Slides, and Drive. The presenter demonstrates five real-world use cases, ranging from researching summer camps and building family budgets to planning vacations and writing presentation scripts. While highlighting significant improvements in Gemini's capabilities, the tutorial also candidly points out existing limitations and areas needing further development, making it highly relevant for product managers and anyone who frequently uses Google Workspace and is looking to integrate AI into their workflow.

## Key insights
- Gemini in Google Workspace has improved significantly but still has room for growth across its applications.
- **Google Sheets** offers a "magical" `=AI()` formula to pull live web data (e.g., weekly prices for summer camps) directly into cells, which can then be dragged down to apply to multiple rows. However, generation is slow (2-3 minutes) and it doesn't proactively list sources, requiring an additional prompt.
- When generating a family budget for a family of four in the Bay Area, Gemini initially suggested a $6,500 mortgage for a $1.3 million home. After refining the prompt for a more realistic $2.5-$3 million home in Palo Alto, it updated the mortgage to $12,000, illustrating how prompt refinement is crucial for accuracy.
- **Google Docs** can effectively summarize content, identify preferences from attached documents (e.g., travel styles from a vacation doc), and recommend new options (e.g., 10 new countries with pros, cons, attractions, and best times to visit). It can also reformat documents based on a user-created style guide. Unlike Sheets, Docs applies edits directly without a prior plan, which the presenter prefers.
- A key limitation in **Google Docs** is image generation; Gemini creates images in the chat window, requiring manual copy-pasting into the document, and doesn't insert them directly.
- **Google Slides** can generate individual, aesthetically pleasing slides (e.g., "Five Levels of AI Native"), but currently cannot create full presentations from scratch, a feature available in competitors like Replet and Clockode.
- Similar to Docs, image generation for infographics in **Google Slides** is cumbersome, requiring manual activation of the "create images" tool and then manual insertion from the chat window, with the resulting images sometimes appearing "clip arty."
- **Google Drive** offers useful functionality for summarizing folder contents and surfacing information from files without needing to open each one (e.g., summarizing a demo folder, finding "five steps to become AI native"). However, it lacks features to help organize files and folders.
- Overall, Google has a "golden opportunity" due to its vast user base and access to personal context within Workspace, but it needs to accelerate its AI development as competitors like ChatGPT and Claude are rapidly advancing.
- The quality of Gemini's output is directly proportional to the quality and context of the input, making attached style guides and reference materials (e.g., a 42-page document for a talk track) essential for optimal results.

## Use cases
- Researching and comparing services (e.g., summer camps) by generating tables and fetching real-time pricing data.
- Creating and refining detailed financial budgets (e.g., monthly family budget, including income, expenses, and mortgage estimates).
- Summarizing extensive travel history and preferences to generate new vacation recommendations.
- Reformatting and styling existing markdown or plain text documents according to specific style guides.
- Writing talk tracks, presentation scripts, or slide copy for professional talks and presentations, referencing multiple source materials.
- Generating individual, visually appealing slides, diagrams, or flowcharts for presentations.
- Efficiently finding and summarizing information across numerous files within Google Drive without opening them.
- Product leaders seeking to push their teams to be more AI-native and adopt AI tools.
- Any user who frequently works within Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, or Drive and wants to automate or enhance their workflow with AI.

## Patterns & frameworks
- **`=AI(...)` Formula in Google Sheets:** This is a repeatable pattern for integrating live web search data directly into a spreadsheet. Users type `=AI()` followed by a natural language prompt (e.g., `=AI("get the weekly price for this camp")`) to fetch data, which can then be dragged to apply to multiple cells, effectively automating data collection.
- **Style Guide Integration for AI Generation:** A pattern for consistent content creation. Users can create a style guide (by feeding Gemini existing writing samples and prompting it to generate the guide), then attach this guide as a reference when prompting Gemini to write new documents or talk tracks, ensuring output adheres to a specific tone, structure, and formatting.
- **Context-Rich Prompting:** A general framework emphasizing that providing Gemini with extensive and relevant reference materials (e.g., multiple articles, lengthy documents) and clear instructions significantly improves the quality and accuracy of its generated output.
- **"Five Levels of AI Native":** This is mentioned as a framework for a slide, likely representing a progression or maturity model for individuals or organizations in adopting and leveraging AI.
- **"RAMP's AI Proficiency Framework":** Also mentioned as content for a slide, suggesting a structured framework developed by RAMP for assessing or improving AI proficiency.

# How OpenAI's Codex Team Builds with Codex (43 Min) | Alex & Romain

Video ID: `9qXc-THAvc0`

## Summary
This video delves into the product development philosophy of OpenAI's Codex team, highlighting a radical shift in how they build with AI. Alex and Romain explain that traditional roles are blurring, with designers writing code and PMs directly contributing through rapid prototyping and small PRs, enabled by AI agents like Codex Spark. The discussion emphasizes extreme agency, delegation of tasks to AI, and a "fill-in-the-gaps" approach to product management, rather than traditional leadership. This approach, characterized by lean specs and a focus on community feedback, offers critical insights for product managers, engineers, designers, and startup founders seeking to leverage AI for more efficient and fluid development.

## Key insights
- **Lean Specifications:** The Codex team writes "very, very few specs," often just "10 bullets or something," empowering individuals closest to the execution to make decisions and iterate quickly.
- **Blurred Roles & Increased Agency:** AI agents enable role blurring; for instance, designers on the Codex team now write "more code than was written by an engineer six months ago." Product Managers, like Alex, find it "often faster to send a PR" for small changes themselves than to communicate and prioritize tasks with engineers.
- **Rapid Iteration with Codex Spark:** Codex Spark boasts "1,200 tokens a second on average," facilitating "insane speed of thought" for rapid iteration on projects like adding decorations to a 2D game in seconds. For "very complex tasks like millions of lines of code," GPT 5.4 is used.
- **PMs as Builders and Strategists:** PMs leverage Codex's "plan mode" as a brainstorming partner to analyze existing code, generate ideas, and develop a "better mental model" for complex changes, which they then share with engineers, rather than dictating detailed requirements.
- **AI-Driven, Quality-Focused Development:** The "vast majority of code" for the Codex app was generated by an AI agent, yet the team remains highly focused on system quality and strategic direction. PMs focus on aligning the vision and ensuring high quality rather than owning complex system maintenance.
- **Open-Source and Community-Led Product Evolution:** The Codex harness is open-source, allowing power users to "live in the future" by building and experimenting with advanced features like sub-agents. This community engagement provides valuable feedback and pulls the core team into developing future product directions.
- **Strategic Product Evolution with the Codex App:** The Codex app was developed in response to observing advanced users managing parallel tasks by "t-muxing" "18 terminals across three monitors." The app provides an intuitive interface for "agentic delegation" and managing multiple AI agents simultaneously, even converting skeptics like Peter from OpenClaw.
- **OpenAI's Planning Philosophy:** The team plans either "near-term (up to eight weeks)" for concrete, rallying goals, or "long-term (a year from now)" for abstract "vibe" goals (e.g., infinitely many autonomous models), deliberately avoiding "medium-term" roadmaps.
- **PM as a "Fill-in-the-Gaps" Role:** Alex views product management not as a "good leadership position" but as a "fill in the gaps position," arguing that "the fewer people you need in a room to do anything, just the better that thing goes." He suggests many PMs might benefit from converting to engineering or design roles based on their fundamental interests.
- **Recruiting for Agency:** The Codex team prioritizes candidates with high agency—self-starters with energy and ideas, who aren't afraid to challenge existing notions. They look for what candidates have "built" and their "ideas" over traditional credentials like college degrees or CVs.

## Use cases
- **Rapid prototyping and feature development:** Quickly generate new features for iOS apps or iterate on game designs.
- **Brainstorming and planning:** Use Codex's "plan mode" to get ideas and create development plans based on existing code.
- **Automating repetitive coding tasks:** Delegate tasks like integrating Figma designs, deploying to Vercel, or creating Linear tasks for project management.
- **Self-service issue resolution:** Engineers and even PMs can directly make small code changes and send PRs, bypassing communication overhead for minor tasks.
- **Empowering cross-functional roles:** Designers can contribute directly to the codebase, and PMs can prototype features themselves.
- **Onboarding new team members:** Encourage new hires to use AI tools to quickly get up to speed on existing codebases and team workflows.
- **Community-driven product development:** Leverage open-source components to gather user feedback and build innovative features in collaboration with external developers.
- **Strategic planning in fast-evolving fields:** Employ near-term and long-term "vibe" planning to navigate rapidly changing technological landscapes.
- **Optimizing team structure:** Rethink traditional team compositions, potentially reducing the need for numerous PMs in early-stage startups or highly technical teams.

## Patterns & frameworks
- **"Fill-in-the-Gaps" PM:** This framework redefines the Product Manager's role as a fluid, responsive function focused on identifying and addressing unmet needs, unblocking progress, and coordinating efforts within a highly autonomous team, rather than dictating strategy from a top-down position. It emphasizes direct contribution and flexibility.
- **Near-term vs. Long-term Planning:** OpenAI's specific planning approach, where teams focus either on concrete, immediately actionable goals (up to 8 weeks) or high-level, aspirational "vibe" goals (a year out or more), while intentionally avoiding rigid medium-term roadmaps due to the rapid pace of AI development.
- **Collapsing the Talent Stack (Scott Bellski):** This mental model suggests that as AI tools become more capable, the traditional boundaries between roles like engineering, design, and product management blur. Individuals become more cross-functional, leading to smaller, more efficient teams where core skills and direct building are highly valued.
- **Agentic Delegation:** This describes the practice of entrusting significant portions of work, particularly coding and task management, to AI agents. It frees up human developers to focus on higher-level problem-solving, creative input, and strategic decision-making, enabling parallel workstreams and increased individual output.
- **Community-Driven Open Source Development:** A strategy where the core product or its harness is open-source, allowing a vibrant community of power users to build, experiment, and even fork new features. This fosters innovation, provides invaluable real-world feedback, and actively pulls the core development team towards future product possibilities.

# We All Depend on Open Source. We Will Defend It Together

Source: https://akrites.org/letter/

## Summary
Published June 25, 2026, this open letter announces **Akrites**, a Linux Foundation initiative joining 20+ major tech and finance companies (AWS, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, JPMorganChase, and others) to collectively defend critical open source software. The core problem: AI has collapsed the time needed to discover vulnerabilities from weeks to minutes, overwhelming maintainers and outpacing the industry's fragmented, uncoordinated response. Akrites creates a single, confidential coordination body for finding, fixing, and responsibly disclosing vulnerabilities upstream before they can be exploited.

## Key takeaways
- **AI has fundamentally broken the old security equilibrium** — what once took expert humans weeks now takes AI minutes, often surfacing multiple vulnerabilities in a single pass, creating a pipeline attackers can exploit at scale.
- **Uncoordinated disclosure is making things worse** — when dozens of companies independently scan the same library and file separate reports, maintainers get buried in noise and the risk of a vulnerability leaking before a fix exists goes up for everyone.
- **Akrites provides one confidential, trusted coordination point** — a shared Security Incident Response Team gives maintainers a single partner instead of a flood of conflicting reports, and acts as maintainer of last resort for abandoned critical packages.
- **Success is measured in patch deployment, not publication** — the initiative focuses on ensuring fixes reach real systems before adversaries can reverse-engineer disclosures into working exploits.
- **Under 5% of AI-discovered vulnerabilities have been patched** (per Endor Labs) — the gap between discovery and remediation is already critical, not hypothetical.
- **Public-private coordination is explicitly part of the plan** — Akrites will align with government efforts so defenders across sectors move together, covering critical infrastructure like banking, telecom, energy, and utilities.

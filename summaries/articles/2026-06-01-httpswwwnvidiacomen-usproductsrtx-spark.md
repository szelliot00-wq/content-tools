# Nvidia RTX Spark

Source: https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/products/rtx-spark/

## Summary
The NVIDIA RTX Spark is a new "Superchip" that combines an NVIDIA AI processor and RTX graphics into a single chip designed for Windows PCs. It targets the thinnest, most power-efficient laptops and compact desktops, delivering capabilities across AI development, creative work, and gaming. The chip supports personal AI agents running locally, and launches with devices from major OEMs including ASUS, Dell, HP, Lenovo, Microsoft, and MSI.

## Key takeaways
- **Single-chip design**: Fuses Blackwell RTX GPU (up to 6,144 cores) and a 20-core CPU into one "Superchip" with up to 128 GB unified memory.
- **1 Petaflop AI performance**: FP4 precision enables serious on-device AI workloads; CUDA runs natively, making it suitable for AI development and agent-based tasks.
- **Agentic PC concept**: Positioned as a shift from "tool to teammate"—personal AI agents can run 24/7 locally on the device.
- **Ultra-efficient and slim**: Marketed as the most power-efficient RTX chip ever, enabling all-day battery life in thin laptop form factors.
- **Broad software ecosystem**: Access to 1,000+ RTX-accelerated apps and games, plus NVIDIA Studio tools, DLSS, Reflex, ray tracing, and G-SYNC.
- **Wide OEM availability**: Laptops from ASUS ProArt P16, Dell XPS 16, HP OmniBook X 14, Lenovo Yoga Pro 9n, Microsoft Surface Laptop Ultra, and MSI Prestige N16 Flip AI+; desktops from Acer, ASUS, Dell, Gigabyte, HP, Lenovo, and MSI.

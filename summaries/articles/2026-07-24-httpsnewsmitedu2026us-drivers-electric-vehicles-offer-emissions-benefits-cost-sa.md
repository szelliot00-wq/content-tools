# For most US drivers, EVs offer emissions benefits and cost savings

Source: https://news.mit.edu/2026/us-drivers-electric-vehicles-offer-emissions-benefits-cost-savings-0512

## Summary
A May 2026 MIT study published in *Environmental Research Letters* found that electric vehicles (EVs) produce 40–60% fewer greenhouse gas emissions and are cost-competitive with gas-powered vehicles for most U.S. drivers, even without tax credits. Researchers from MIT's Institute for Data, Systems, and Society analyzed thousands of zip codes, incorporating driving patterns, climate, electricity mix, fuel prices, and traffic to build a more comprehensive picture than prior studies. The work also updated the public tool carboncounter.com to help individuals compare lifetime emissions and costs across nearly any vehicle on the market.

## Key takeaways
- EVs reduce emissions by 40–60% in most U.S. locations, with the largest benefits in urban areas with cleaner grids and heavier traffic.
- Individual driving behavior matters as much as regional factors like the local electricity mix when determining an EV's emissions benefit.
- Cold climates reduce EV range but have minimal impact on annual emissions savings — even in the worst conditions, EVs still deliver substantial reductions.
- EVs are cost-competitive with comparable gas vehicles across most of the U.S. on a lifetime ownership basis, without relying on clean vehicle tax credits.
- In areas with affordable electricity, battery-electric vehicles tend to cost less than both plug-in hybrids and combustion-engine vehicles.
- As the electricity grid continues to decarbonize, EV emissions benefits will become more uniform across regions, while differences between individual drivers will persist.

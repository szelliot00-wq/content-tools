# Data is everywhere. The government is buying it without a warrant

Source: https://www.npr.org/2026/03/25/nx-s1-5752369/ice-surveillance-data-brokers-congress-anthropic

## Summary
Government agencies, notably ICE, are increasingly circumventing Fourth Amendment warrant requirements by purchasing vast quantities of sensitive personal data from commercial data brokers. This practice allows them to acquire detailed information, such as location data, financial records, and browsing history, without judicial oversight. A new bipartisan House bill, the "Fourth Amendment Is Not for Sale Act," seeks to address this loophole by requiring agencies to obtain a warrant before buying such data, aiming to protect individual privacy and civil liberties.

## Key takeaways
- Government agencies are actively purchasing sensitive personal data from commercial data brokers to bypass the need for warrants.
- This purchased data includes highly personal information like location tracking, financial transactions, and internet browsing history.
- The practice creates a legal loophole, enabling surveillance without the judicial review typically mandated by the Fourth Amendment.
- There is a lack of regulation within the data broker industry, allowing for extensive collection and sale of personal data without individuals' consent or knowledge.
- A bipartisan legislative effort, the "Fourth Amendment Is Not for Sale Act," has been introduced in the House to mandate warrant requirements for government data purchases from brokers.
- This unregulated acquisition and use of data by the government raises significant privacy and civil liberties concerns for individuals.

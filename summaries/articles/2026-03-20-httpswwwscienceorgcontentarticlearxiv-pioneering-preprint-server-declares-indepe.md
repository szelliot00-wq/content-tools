# ArXiv Declares Independence from Cornell

Source: https://www.science.org/content/article/arxiv-pioneering-preprint-server-declares-independence-cornell

## Summary
ArXiv, the venerable preprint server crucial to scientific communication, is transitioning from its long-standing home at Cornell University to become an independent, non-profit organization known as the ArXiv Institute. This strategic move aims to ensure the platform's long-term sustainability, enhance its governance, and secure diversified funding beyond its current reliance on Cornell and various grants. While maintaining strong operational ties with Cornell, the new structure will allow ArXiv to better scale with its growing global usage and adapt to the evolving needs of the open science landscape.

## Key takeaways
-   **Independence and New Governance**: ArXiv is evolving from being primarily hosted by Cornell University to an independent non-profit entity, the "ArXiv Institute," with its own executive director and board of directors.
-   **Reasons for Transition**: The move is driven by the need for greater financial sustainability, improved scalability for its rapidly growing user base (20,000 papers/month), and a governance structure better suited for a global, critical research infrastructure.
-   **Continued Cornell Ties**: While independent, ArXiv will maintain strong operational connections with Cornell, including physical server hosting at Cornell Tech and some staff remaining affiliated with the university.
-   **Focus on Sustainability**: The new structure aims to diversify funding sources beyond its current model, which relies heavily on grants, library contributions, and Cornell's support, to ensure long-term stability.
-   **Commitment to Open Science**: This change is intended to bolster ArXiv's role as a free, open-access platform, ensuring it can continue to serve the scientific community effectively into the future.

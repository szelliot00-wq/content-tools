# Sam Neill has died

Source: https://www.theguardian.com/film/2026/jul/13/sam-neill-death-actor-dies-aged-78

## Summary
Sam Neill, the acclaimed New Zealand actor best known for roles in *Jurassic Park*, *The Piano*, and *Peaky Blinders*, died on Monday 13 July 2026 in Sydney, Australia, aged 78, surrounded by family. His death was described as sudden and unexpected, despite him having recently been declared cancer-free following a 2022 diagnosis of stage three angioimmunoblastic T-cell lymphoma. Over a five-decade career spanning more than 150 credits, Neill built a reputation for charismatic romantic leads and compelling villains across both film and television.

## Key takeaways
- Neill died on 13 July 2026 in Sydney; no cause of death was given, though his family noted he remained cancer-free at the time.
- He was born in Northern Ireland in 1947 and moved to New Zealand at age seven, later becoming one of the country's most celebrated cultural exports.
- His breakthrough came with *Jurassic Park* (1993) as Dr. Alan Grant — a role originally offered to Harrison Ford — and with Jane Campion's Oscar-winning *The Piano* the same year.
- He had over 150 credits across five decades, including *Peaky Blinders*, *Hunt for the Wilderpeople*, *Reilly: Ace of Spies*, and the *Jurassic World* sequels.
- Neill was a serious contender to replace Roger Moore as James Bond in 1986 but lost the role to Timothy Dalton.
- In 2023 he published a memoir disclosing his cancer diagnosis and underwent monthly chemotherapy for the rest of his life, even after remission.
- He was knighted in 2022 and owned a winery, Two Paddocks, in Central Otago, New Zealand.
- He is survived by four children and six grandchildren.

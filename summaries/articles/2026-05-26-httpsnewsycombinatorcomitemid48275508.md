# Ask HN: Is anyone working at least 4 hours daily on an Apple Vision Pro?

Source: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48275508

## Summary
A Hacker News thread asking whether anyone uses the Apple Vision Pro (AVP) for 4+ hours of daily work, a follow-up to a similar 2024 thread. Responses are mixed: a handful of users do use it extensively as a productivity tool (primarily as a virtual monitor for macOS), while others stopped due to app quality issues, comfort limitations, or simply prefer traditional screens. The broader discussion touches on AVP's uncertain future, its niche appeal, and whether spatial computing will eventually go mainstream.

## Key takeaways
- **Primary use case is virtual monitor, not immersion.** Heavy users run it as a large virtual display connected to a MacBook/Mac Mini, rarely using full VR/immersive environments.
- **Travel is a standout use case.** Several users find it more practical than a laptop on planes/trains — no tray table needed, no bright screen disturbing others.
- **Comfort limits daily wear.** Weight and heat buildup make extended sessions (2+ hours) uncomfortable; strap choice matters significantly.
- **App ecosystem is a major weakness.** Missing native apps (Netflix, Spotify), MAM/enterprise compatibility issues, and under-investment from developers are cited as blockers.
- **AVP's future is uncertain but not dead.** Reports of cancellation were walked back; it appears on hold while Apple focuses on a glasses form factor.
- **Price point and developer relations are structural problems.** Apple's historically hostile stance toward developers led major streaming platforms to sit out the AVP launch.
- **AI investment is cannibalizing VR/AR capital.** One commenter notes that funding that previously flowed into VR is now going to AI.
- **Long-term optimism remains.** Several commenters believe spatial computing is the future, just 10–20 years away from mainstream adoption.

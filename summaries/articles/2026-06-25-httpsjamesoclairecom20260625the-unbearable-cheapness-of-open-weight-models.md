# The Unbearable Cheapness of Open Weight Models

Source: https://jamesoclaire.com/2026/06/25/the-unbearable-cheapness-of-open-weight-models/

## Summary
James O'Claire observes a dramatic pricing gap between Chinese open-weight models like DeepSeek V4 (~$0.09/M tokens) and Western frontier models from Anthropic and OpenAI (~$5.00/M tokens) — nearly a 50x difference. He argues that Anthropic and OpenAI have painted themselves into a high-cost corner and are responding by positioning their models as luxury/premium products rather than competing on price. He also raises concern that these companies may lobby for government restrictions on Chinese open-weight models to manufacture scarcity, and calls for the US to produce competitive open-source models instead.

## Key takeaways
- DeepSeek V4 costs roughly 50x less per token than Anthropic/OpenAI frontier models, a gap that is hard to explain by capability alone.
- Anthropic and OpenAI appear to be adopting a luxury-brand strategy — using premium branding and access gates to justify high prices on what is becoming a commodity product.
- The author fears these companies will push for government bans on Chinese open-weight models as a way to suppress competition rather than competing on merit.
- True open-source models (with open training data pipelines, e.g., Allen AI's OLMo) are presented as the long-term answer, with the US NSF/Nvidia/Allen AI collaboration as a promising development.
- Open-weight models may be cheap due to broad community hardware optimization, loss-leader pricing, or both — the article doesn't resolve which.

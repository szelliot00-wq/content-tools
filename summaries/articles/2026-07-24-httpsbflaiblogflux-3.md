# Flux 3

Source: https://bfl.ai/blog/flux-3

## Summary
Black Forest Labs announced FLUX 3, a new multimodal foundation model released in Early Access on July 23, 2026. Unlike previous single-modality models, FLUX 3 jointly learns from images, video, and audio within a unified architecture, treating all modalities as complementary projections of the same underlying reality. The model demonstrates strong performance in video generation (with native audio), image synthesis, and action prediction for physical AI applications like robotics.

## Key takeaways
- FLUX 3 is a unified multimodal model trained simultaneously on images, video, and audio — no separate specialized models per modality.
- Video generation supports up to 20 seconds with native audio at 720p, and outperformed competitors in blind comparisons (e.g., preferred over Runway Gen-4.5 in 77% of comparisons, Luma Ray 3.2 in 93%).
- Image generation shows significant improvements over prior FLUX versions, particularly in handling complex prompts and multilingual text rendering.
- The model extends to action prediction and robotics via FLUX-mimic, a partnership with mimic robotics tested on real production tasks at Audi.
- The underlying approach is called Self-Flow, which aligns multimodal generation and understanding within a single architecture.
- Capabilities are rolling out in phases (Early Access now for video; image and additional features coming in following weeks).
- Long-term goal is to unify perceptual, action, and language prediction into a single model for physical and digital environments.

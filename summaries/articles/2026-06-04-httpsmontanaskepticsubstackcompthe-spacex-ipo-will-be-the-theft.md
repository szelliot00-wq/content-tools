# The SpaceX IPO Will Be the Theft of the Century

Source: https://montanaskeptic.substack.com/p/the-spacex-ipo-will-be-the-theft

## Summary
Written by commercial trial lawyer Lawrence Fossi, this Substack article argues that the upcoming SpaceX IPO is structured to massively enrich insiders (Elon Musk and early investors) at the expense of ordinary retail and retirement investors. The author contends that SpaceX's core technology (Starship) is far from commercially viable, while rule changes by Nasdaq, S&P 500, and FTSE Russell will force passive index fund holders to buy SpaceX shares at inflated valuations. After insiders cash out, public shareholders will face a company with a ~$170B funding gap and no meaningful legal recourse due to mandatory arbitration and restricted voting rights.

## Key takeaways
- **Starship is not viable:** The most advanced version only reached 121 miles altitude vs. the ~300 miles needed for Starlink deployment, making it "functionally useless" for SpaceX's stated business plans.
- **Index rule changes engineered to benefit insiders:** Nasdaq, S&P 500, and FTSE Russell all changed eligibility rules in ways that will force passive index funds to buy SpaceX shares — effectively making millions of 401k/IRA holders involuntary buyers at peak valuations.
- **Low float + forced index buying = artificial price inflation:** Only ~5% of shares will float at IPO, but Nasdaq will weight it as if 15% float, then switch to 100% of shares outstanding after the 180-day lock-up expires — timed to coincide with a December index rebalance.
- **Massive capital shortfall post-IPO:** The IPO raises ~$85B but SpaceX has ~$235B in capital needs through 2030, guaranteeing heavy dilutive stock issuances for public shareholders.
- **No legal recourse for investors:** Shareholders face mandatory arbitration, no meaningful voting rights, and disputes heard by Musk-friendly Texas courts with no juries.
- **Broader market risks:** The IPO could trigger selling in equities and crypto as investors raise cash, potentially pop the AI investment bubble, and permanently damage public trust in index funds.
- **Don't short it:** Despite poor fundamentals, Musk's cult-like following makes shorting SpaceX extremely dangerous — the author advises simply avoiding the stock entirely.

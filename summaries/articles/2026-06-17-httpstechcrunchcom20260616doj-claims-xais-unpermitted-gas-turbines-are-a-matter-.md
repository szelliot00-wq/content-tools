# DOJ claims xAI's gas turbines are a matter of 'national and energy security'

Source: https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/16/doj-claims-xais-unpermitted-gas-turbines-are-a-matter-of-national-economic-and-energy-security/

## Summary
The DOJ has intervened on xAI's behalf in a lawsuit filed by the NAACP, arguing that shutting down xAI's 57 unpermitted natural gas turbines at its Memphis data centers would threaten national, economic, and energy security. The DOJ specifically cited Grok as one of four AI models supporting "mission-critical" military operations. The turbines, which xAI claims are exempt from state pollution rules by remaining on trailers, have worsened air quality in an already heavily polluted region.

## Key takeaways
- The DOJ sided with xAI against an NAACP/Southern Environmental Law Center lawsuit, framing the turbines as essential to national security and military operations.
- xAI operates 57 unpermitted natural gas turbines at its Memphis Colossus data centers, more than doubling since last year.
- xAI argues the turbines are exempt from Mississippi air pollution regulations because they remain on trailers; federal law may still classify them as stationary sources subject to regulation.
- The turbines have increased three major pollutants — PM2.5, formaldehyde, and NOx — all linked to serious health conditions including asthma, cancer, and cardiovascular disease.
- xAI is now a division of SpaceX, which plans to spend $2.8 billion on additional gas turbines over the next three years, with at least $2 billion earmarked for mobile units.

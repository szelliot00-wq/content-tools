# Tesla's supervised self-driving software gets Dutch okay, first in Europe

Source: https://www.reuters.com/business/teslas-self-driving-software-gets-dutch-go-ahead-boost-eu-ambitions-2026-04-10/

## Summary
Tesla's "Full Self-Driving" (FSD) software has received its first European regulatory approval from the Dutch vehicle authority RDW, allowing its use on European roads. This approval is for a Level 2 assisted driving system, meaning it requires constant driver supervision and hands-on engagement despite its advanced capabilities like autonomous lane changes, junction navigation, and parking. This marks a significant step for Tesla, enabling it to potentially expand its FSD offering as a monthly subscription service to customers across Europe, similar to its U.S. model.

## Key takeaways
- Tesla's "Full Self-Driving" (FSD) software has been approved by the Dutch RDW, making it the first such Level 2 assisted driving system to gain regulatory clearance for use in Europe.
- The approval specifically designates FSD as a Level 2 system, emphasizing that it requires continuous driver supervision, alertness, and hands on the steering wheel, despite its autonomous functionalities.
- This regulatory nod is crucial for Tesla's European expansion strategy, potentially allowing it to sell FSD as a monthly subscription service to customers across the continent.
- FSD's capabilities under this approval include automatic lane changes, navigation through junctions, and autonomous parking, but it is not a fully autonomous system.
- The approval could accelerate the regulatory process for FSD in other European countries, highlighting the evolving landscape of autonomous vehicle regulations within the region.

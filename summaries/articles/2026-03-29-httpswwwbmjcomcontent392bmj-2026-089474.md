# The United States is driving a public health emergency of international concern

Source: https://www.bmj.com/content/392/bmj-2026-089474

## Summary
The article contends that the United States' domestic and international policies and actions are actively exacerbating global public health emergencies. It highlights the US's significant historical and ongoing contributions to climate change, its vaccine nationalism and underfunding of global health security during pandemics, and its economic policies that perpetuate global health inequities. The authors argue that these actions undermine international cooperation and call for a new World Health Organization framework to hold wealthy nations accountable for their global health impacts.

## Key takeaways
- The United States is identified as a primary driver of global public health emergencies due to its policies and actions.
- The US's historical and ongoing contributions to climate change are directly linked to global health crises like heat stress, food insecurity, and vector-borne diseases.
- Vaccine nationalism and insufficient funding for global health security during pandemics, exemplified by the US's response to COVID-19, undermine global health equity and preparedness.
- US economic policies, including intellectual property protection on medicines, hinder global access to essential health technologies and can weaken health systems in lower-income countries.
- The article advocates for a new World Health Organization (WHO) framework, such as an Independent International Health Emergency Committee (IIHEC), to assess and hold nations accountable for their contributions to or mitigation of global health emergencies.

# LinkedIn is a cesspool of scammers and identity theft

Source: https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48916375

## Summary
This is a Hacker News discussion thread (not a standalone article) reacting to a post titled "LinkedIn is a cesspool of scammers and identity theft." Users share grievances about LinkedIn's spam emails, dark UX patterns, scam-heavy content, privacy concerns, and Microsoft's indifference to the platform's deterioration. The consensus is largely negative, with several users having already deleted or minimized their accounts.

## Key takeaways
- LinkedIn is widely perceived as overrun with scammers, fake profiles, and AI-generated content ("LLMs talking to each other").
- The platform uses dark patterns for email — periodically creating new email categories so unsubscribe actions don't fully stick.
- LinkedIn can silently create accounts using linked Google credentials when users click a link, requiring manual cleanup.
- LinkedIn's bad behavior (privacy violations, spam, class action lawsuits) predates its Microsoft acquisition, and Microsoft has not improved things.
- Some users keep dormant accounts solely to prevent username squatting by bad actors.
- There is no clear alternative: job seekers note that competing platforms like Indeed are similarly problematic.
- A minority report that a single full unsubscribe has worked long-term, suggesting inconsistent enforcement of email preferences.

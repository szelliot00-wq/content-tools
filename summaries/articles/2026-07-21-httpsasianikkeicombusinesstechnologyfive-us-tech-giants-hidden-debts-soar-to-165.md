# Five US tech giants' hidden debts soar to $1.65T on opaque AI funding

Source: https://asia.nikkei.com/business/technology/five-us-tech-giants-hidden-debts-soar-to-1.65tn-on-opaque-ai-funding

## Summary
A Nikkei study finds that hidden debt among five major US tech giants has grown eightfold in roughly four years, reaching an estimated $1.65 trillion — surpassing their transparent, on-balance-sheet debt. The liabilities stem primarily from data center leases and GPU supply contracts tied to surging AI investment. Meta is highlighted as a notable example, with off-balance-sheet debt of approximately $420 billion, nearly triple its disclosed debt. This opacity makes it increasingly difficult for investors to accurately assess financial risk at these companies.

## Key takeaways
- Five unnamed US tech giants carry an estimated **$1.65 trillion in hidden (off-balance-sheet) debt**, up ~8x in about four years.
- The liabilities are driven by **data center leases and GPU supply contracts** — commitments that don't appear directly on balance sheets.
- **Meta's off-balance-sheet debt alone is ~$420 billion**, nearly 3x its transparent/disclosed debt.
- Hidden debt now **exceeds actual reported debt** at these companies, raising serious risk-assessment challenges for investors.
- The rapid growth of AI infrastructure spending is the primary driver of this liability expansion.

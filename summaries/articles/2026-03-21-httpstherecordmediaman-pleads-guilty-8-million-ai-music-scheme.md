# Man pleads guilty to $8M AI-generated music scheme

Source: https://therecord.media/man-pleads-guilty-8-million-ai-music-scheme

## Summary
Matthew Stikkers, 26, pleaded guilty to wire fraud for orchestrating an $8 million scheme involving AI-generated music. He created shell companies and fake artist profiles across various streaming platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music, then used bots and other illicit methods to generate millions of artificial streams. The scheme defrauded music distributors and streaming services by claiming royalties for non-existent listens, with the actual music often being AI-generated compositions.

## Key takeaways
- An individual successfully defrauded music streaming platforms and distributors of $8 million using AI-generated music and bot-driven artificial streams.
- The scheme involved creating fake artists and shell companies to distribute AI-created music, which was then streamed millions of times by bots to generate fraudulent royalties.
- This case highlights vulnerabilities in music streaming royalty systems to sophisticated fraud schemes, especially those leveraging AI for content creation and bots for artificial engagement.
- Matthew Stikkers faces significant penalties, including up to 20 years in prison and a substantial fine, underscoring the legal risks of such digital fraud.

# Jurassic Park computers in excruciating detail

Source: https://fabiensanglard.net/jurrasic_park_computers/index.html

## Summary
Fabien Sanglard's article meticulously identifies and documents every real computer shown in the 1993 film Jurassic Park. The production used millions of dollars worth of actual hardware loaned by Silicon Graphics and Apple, which was considered essential because audiences were too sophisticated to be fooled by fakes. The article cross-references film screenshots with period manuals, ads, and technical documentation to identify each machine.

## Key takeaways
- The film featured roughly $1.725 million worth of real computer hardware, including $875,000 from Silicon Graphics and $350,000 from Apple.
- Identified machines include the Apple PowerBook 100, SGI R4000 Indigo, SGI IRIS Crimson, Macintosh Quadra 700, Thinking Machines CM-5, and a Motorola Envoy.
- The Thinking Machines CM-5 is the supercomputer Dennis Nedry brags about in his famous monologue ("network 8 connection machines...").
- One unidentified device was later traced by a Hacker News user to an original frogdesign mockup that the designer showed to Spielberg on a plane.
- The 3D file system navigator shown in the film ("It's a Unix system!") was a real SGI IRIX tool called the 3D File System Navigator.
- Nedry's workstation code was real and written using Classic Mac OS API functions, not gibberish.
- The production went to great lengths for authenticity — even period-correct keyboards with 6-pin Mini-DIN connectors were correctly matched to their SGI workstations.

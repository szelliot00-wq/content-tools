# Returning to Rails in 2026

Source: https://www.markround.com/blog/2026/03/05/returning-to-rails-in-2026/

## Summary
The article, a speculative piece from March 2026, recounts the author's positive experience returning to Ruby on Rails after a prolonged hiatus. Despite initial expectations of increased complexity, the author discovers that Rails has significantly evolved, integrating powerful tools like Hotwire for frontend development, simplifying containerization with Docker, and streamlining deployment. The author concludes that Rails maintains its exceptional developer productivity and remains a highly effective and enjoyable framework for building modern web applications, successfully adapting to contemporary development needs.

## Key takeaways
- Rails in 2026, particularly with Hotwire (Turbo and Stimulus), effectively addresses frontend challenges without necessitating a separate single-page application (SPA) framework, significantly boosting developer productivity.
- The overall developer experience (DX) and speed of development remain core strengths, making Rails a highly productive choice for building robust applications quickly.
- Modern operational aspects like containerization (`rails new --docker`) and streamlined deployment to platforms such as Fly.io and Render are well-integrated and simplify infrastructure management.
- Significant improvements in database interaction (e.g., `insert_all`, `upsert_all`) and mature background job processing (Active Job with GoodJob) enhance application performance and scalability.
- The broader Rails ecosystem, including testing (RSpec), authentication (Devise), and real-time features (Action Cable), continues to be robust, well-supported, and easily integrated.
- Contrary to some past predictions of its decline, Rails has successfully adapted and evolved with modern web development trends, proving its continued relevance and vitality in 2026.

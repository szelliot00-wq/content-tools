# There is a shadow hanging over this Fable thing

Source: https://12gramsofcarbon.com/p/tech-things-there-is-a-massive-shadow

## Summary
The author (theahura, writing for *12 Grams of Carbon*) describes being interrupted while vibe-coding on a Friday when their AI agent suddenly stopped working — because the US government issued an export control directive forcing Anthropic to disable all access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for every user worldwide. The stated rationale is a discovered jailbreak technique, though Anthropic disputes its severity and notes the capability is available in other public models. The author is deeply conflicted, acknowledging legitimate AI safety concerns while expressing serious skepticism about the current administration's motives given its history of politically motivated corporate targeting.

## Key takeaways
- The US government ordered Anthropic to shut off Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all users globally, citing a jailbreak that Anthropic says is minor and already replicable by other public models like GPT-5.5.
- The directive was issued at 5:21 PM on a Friday — a timing pattern the author links to the administration's repeated practice of releasing market-sensitive news after trading hours to dampen immediate volatility.
- The author suspects political motivation: the Trump administration has previously tried to declare Anthropic a supply chain risk while continuing to use its models militarily, and Anthropic's competitors (e.g., OpenAI) have allies inside the administration.
- The shutdown could undermine investor confidence in the entire AI infrastructure buildout — if the government can unilaterally cut access to a model used by hundreds of millions, the multi-trillion-dollar data center investment thesis looks shakier.
- The author notes an ironic dynamic: AI CEOs warning loudly about their own products' dangers may have handed regulators (and political adversaries) the justification to pull those products from the market.
- Silicon Valley figures who warned a Democratic government would "pick winners and losers" in AI are now silent or supportive, which the author reads as cynical opportunism rather than principled concern.

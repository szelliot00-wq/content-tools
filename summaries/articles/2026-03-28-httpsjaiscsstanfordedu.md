# Go hard on agents, not on your filesystem

Source: https://jai.scs.stanford.edu/

## Summary
The article "Go hard on agents, not on your filesystem" critiques current AI agent designs that often operate in isolated, self-contained environments, leading to friction with human users' established workflows. It advocates for a "low-friction" approach where agents act as powerful, iterative assistants that seamlessly integrate with and leverage the user's existing tools like the filesystem, text editors, and version control. The core idea is to design agents that augment human capabilities by providing quick, editable suggestions, allowing humans to maintain control and refine the output within their familiar environments. This fosters human-agent synergy, making agents "good enough" for rapid iteration rather than striving for isolated perfection.

## Key takeaways
- Current AI agent designs often create an "uncanny valley" experience by operating in isolated, self-contained environments, leading to friction with existing user workflows.
- Agents should be designed with a "low-friction" approach, integrating seamlessly with and leveraging users' existing tools like the filesystem, text editors, and version control.
- The goal is to augment human capabilities by providing quick, iterative suggestions and drafts, making it easy for users to accept, modify, or reject output within their familiar environments.
- Agents should enable rapid iteration and exploration of ideas, effectively reducing the "cost of trying" new approaches.
- The emphasis is on fostering "human-agent synergy," where agents handle initial drafting and repetitive tasks, freeing humans to focus on higher-level reasoning, creativity, and refinement.

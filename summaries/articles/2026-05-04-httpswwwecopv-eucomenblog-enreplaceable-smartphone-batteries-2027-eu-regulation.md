# Removable batteries in smartphones will be mandatory in the EU starting in 2027

Source: https://www.ecopv-eu.com/en/blog-en/replaceable-smartphone-batteries-2027-eu-regulation/

## Summary
The European Union has passed a regulation requiring smartphones sold in the EU to feature user-replaceable batteries starting in 2027. This legislation is part of the EU's broader push toward sustainability, repairability, and reducing electronic waste. The rule aims to give consumers more control over their devices and extend the overall lifespan of smartphones.

## Key takeaways
- **Mandatory replaceable batteries**: All smartphones sold in the EU must have user-removable/replaceable batteries by 2027.
- **Environmental goal**: The regulation is designed to reduce e-waste and promote a more circular economy for electronics.
- **Consumer empowerment**: Users will be able to replace their own batteries without needing manufacturer servicing, potentially saving money and extending device lifespans.
- **Industry impact**: Smartphone manufacturers, including major brands, will need to redesign their devices to comply with EU standards.
- **Part of broader EU policy**: This rule fits within the EU's wider Right to Repair initiative, which seeks to make electronics more durable and repairable.

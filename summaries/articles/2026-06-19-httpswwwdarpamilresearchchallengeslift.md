# DARPA Heavy Life Challenge

Source: https://www.darpa.mil/research/challenges/lift

## Summary
The DARPA Lift Challenge is a $6.5 million prize competition aimed at revolutionizing heavy vertical-lift drone technology. It challenges university researchers, independent innovators, and industry teams to design drones capable of carrying payloads more than four times their own weight — far exceeding the current typical 1:1 payload-to-weight ratio. The competition finals will be held at the National Museum of the U.S. Air Force in Dayton, Ohio, August 2–9, 2026, with public access August 6–9.

## Key takeaways
- Current multirotor drones have a low payload-to-weight ratio (typically 1:1 or less), limiting their usefulness in military, commercial, and emergency response applications.
- The Lift Challenge sets a target of drones carrying payloads more than 4x their own weight, which would be a major performance breakthrough.
- DARPA is offering $6.5 million in prize money to incentivize non-traditional solvers — not just established defense contractors — including universities and independent innovators.
- The competition takes place August 2–9, 2026 at the National Museum of the U.S. Air Force in Dayton, Ohio; public attendance is open August 6–9 (limited seats, reservation required).
- Applications span both military (complex warfighter missions) and civilian sectors (infrastructure inspection, package delivery, disaster response).

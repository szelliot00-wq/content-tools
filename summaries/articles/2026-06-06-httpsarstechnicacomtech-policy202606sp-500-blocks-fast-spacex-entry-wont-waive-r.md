# S&P 500 rejects SpaceX, also blocking entry for OpenAI and Anthropic

Source: https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/06/sp-500-blocks-fast-spacex-entry-wont-waive-rule-for-unprofitable-ai-firms/

## Summary
The S&P 500 index (managed by S&P Dow Jones Indices) rejected SpaceX's request for expedited entry following its IPO, refusing to bend standard rules for so-called "MegaCap" companies. SpaceX had sought waivers on multiple requirements — including the 12-month seasoning period, the 10% public float rule, and profitability requirements — to accommodate its plan to only offer ~3% of shares publicly and its current unprofitable status with $29B in debt. The decision also closes the door on similar fast-track index entry for AI companies like OpenAI and Anthropic after their anticipated IPOs.

## Key takeaways
- S&P Dow Jones Indices held a monthlong consultation on whether to create special rules for MegaCap companies, then declined to do so.
- SpaceX wanted waivers on three key S&P 500 requirements: the 12-month IPO seasoning period (shortened to 6 months), the minimum 10% public float, and quarterly profitability standards.
- SpaceX is currently unprofitable with $29B in debt, driven largely by spending on AI infrastructure.
- The rejection blocks a potential precedent that OpenAI and Anthropic could have used to gain accelerated S&P 500 entry after their own IPOs.
- The ruling is seen as a relief by those concerned about passive investors and retirement funds gaining outsized exposure to speculative AI and space ventures.

# Optimizing Content for Agents

Source: https://cra.mr/optimizing-content-for-agents/

## Summary
The article advocates for "Agent Optimization" (AO), a new approach to content creation that prioritizes machine understanding and actionability for AI agents. It argues that as AI agents become primary consumers of information, content must be structured and presented in a way that allows them to efficiently browse, summarize, synthesize, and act upon data. The piece emphasizes moving beyond human-centric design to create content that serves as a reliable, machine-readable knowledge base.

## Key takeaways
- Content creators must optimize their digital content for AI agents, a concept dubbed "Agent Optimization" (AO), similar to how SEO optimized for search engines.
- Semantic structure is paramount: utilize proper HTML (headings, lists, `article` tags), structured data (Schema.org, JSON-LD), and avoid formats like PDFs that are difficult for agents to parse.
- Content should be clear, concise, complete, and unambiguous, avoiding jargon or providing explanations, to ensure agents can easily understand and extract information.
- Embed actionable information, including clear calls to action, identified entities, and structured data for specific tasks (e.g., booking, purchasing, contacting).
- Multimodal considerations are important; provide descriptive alt text for images, captions, and transcripts for videos to enhance agent comprehension.
- Establish expertise, experience, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness (E-E-A-T) through clear author information, publication dates, and reputation signals to build agent trust.
- Regularly test content with AI tools (e.g., LLMs) to evaluate how agents perceive and utilize the information, and iterate based on the feedback received.

# Meta is adding rate limits and soft paywall to smart glasses

Source: https://www.theverge.com/gadgets/959899/meta-ai-glasses-paywall-rate-limit

## Summary
Meta is introducing rate limits and a subscription paywall ($19.99/month Meta One Premium) for certain AI features on its Ray-Ban smart glasses, most notably the Conversation Focus feature. The Verge's Sean Hollister argues this is disingenuous because Conversation Focus runs entirely on-device and doesn't use Meta's servers — it even works in Airplane Mode — making the "rate limit" justification hollow. Free users will be capped at 3 hours/month; paid subscribers get 15 hours/month.

## Key takeaways
- Meta is capping the Conversation Focus feature at 3 hours/month for free users, with 15 hours/month available via a $19.99/month Meta One Premium subscription.
- Conversation Focus amplifies nearby voices in noisy environments and runs fully on-device — it requires no internet connection and uses no Meta servers, making the rate limit rationale highly questionable.
- Meta framed this as a "rate limit," not a paywall, claiming all glasses owners still get "free monthly usage" — but critics see this as semantics.
- The move likely reflects financial pressure: Meta recently laid off ~8,000 employees (~10% of its workforce) partly to offset rising AI investment costs.
- This sets a concerning precedent for hardware makers charging recurring fees for features that run on hardware customers already paid for.

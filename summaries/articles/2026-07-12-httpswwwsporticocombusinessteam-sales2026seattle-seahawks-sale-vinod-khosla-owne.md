# Vinod Khosla to Buy Seattle Seahawks for $9.6B

Source: https://www.sportico.com/business/team-sales/2026/seattle-seahawks-sale-vinod-khosla-owner-billions-1234938616/

## Summary
Tech billionaire Vinod Khosla and his family have agreed to purchase the Seattle Seahawks for $9.6 billion from the estate of late owner Paul Allen. The deal ranks among the largest sports team transactions ever, trailing only the $10 billion sale of the LA Lakers. Khosla, co-founder of Sun Microsystems and founder of Khosla Ventures, beat out competing bids including one from billionaire Aditya Mittal.

## Key takeaways
- The $9.6B price is one of the largest control-sale valuations in sports history, well above the Seahawks' estimated $6.59B market value.
- Khosla's wife Neeru will serve as the NFL-designated control owner; their son Neal will also hold a leadership role.
- The sale fulfills the terms of Paul Allen's will, which directed his sports assets (Seahawks and Portland Trail Blazers) to be sold for charitable causes. The Trail Blazers were separately sold for $4.1B to a group led by Tom Dundon.
- Khosla must sell his minority stake in the San Francisco 49ers before this deal closes, per NFL rules.
- NFL ownership financing rules cap debt at $1.5B and require the control owner to hold at least 30% equity.
- Khosla had previously invested in the 49ers at a valuation above $8.5B, giving him prior familiarity with NFL ownership — a common path for new NFL control buyers.

# US Court of Appeals: TOS may be updated by email, use can imply consent [pdf]

Source: https://cdn.ca9.uscourts.gov/datastore/memoranda/2026/03/03/25-403.pdf

## Summary
The US Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit affirmed a district court's decision to compel arbitration in *Pattiz v. Google LLC*. The court ruled that Google provided reasonable notice of updated Terms of Service (TOS) by sending an email with a link to the new terms and explicitly stating that continued use implied acceptance. By continuing to use Google's services after receiving this notification, the user implicitly consented to the updated TOS, including its arbitration clause.

## Key takeaways
- Companies can effectively update their Terms of Service (TOS) and bind users to them by providing clear email notification of changes.
- For such updates to be valid, the email notification must clearly inform users of the changes, provide an accessible link to the full updated TOS, and explicitly state that continued use of the service will constitute acceptance of the new terms.
- Users' continued use of a service after receiving reasonable and explicit notice of updated TOS is considered an unambiguous manifestation of assent, implying consent to the new terms, including potentially critical provisions like arbitration clauses.
- This ruling reinforces the enforceability of "sign-in wrap" or similar agreements where direct notification combined with continued usage establishes a binding contract.

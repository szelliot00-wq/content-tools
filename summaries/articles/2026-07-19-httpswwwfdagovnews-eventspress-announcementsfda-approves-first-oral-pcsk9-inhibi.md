# FDA approves new kind of cholesterol pill

Source: https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-approves-first-oral-pcsk9-inhibitor-lower-ldl-cholesterol-adults-high-cholesterol

## Summary
The FDA approved Lipfendra (enlicitide) on July 17, 2026 — the first oral PCSK9 inhibitor for lowering LDL ("bad") cholesterol in adults with hypercholesterolemia, including those with hereditary high cholesterol (HeFH). Previously, PCSK9 inhibitors were only available as injectable therapies. Clinical trials across 3,207 patients showed LDL reductions of 56–59% compared to placebo. The drug was approved for Merck Sharp & Dohme LLC under Priority Review.

## Key takeaways
- **First oral PCSK9 inhibitor**: Lipfendra is a once-daily pill, making it more convenient than existing PCSK9 inhibitors which required injections.
- **Strong efficacy**: Reduced LDL cholesterol by an average of 56% in high-risk cardiovascular patients and 59% in HeFH patients at 24 weeks, on top of existing statin therapy.
- **Broad indication**: Approved as an add-on to diet, exercise, and maximally tolerated statin therapy for adults who still need further LDL reduction.
- **Favorable safety profile**: Adverse reaction rates were similar to placebo in the first trial; the second trial noted diarrhea and dizziness as the most common side effects, with comparable discontinuation rates.
- **Addresses a major public health need**: Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death in the U.S., and elevated LDL is a key modifiable risk factor.
- **Accelerated review**: Received Priority Review and was evaluated under the FDA's Commissioner's National Priority Voucher (CNPV) pilot program.

# Winners of the 2026 Kokuyo Design Awards

Source: https://spoon-tamago.com/winners-of-the-2026-kokuyo-design-awards/

## Summary
The article announces the winners of the 2026 Kokuyo Design Awards, centered on the theme "Circulation" (巡る), which explores the flow and connection of things in our lives. The Grand Prix was awarded to Miki Seki's "Paper Weight," a clever design that transforms small paper scraps into functional and aesthetic weights by layering and gluing them. Other notable winners included "A Bottle for One Flower," a minimalist vase made from a recycled paper tube, and "Tsugi," a system for binding together partially used notebooks. The awards highlight innovative approaches to sustainability and everyday utility in stationery design.

## Key takeaways
- The Kokuyo Design Awards actively promotes innovative design, particularly focusing on how everyday items can embody sustainability and mindful consumption.
- The 2026 awards theme, "Circulation," encouraged designers to consider the lifecycle of materials and the interconnectedness of products in daily life.
- The Grand Prix winner, "Paper Weight," exemplifies how simple design can effectively address waste by upcycling common scraps into useful and appealing objects.
- Other awarded designs, such as "A Bottle for One Flower" and "Tsugi," also showcase creative solutions for material reuse and extending product longevity in practical ways.
- The awards emphasize a blend of aesthetic appeal, functional utility, and environmental consciousness in stationery and office product design.

# Micron locks in historically high memory prices for five years

Source: https://www.theregister.com/systems/2026/06/25/micron-locks-in-historically-high-memory-prices-for-five-years/5261854

## Summary
Micron has signed 16 "strategic customer agreements" (SCAs) with major buyers, locking in memory prices at historically high levels through 2030. The deals include a price floor that guarantees gross margins exceeding Micron's best-ever quarterly margins, while a price ceiling protects buyers if prices rise further. Micron's Q3 results reflect the ongoing memory shortage, with revenue up 346% year-over-year and net income of $28.9 billion at an 84.9% gross margin.

## Key takeaways
- Micron signed 16 five-year SCAs (mostly 2026–2030) that lock in a price floor delivering margins "well above" any prior peak — historically exceptional profitability guaranteed for years.
- Customers are accepting these terms because Micron's CEO sees no near-term relief: supply shortages are expected to persist, with industry supply only beginning gradual improvement around 2028.
- New fab construction won't close the gap — increasing complexity of memory types (especially high-bandwidth memory for AI) means factories take longer to build and still won't produce enough.
- SCAs cover only ~40% of Micron's revenue; the remaining inventory will be sold at spot or negotiated prices, keeping upside open.
- Customers pay upfront under the SCAs, giving Micron capital to fund further fab expansion.
- Q3 revenue hit $41.5 billion (346% YoY); Q4 guidance is $50 billion with ~86% gross margins — records expected to keep climbing.
- IT buyers face real pain: server DRAM content per unit is shrinking as customers prioritize shipping more units over more memory per unit amid tight allocations.

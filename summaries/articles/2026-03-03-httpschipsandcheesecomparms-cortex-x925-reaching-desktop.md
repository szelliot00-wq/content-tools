# Arm's Cortex X925: Reaching Desktop Performance

Source: https://chipsandcheese.com/p/arms-cortex-x925-reaching-desktop

## Summary
The Arm Cortex X925 aims to close the performance gap with desktop x86 CPUs, featuring significant architectural improvements like a wider instruction fetch and decode, larger caches, and enhanced branch prediction. Benchmarks suggest the X925 can achieve performance parity with or even surpass some mainstream x86 desktop processors in single-threaded workloads. While still facing challenges in multi-core scaling and power efficiency at peak performance, the X925 represents Arm's most competitive step yet towards high-performance computing beyond traditional mobile devices.

## Key takeaways
- The Arm Cortex X925 is Arm's latest high-performance core, designed to compete directly with desktop x86 CPUs.
- It features major architectural enhancements, including a 16-wide instruction fetch, 10-wide decode, larger L1/L2 caches, and an improved branch predictor.
- Early benchmarks indicate the X925 can achieve single-threaded performance comparable to or exceeding some Intel Raptor Lake/Meteor Lake and AMD Zen 4 desktop CPUs.
- The X925 achieves this performance while potentially offering better power efficiency in many scenarios, though power consumption increases significantly at its highest performance levels.
- While single-core performance is impressive, multi-core scaling and sustained power efficiency under heavy multi-threaded loads remain areas for further optimization compared to established desktop platforms.
- This core is a crucial step for Arm in expanding its presence in laptops, desktops, and potentially servers, demonstrating its capability to deliver competitive high-end performance.

# YouTube locked my accounts and I can't cancel my subscription

Source: https://pocketables.com/2026/04/ai-music-corporate-control-and-the-creator-who-cant-even-leave.html

## Summary
This article details the troubling extent of corporate control over creators' digital lives, specifically through the lens of a user unable to cancel their YouTube Premium subscription after their accounts were locked. The user's primary YouTube account was rendered inaccessible due to a suspected bot attack on an entirely separate, non-primary Google account, leading to a perpetual charge for a service they cannot access or cancel. Despite exhaustive attempts to verify identity and resolve the issue with unresponsive customer support, the platform's automated systems and lack of human recourse have effectively held the user's digital assets and subscription hostage. The piece critiques this trend, arguing that it exemplifies how corporations, increasingly reliant on AI, trap users within their ecosystems, denying them autonomy and access to their own content and finances.

## Key takeaways
-   Corporations like Google (YouTube) exert significant and often unassailable control over users' digital accounts and services, making it difficult to exit or manage subscriptions.
-   Automated account security measures, even when triggered by unrelated issues on secondary accounts, can lead to primary account lockouts and an inability to cancel paid subscriptions.
-   Lack of effective human customer support and reliance on automated AI systems leave users with no recourse when digital services become inaccessible or subscriptions cannot be canceled.
-   This issue highlights the dangers of centralizing one's digital life on single platforms, as users can be held hostage to platform policies and technical issues, potentially losing access to content and incurring unwanted charges.
-   The incident serves as a cautionary tale about the need for greater user autonomy, transparency, and accessible support within digital ecosystems to prevent users from being trapped.

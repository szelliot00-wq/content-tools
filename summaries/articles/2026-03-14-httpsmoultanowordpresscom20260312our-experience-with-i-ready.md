# Our Experience with I-Ready

Source: https://moultano.wordpress.com/2026/03/12/our-experience-with-i-ready/

## Summary
The article details a parent's highly negative experience with I-Ready, an educational software mandated in their child's elementary school. The author criticizes its excessively long and stressful diagnostic assessments, which often lead to student fatigue and disengagement. Furthermore, the personalized lessons are described as repetitive, boring, and inflexible, failing to genuinely adapt to individual student needs or pace. Ultimately, the parent concludes that I-Ready is a poorly designed time-sink that hinders effective learning and risks fostering a negative attitude towards education.

## Key takeaways
-   I-Ready diagnostic assessments are criticized for being excessively long (up to 90 minutes) and stressful for elementary students, potentially leading to fatigue and inaccurate data.
-   The personalized learning content is often repetitive, boring, and inflexible, failing to adequately adapt to individual student pace or existing knowledge.
-   The program's gamification elements are deemed insufficient to sustain student engagement given the perceived low quality and tedium of the lessons.
-   Mandated time spent on I-Ready is viewed as a significant time-sink that detracts from more effective and engaging educational activities.
-   The author concludes that I-Ready, in their experience, hinders genuine learning and can foster disengagement rather than promoting a positive attitude towards education.
-   The adoption and continued use of such programs are often driven by district mandates, potentially overriding teacher discretion or student-centric pedagogical approaches.

# Amiga 1000: Ten years ahead of its time

Source: https://dfarq.homeip.net/amiga-1000-ten-years-ahead-of-its-time/

## Summary
Published on July 22, 2026 by Dave Farquhar, this article commemorates the 41st anniversary of the Commodore Amiga 1000 (launched July 23, 1985), arguing it was a decade ahead of its time. The Amiga featured full preemptive multitasking, color graphics with up to 4,096 colors, stereo sound, and a GUI — capabilities that PCs didn't match until Windows 95. Despite its technical brilliance, Commodore's poor marketing and corrupt leadership under Irving Gould doomed the platform, and the company went bankrupt less than nine years after the Amiga's debut.

## Key takeaways
- The Amiga 1000 had full preemptive multitasking in 1985 — the same capability Windows didn't deliver until 1995, a full decade later.
- Its custom chipset enabled 4,096-color graphics, stereo sound, and a GUI running on just a 7.14 MHz Motorola 68000 CPU and 256 KB of RAM.
- A fully configured system cost around $2,000 at launch, but was competitive with Apple's $2,495 Mac despite being far more capable.
- Early instability was a software problem, not a hardware one — developers eventually learned to write well-behaved Amiga code.
- Commodore's fatal flaw was marketing: engineers built an extraordinary machine but leadership had no vision for selling it, unlike Apple.
- CEO Irving Gould is blamed for looting the company, accelerating its 1994 bankruptcy — a management failure, not a product failure.
- Commodore sold approximately 4.91 million Amigas total; PCs didn't fully catch up until 1995 when Windows 95 arrived.

# Ex-Apple engineer says Apple deliberately slows older phones via updates

Source: https://www.macobserver.com/news/ex-apple-software-engineer-claims-apple-deliberately-slows-down-old-iphone-models/

## Summary
A woman claiming to be a former Apple software engineer alleged in a viral video that Apple deliberately pushes hidden code in software updates to slow down older iPhones, forcing users to upgrade. The claim has reignited debate around planned obsolescence and draws comparisons to the 2017 "Batterygate" scandal, in which Apple admitted to throttling older devices — though it attributed that to battery management, not manipulation. The new allegation remains unverified and Apple has not responded.

## Key takeaways
- An alleged ex-Apple engineer claims the company intentionally injects code into updates to degrade older iPhone performance around new product launches.
- The claim is unproven and Apple has not issued any response.
- It echoes the 2017 Batterygate controversy, where Apple admitted to slowing older iPhones but said it was to prevent unexpected shutdowns due to aging batteries.
- The viral video has sparked renewed public discussion about planned obsolescence.
- Separately, the iPhone 18 Pro is rumored to feature under-display Face ID sensors and replace the pill-shaped cutout with a smaller punch hole.

# US appeals court declares 158-year-old home distilling ban unconstitutional

Source: https://www.theguardian.com/law/2026/apr/11/appeals-court-ruling-home-distilling-ban-unconstitutional

## Summary
A U.S. appeals court has issued a landmark ruling, declaring a 158-year-old federal ban on home distilling unconstitutional. This decision overturns a long-standing prohibition, allowing individuals to produce distilled spirits for their own consumption without federal interference. The ruling represents a significant victory for personal liberty and could have broad implications for federal regulatory power over private activities.

## Key takeaways
- A US appeals court has declared the 158-year-old federal ban on home distilling unconstitutional.
- The ruling effectively legalizes the personal production of distilled spirits for individual consumption.
- This decision marks a significant win for personal liberty and challenges the extent of federal regulatory authority over private, non-commercial activities.
- The ruling could set a precedent nationwide, but may face further appeals, potentially to the Supreme Court.

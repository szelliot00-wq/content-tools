# Sal Khan's AI revolution hasn't happened yet

Source: https://www.chalkbeat.org/2026/04/09/sal-khan-reflects-on-ai-in-schools-and-khanmigo/

## Summary
The article reflects on Sal Khan's vision for AI in education, particularly with Khanmigo, acknowledging that the anticipated revolution hasn't yet materialized on a widespread scale. Despite initial excitement and promising pilot programs, Khan notes slower adoption rates and significant challenges in fully integrating AI as a personalized tutor and teaching assistant in classrooms. He remains optimistic but emphasizes the ongoing work required to overcome technical hurdles, improve effectiveness, and ensure educators embrace these tools.

## Key takeaways
- Sal Khan admits that the "AI revolution" he envisioned for education, particularly with Khanmigo, has not yet fully come to pass, despite early optimism.
- While Khanmigo shows great potential in pilot programs as a personalized tutor and teacher assistant, its widespread adoption and transformative impact are still nascent.
- Teachers play a crucial role in the successful integration of AI, and their comfort level and training are critical factors for adoption.
- Khan acknowledges challenges such as AI "hallucinations" (inaccuracies) and the need for significant refinement to make the tool consistently reliable and effective.
- The article highlights the discrepancy between the high initial hype surrounding AI in education and the current reality of slower, more deliberate implementation and impact.
- Khan remains committed to the vision, believing AI can eventually free up teachers for higher-level tasks and provide truly personalized learning, but recognizes it's a longer process than initially thought.

# Benchmarks in Leipzig

Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05818

## Summary
A group of 49 mathematicians created a benchmark dataset of 100 research-level mathematics questions during a workshop at the Max Planck Institute in Leipzig (April–May 2026). They evaluated state-of-the-art LLMs across three stages of increasing computational effort, finding that the hardest models with extended thinking left only 2 of 100 questions unsolved.

## Key takeaways
- 49 mathematicians hand-crafted 100 hard, research-level math questions with known answers specifically to challenge LLMs
- Stage 1 (single attempt, 5 LLMs): 41 questions completely unsolved
- Stage 2 (20 runs each, 3 models): unsolved count dropped to 16
- Stage 3 (3 runs, 2 "heavy-thinking" models): only 2 questions remained unsolved
- The rapid improvement across stages suggests that more compute and extended reasoning ("thinking") modes dramatically boost LLM mathematical capability
- The results indicate frontier LLMs now have impressive but not yet complete mathematical reasoning at the research level

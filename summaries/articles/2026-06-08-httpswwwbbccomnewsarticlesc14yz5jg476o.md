# Richard Scolyer Has Died

Source: https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c14yz5jg476o

## Summary
Professor Richard Scolyer, a pioneering Australian pathologist and co-director of the Melanoma Institute Australia, has died at 59, three years after being diagnosed with a glioblastoma brain tumour. He made global headlines in 2023 by becoming the first brain cancer patient to receive combination pre-surgery immunotherapy — a treatment approach he and his colleague Professor Georgina Long developed based on their own melanoma research breakthroughs. He documented his journey publicly and died as he lived: contributing to science and inspiring others.

## Key takeaways
- Scolyer underwent a world-first experimental treatment for glioblastoma in 2023, combining pre-surgery immunotherapy and a personalised tumour vaccine, based on methods he and Long pioneered for melanoma.
- His and Long's melanoma immunotherapy research has dramatically improved survival rates for advanced melanoma patients globally — from under 10% to roughly 50% essentially cured.
- Scans showed a positive immune response following his treatment, and a small US clinical trial is now underway attempting to replicate the results.
- Scolyer and Long were jointly named Australian of the Year in 2024 for their contributions to cancer research.
- In his farewell letter, Scolyer called on scientists to remain bold and inquisitive, and urged governments to fund medical innovation.
- He is survived by his wife Katie Nicholl and three children.

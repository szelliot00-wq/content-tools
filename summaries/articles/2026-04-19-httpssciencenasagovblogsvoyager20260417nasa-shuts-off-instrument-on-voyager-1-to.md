# NASA Shuts Off Instrument on Voyager 1 to Keep Spacecraft Operating

Source: https://science.nasa.gov/blogs/voyager/2026/04/17/nasa-shuts-off-instrument-on-voyager-1-to-keep-spacecraft-operating/

## Summary
NASA has powered down the Plasma Wave Subsystem (PWS) instrument on Voyager 1, the most distant human-made object, to address its declining power levels. This strategic move aims to conserve dwindling electrical power, ensuring essential engineering systems and the ability to transmit data back to Earth remain operational. The measure is critical for extending the historic spacecraft's mission life as it continues its journey through interstellar space, nearly 47 years after its launch.

## Key takeaways
- NASA has switched off the Plasma Wave Subsystem (PWS) instrument on Voyager 1, one of its five remaining active science instruments.
- The primary reason for this action is to conserve the spacecraft's dwindling electrical power supply, generated by its radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTGs).
- The goal is to extend Voyager 1's operational lifespan, prioritizing essential engineering systems and its ability to communicate data back to Earth.
- Voyager 1 is nearly 47 years into its mission and, along with its twin Voyager 2, is the only human-made object operating in interstellar space.
- This power management strategy is part of ongoing efforts to maximize the mission's longevity, even if it means sacrificing some scientific data collection.

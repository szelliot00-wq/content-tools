# AGPLv3§74 Empowers Users to Thwart Badgeware Like OnlyOffice

Source: https://sfconservancy.org/blog/2026/apr/16/badgeware-onlyoffice-nextcloud-affero-gpl/

## Summary
The article from the Software Freedom Conservancy discusses how Section 7, Paragraph 4 (§7§4) of the GNU Affero General Public License version 3 (AGPLv3) provides users with legal protection against "badgeware" — a practice where software vendors add clauses requiring their branding or logos to remain visible in the user interface. The piece uses OnlyOffice, a document editing suite often integrated with Nextcloud, as a specific example of software that imposes such badgeware restrictions. The Conservancy argues that AGPLv3's provisions explicitly nullify these additional restrictions, empowering users and organizations to remove such branding requirements without violating the license.

## Key takeaways
- **Badgeware** refers to additional license clauses that require users to display vendor branding/logos, which the Conservancy considers a restriction on user freedom.
- **AGPLv3 §7§4** specifically prohibits additional restrictions that require display of advertising material, giving users the legal right to ignore or remove badgeware clauses.
- **OnlyOffice** is cited as a real-world example of a project that attempts to impose badgeware conditions despite being licensed under the AGPLv3.
- Users integrating AGPLv3-licensed software (e.g., via Nextcloud) can legally strip out badgeware requirements, as those clauses are rendered unenforceable by the license itself.
- The Software Freedom Conservancy encourages users to understand their rights under copyleft licenses to resist corporate attempts to limit software freedom.

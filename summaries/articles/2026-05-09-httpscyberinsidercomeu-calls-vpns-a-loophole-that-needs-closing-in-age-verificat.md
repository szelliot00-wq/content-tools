# EU calls VPNs "a loophole that needs closing" in age verification push

Source: https://cyberinsider.com/eu-calls-vpns-a-loophole-that-needs-closing-in-age-verification-push/

## Summary
The EU is pushing for stricter age verification measures on online platforms, particularly for adult content, and has identified VPNs as a significant obstacle to enforcing these measures. Officials are characterizing VPNs as a "loophole" that allows users to bypass age verification requirements by masking their location or identity. This signals a potential regulatory push to restrict or regulate VPN usage within the EU as part of broader digital safety and child protection efforts.

## Key takeaways
- The EU is actively pursuing stronger age verification systems for online platforms, especially those hosting adult content.
- European regulators view VPNs as a tool that undermines age verification enforcement, calling them a "loophole that needs closing."
- This stance suggests potential future EU regulations that could restrict VPN usage or require VPN providers to comply with age verification mandates.
- The move raises significant concerns around digital privacy and civil liberties, as VPNs are widely used for legitimate privacy and security purposes.
- The article generated discussion (via Hacker News), reflecting public interest and concern about the implications for internet freedom.

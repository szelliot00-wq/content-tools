# Companies Are Just a Graph of Algorithms

Source: https://danielmiessler.com/blog/companies-graph-of-algorithms

## Summary
Every business, regardless of complexity, can be decomposed into a hierarchical graph of algorithms — discrete, optimizable steps. AI's ability to map, analyze, and continuously optimize these steps means companies will soon face unprecedented pressure to streamline operations, reduce redundancy, and automate workflows. Consulting firms will drive this transformation, and the result will be leaner organizations requiring fewer human workers.

## Key takeaways
- Every business process is an algorithm, and every company is a graph of interconnected algorithms that can be mapped and visualized.
- Transparency (making processes explicit and legible) is the prerequisite for AI-driven optimization — you can't optimize what you haven't articulated.
- AI will enable continuous, ongoing optimization of every department (marketing, HR, customer support, etc.), not just a one-time audit.
- Consulting firms like Accenture, McKinsey, and KPMG will productize this "transparency + optimization" pitch to executive teams.
- No company is exempt — greater complexity just means a larger graph, which is harder for humans but not for AI.
- The net result will be smaller, more efficient companies with significantly fewer human roles.
- The author frames this as a call to prepare — both as a business owner and as an employee — rather than a reason for despair.

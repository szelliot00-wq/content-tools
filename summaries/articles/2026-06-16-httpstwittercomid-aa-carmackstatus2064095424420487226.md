# John Carmack on Fabrice Bellard

Source: https://twitter.com/ID_AA_Carmack/status/2064095424420487226

## Summary
John Carmack, legendary game programmer, posted on X (formerly Twitter) expressing his admiration for Fabrice Bellard, calling him "almost certainly a better overall programmer" than himself. The post was prompted by a thread from Spencer Baggins highlighting Bellard's outsized but largely anonymous impact on the internet — specifically that Bellard wrote FFmpeg, the software underlying video streaming on YouTube, Netflix, TikTok, and most of the modern web. The post went viral, garnering over 598k views and 10k+ likes.

## Key takeaways
- John Carmack, one of the most respected programmers in history, publicly defers to Fabrice Bellard as a superior overall programmer.
- Fabrice Bellard is a French engineer based in Paris who has spent ~30 years building foundational software that powers the internet.
- His most notable work (FFmpeg) is the backbone of virtually all video streaming — YouTube, Netflix, TikTok, and beyond — yet he remains largely unknown to the public.
- This is a rare case of one programming legend explicitly acknowledging another as more technically accomplished.
- The post highlights how the most impactful software contributors are often invisible to mainstream audiences despite their work being used by billions daily.

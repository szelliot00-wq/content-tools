# About 10% of AMC movie showings sell zero tickets. This site finds them

Source: https://walzr.com/empty-screenings

## Summary
A website called "Empty Screenings" tracks AMC movie showings that have sold zero tickets, revealing that approximately 10% of all AMC screenings go completely unattended. The site aggregates this data and makes it publicly accessible, highlighting an interesting and somewhat surprising inefficiency in movie theater operations. The article generated notable discussion on Hacker News, accumulating over 80 comments and more than 100 points.

## Key takeaways
- Approximately 10% of AMC movie showings sell zero tickets, meaning theaters are running screenings for no audience at all.
- A dedicated website (walzr.com/empty-screenings) has been built to find and display these empty screenings in real time.
- This data suggests significant operational inefficiency or inflexibility in how AMC schedules its showings.
- The concept attracted significant community interest on Hacker News, sparking discussion likely around theater economics, scheduling practices, and potential use cases (e.g., finding a private showing).
- AMC's ticketing data appears to be accessible enough that a third-party site can monitor seat sales across showings.

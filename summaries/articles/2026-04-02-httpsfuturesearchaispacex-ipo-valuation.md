# A forecast of the fair market value of SpaceX's businesses

Source: https://futuresearch.ai/spacex-ipo-valuation/

## Summary
The article presents a "sum-of-the-parts" valuation forecast for SpaceX, projecting its fair market value up to a potential IPO in 2028. It models each of SpaceX's key business units—Starlink, Starship, Launch services, and others—individually based on specific assumptions regarding market share, revenue, and expenditures. The analysis estimates SpaceX's enterprise value could reach approximately $168 billion by 2028, with Starlink identified as the primary driver of this projected growth.

## Key takeaways
- SpaceX's enterprise value is forecast to reach approximately $168 billion by 2028, based on a "sum-of-the-parts" valuation methodology.
- Starlink is identified as the most significant value driver, projected to contribute around $80 billion to SpaceX's total valuation by 2028, driven by subscriber growth and ARPU.
- The valuation relies on detailed projections for each business unit, including assumed market shares, revenue growth rates, profit margins, and substantial capital expenditures, particularly for Starlink and Starship development.
- The model suggests Starlink could potentially pursue its own IPO before a full SpaceX IPO, offering a separate path for monetization.
- Significant capital investments are required to achieve the projected growth and value across all segments, especially for Starlink's constellation build-out and Starship's operational deployment.

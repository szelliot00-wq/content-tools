# AI is too expensive

Source: https://www.wheresyoured.at/ai-is-too-expensive/

## Summary
Ed Zitron argues that AI is economically unviable at every level of the stack. Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon) have invested over $800 billion in AI infrastructure but cannot plausibly generate the $3–6 trillion in AI-specific revenue needed to break even. AI labs like Anthropic and OpenAI burn money faster than they earn it with no credible path to profitability, while enterprise customers are blowing through annual token budgets in months with no way to measure ROI. Zitron contends the entire boom is driven by executive herd mentality and deliberate obfuscation by AI vendors.

## Key takeaways
- Hyperscalers need to generate $3–6 trillion in AI-specific revenue just to break even on their capex — current AI revenues are a fraction of that and not growing fast enough
- Outside of Anthropic and OpenAI's own compute spend, hyperscaler revenue backlogs show no significant organic enterprise AI demand; the RPO jumps are almost entirely driven by circular money flows between the hyperscalers and the labs they fund
- Anthropic spent ~$3 to earn $1 of revenue (per its own March 2026 court affidavit), has committed $330B+ in cloud obligations over four years, and is raising billions every few months despite allegedly hitting $3B/month in revenue — the math doesn't work
- Neither OpenAI nor Anthropic is "profitable on inference"; gross margins are falling (Anthropic ~40%, OpenAI ~33%) while compute costs keep rising
- Enterprise customers (Uber, ServiceNow, Salesforce, Stripe) are exhausting annual AI budgets within months, with no telemetry from Anthropic to understand why — Anthropic does not provide standard usage transparency or SLAs
- Zillow is a case study in AI excess: on track to spend $7–10M on AI tokens in 2026 (up to 50% of its 2025 net income), while engineer review load increased by 29,000 hours/month and internal culture shows engineers burning tokens to hit adoption targets, not to deliver value
- Token budgets are structurally unmeasurable — LLM token consumption per task is non-deterministic and model-dependent, making any budget projection unreliable
- Zitron's core thesis: AI spending is driven by executive FOMO and vendor pressure, not demonstrated ROI — and the first major company to cut its token budget will trigger a broader pullback

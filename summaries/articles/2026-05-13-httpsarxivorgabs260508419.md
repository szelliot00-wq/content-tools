# Deterministic Fully-Static Whole-Binary Translation Without Heuristics

Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08419

## Summary
This article presents a novel approach to whole-binary translation that is both deterministic and fully static, eliminating the need for heuristics. The research addresses the longstanding challenge of translating binary code from one architecture to another without runtime profiling or guesswork. The method aims to achieve complete and reliable translation of entire binaries in a static (ahead-of-time) manner with guaranteed correctness.

## Key takeaways
- The approach is **deterministic**, meaning the translation process produces consistent, predictable results without relying on probabilistic or heuristic methods.
- It operates **fully statically**, performing all translation before execution rather than relying on dynamic/runtime analysis or just-in-time compilation.
- The technique targets **whole-binary translation**, meaning it handles the entire binary rather than partial or function-level translation.
- Eliminating heuristics is a significant advancement, as traditional binary translation tools often rely on educated guesses to distinguish code from data, which can lead to errors.
- The work likely has implications for **cross-architecture compatibility**, emulation, security analysis, and legacy software support.

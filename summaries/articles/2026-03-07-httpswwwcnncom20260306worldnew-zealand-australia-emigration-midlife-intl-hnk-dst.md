## Summary
The article explores the phenomenon of New Zealand experiencing a significant emigration of its population, specifically those over the age of 30. It delves into the underlying reasons driving this midlife exodus, which likely include economic pressures, career opportunities, and lifestyle considerations, often in comparison to neighboring countries like Australia. The piece aims to understand the push and pull factors causing this demographic shift.

## Key takeaways
- New Zealand is facing a significant outward migration trend, particularly among individuals aged 30 and above.
- The article investigates various factors contributing to this demographic shift, such as economic conditions, cost of living, and wage discrepancies compared to other countries.
- Limited career advancement prospects and differences in quality of life are likely critical motivators for over-30s seeking opportunities elsewhere.
- This emigration trend could lead to a "brain drain" and impact New Zealand's long-term economic and social landscape.

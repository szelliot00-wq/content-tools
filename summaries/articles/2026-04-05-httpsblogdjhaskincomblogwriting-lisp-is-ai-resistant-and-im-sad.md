# Writing Lisp is AI resistant and I'm sad

Source: https://blog.djhaskin.com/blog/writing-lisp-is-ai-resistant-and-im-sad/

## Summary
The author expresses sadness over Lisp programming's "AI resistance," finding modern AI development tools largely ineffective for the language. This resistance is attributed to a scarcity of Lisp code for AI training, its highly flexible syntax with extensive macro use, and a cultural tendency towards unique, non-standardized solutions. The author laments their inability to leverage AI tools for Lisp development and fears this could hinder Lisp's future relevance and adoption in an increasingly AI-assisted software landscape.

## Key takeaways
- Lisp is "AI resistant," meaning current AI development tools like Copilot and ChatGPT are largely ineffective for Lisp programming.
- The resistance stems from a critical lack of Lisp code data available for AI model training.
- Lisp's highly flexible syntax and extensive use of macros make it difficult for AI to parse and understand context, as code often deviates from simpler patterns.
- The Lisp programming culture fosters unique, "outside-the-box" solutions rather than standardized ones, which AI struggles to replicate as it relies on common patterns.
- The author's "sadness" comes from wanting to use AI tools to augment their Lisp development but finding them useless.
- There is a concern that Lisp's AI resistance could impede its future relevance and adoption in a software development world increasingly reliant on AI assistance.

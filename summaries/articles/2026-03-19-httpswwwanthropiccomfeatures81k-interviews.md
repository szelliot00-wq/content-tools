# What 81,000 people want from AI

Source: https://www.anthropic.com/features/81k-interviews

## Summary
Anthropic conducted a comprehensive global research initiative, interviewing 81,000 individuals to understand their diverse hopes, needs, and concerns regarding AI. The study aimed to gather broad public sentiment to inform the development of AI that is helpful, harmless, and honest. Key findings revealed strong desires for AI to enhance productivity, assist with learning, and provide trustworthy information, alongside significant concerns about job security, misinformation, and malicious use.

## Key takeaways
- People primarily want AI to enhance productivity, assist with learning, and provide trustworthy information, seeking tools that save time and reduce cognitive load.
- Significant concerns persist around AI causing job displacement, spreading misinformation, and its potential for malicious use.
- There is a strong preference for AI that augments human capabilities, acting as a helpful assistant rather than fully automating or replacing human roles.
- Users emphasize the critical need for AI to be reliable, transparent, and trustworthy, especially when handling sensitive information or critical tasks.
- Individuals desire agency over AI, wanting the ability to customize its behavior and outputs to suit their specific needs and values.
